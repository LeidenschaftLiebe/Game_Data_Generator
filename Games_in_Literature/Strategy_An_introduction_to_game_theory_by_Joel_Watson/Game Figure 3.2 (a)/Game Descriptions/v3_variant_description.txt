V3 (supply chain decision)

A manufacturer moves first and chooses between supply plan U and supply plan D.
If the manufacturer chooses supply plan U, a wholesaler then chooses between contract A and contract B.
If the wholesaler chooses contract A, a retailer then chooses between response R and response T.
Response R leads to 9 for the manufacturer, 2 for the wholesaler, and 5 for the retailer.
Response T leads to 2 for the manufacturer, 4 for the wholesaler, and 4 for the retailer.
If the wholesaler chooses contract B, the retailer chooses between response P and response Q.

Response P leads to 0 for the manufacturer, 5 for the wholesaler, and 4 for the retailer.
Response Q leads to 3 for the manufacturer, 0 for the wholesaler, and 0 for the retailer.
If the manufacturer chooses supply plan D, the wholesaler chooses between contract C and contract E.
Contract E ends immediately with 6 for the manufacturer, 3 for the wholesaler, and 2 for the retailer.
If the wholesaler chooses contract C, the retailer again chooses between response P and response Q.

The retailer cannot tell whether this final choice comes after contract B or contract C.
If the retailer chooses P after contract C, the outcome is 2 for the manufacturer, 2 for the wholesaler, and 2 for the retailer.
If the retailer chooses Q after contract C, the outcome is 1 for the manufacturer, 2 for the wholesaler, and 2 for the retailer.
