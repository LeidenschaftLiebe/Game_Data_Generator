V3 (space mission sample case)

A mission control system randomly assigns an astronaut to be either cooperative or hostile, with probability 0.5 for each type.
The astronaut knows his own type.

If the astronaut is cooperative, he chooses whether to pass no sample case or to hand over a sealed sample case to a scientist.
If he passes nothing, the final reward is 0 for the astronaut and 0 for the scientist.
If he hands over the sealed case, the scientist then chooses whether to open it or refuse it.
If the scientist opens a case from a cooperative astronaut, the final reward is 1 for the astronaut and 1 for the scientist.
If the scientist refuses a case from a cooperative astronaut, the final reward is -1 for the astronaut and 0 for the scientist.

If the astronaut is hostile, he also chooses whether to pass no sample case or to hand over a sealed sample case.
If he passes nothing, the final reward is 0 for the astronaut and 0 for the scientist.
If he hands over the case, the scientist again chooses whether to open it or refuse it.
The scientist cannot tell whether the case comes from a cooperative astronaut or a hostile astronaut.
If the scientist opens a case from a hostile astronaut, the final reward is 1 for the astronaut and -1 for the scientist.
If the scientist refuses a case from a hostile astronaut, the final reward is -1 for the astronaut and 0 for the scientist.
