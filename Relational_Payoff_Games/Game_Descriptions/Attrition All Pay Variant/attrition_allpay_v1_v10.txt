1) Patent Competition (InnovaTech vs BioCore)

Two research firms, InnovaTech and BioCore, decide whether to invest heavily or lightly in an R&D race for a single award, 
and each pays what it invests regardless of the result.
When both invest heavily, they split the prize, but the net result for each is a loss because costs are high.
When both invest lightly, they still split the prize, and each comes out ahead because costs are low.
When one invests heavily and the other lightly, the aggressive investor secures the entire prize and ends with the largest gain, 
while the lighter investor pays its smaller cost yet gains nothing.
