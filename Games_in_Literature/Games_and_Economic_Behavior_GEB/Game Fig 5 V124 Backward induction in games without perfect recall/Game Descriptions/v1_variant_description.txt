V1 [Glacier crossing]

A route leader and a guide are deciding how to cross a glacier.

The route leader first chooses between CampHere (X), NorthApproach (T), and SouthApproach (B).

If the route leader chooses CampHere (X), the trip ends there.
This gives 4 to the route leader, and 4 to the guide.

If the route leader chooses NorthApproach (T), the guide chooses between LeftCrevasse (L) and RightCrevasse (R).
If the route leader chooses SouthApproach (B), the guide again chooses between LeftCrevasse (L) and RightCrevasse (R).

At that point, the guide cannot tell whether the earlier route was NorthApproach (T) or SouthApproach (B).

After that, the route leader chooses between UseUpperRope (U) and UseDeepRope (D).
At that later moment, the route leader cannot tell which earlier combination of choices led there.

After NorthApproach (T) and LeftCrevasse (L), UseUpperRope (U) gives 5 to the route leader, and 0 to the guide, 
while UseDeepRope (D) gives 2 to the route leader, and 1 to the guide.

After NorthApproach (T) and RightCrevasse (R), UseUpperRope (U) gives 2 to the route leader, and 0 to the guide, 
while UseDeepRope (D) gives 0 to the route leader, and 1 to the guide.

After SouthApproach (B) and LeftCrevasse (L), UseUpperRope (U) gives 0 to the route leader, and 1 to the guide, 
while UseDeepRope (D) gives 2 to the route leader, and 0 to the guide.

After SouthApproach (B) and RightCrevasse (R), UseUpperRope (U) gives 2 to the route leader, and 1 to the guide, 
while UseDeepRope (D) gives 5 to the route leader, and 0 to the guide.
