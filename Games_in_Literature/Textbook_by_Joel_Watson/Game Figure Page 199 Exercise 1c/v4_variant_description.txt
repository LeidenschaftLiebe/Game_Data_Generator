V4 (wildlife rescue operation)

A rescue coordinator first decides whether to keep an operation at its ordinary level or escalate it into a full emergency deployment.
If the coordinator keeps the ordinary level, the operation ends immediately and the final reward is 2 for the coordinator, 2 for the logistics officer, and 2 for the veterinary specialist.

If the coordinator escalates the operation, the logistics officer then chooses among deployment A, deployment B, and deployment C.
If the logistics officer chooses deployment C, the operation ends immediately with a final reward of 4 for the coordinator, 3 for the logistics officer, and 1 for the veterinary specialist.

If the logistics officer chooses deployment A, the veterinary specialist then chooses between treatment X and treatment Y.
Treatment X after deployment A leads to a final reward of 3 for the coordinator, 2 for the logistics officer, and 1 for the veterinary specialist.
Treatment Y after deployment A leads to a final reward of 5 for the coordinator, 0 for the logistics officer, and 0 for the veterinary specialist.

If the logistics officer chooses deployment B, the veterinary specialist again chooses between treatment X and treatment Y.
At that point, the veterinary specialist cannot tell whether the operation reached this stage after deployment A or deployment B.
Treatment X after deployment B leads to a final reward of 1 for the coordinator, 2 for the logistics officer, and 6 for the veterinary specialist.
Treatment Y after deployment B leads to a final reward of 7 for the coordinator, 5 for the logistics officer, and 5 for the veterinary specialist.
