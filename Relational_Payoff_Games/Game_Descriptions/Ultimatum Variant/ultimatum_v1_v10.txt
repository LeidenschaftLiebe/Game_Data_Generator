1) Inheritance Division

Two siblings must divide a family inheritance.
The elder sibling decides first: they can offer a fair split or a heavily one-sided offer in their own favour.
The younger sibling may accept or reject. If rejected, both walk away with nothing.






2) Community Fund Allocation

Two community leaders must decide how to distribute a grant.
The chairperson proposes either an equitable plan that benefits both or a plan favouring their own district.
The deputy leader can accept or reject. Rejection means the grant is lost entirely.






3) Family Vacation Budget

Parents give two teenagers money to plan a vacation together.
The older teen proposes how to use the funds: either evenly between activities they both enjoy or mostly for their own preferences.
The younger teen can accept or reject. If they reject, the trip is cancelled.






4) Local Charity Drive

A charity coordinator and a volunteer must decide how to distribute collected donations.
The coordinator can propose a balanced allocation or one that channels more funds to their preferred cause.
The volunteer can agree or refuse. A refusal halts the donation effort entirely.






5) Neighborhood Renovation Plan

Two neighbors are offered city funding to fix shared property.
The one managing the application proposes either a fair division of the benefits or one that gives them more upgrades.
The other neighbor can accept or reject. Rejection voids the funding.






6) Political Campaign Budget

A campaign manager and an advisor must split a donation budget.
The manager may propose an even allocation or one that gives their own department most of the funds.
The advisor can accept or reject. If rejected, the donor withdraws the money.






7) University Research Collaboration

Two professors are dividing a university grant.
The lead professor can propose a cooperative split or a division that gives them more research funding.
The co-investigator can accept or reject. If rejected, the university cancels the grant.






8) Village Water Project

Two local leaders must allocate water from a new well.
The first leader can suggest an equal sharing plan or one that prioritizes their own community.
The second leader can accept or reject. A rejection cancels the entire project.






9) Wedding Gift Split

A newly married couple receives a large wedding gift.
The spouse who managed the finances decides whether to divide it evenly or keep a greater share for themselves.
The other spouse can accept or reject. If rejected, the money is returned to the giver.






10) Environmental Grant Sharing

Two NGOs are dividing an environmental grant.
The larger organization can propose an equal split or keep a larger portion for its own operations.
The smaller NGO can accept or reject. A rejection causes the grant to be withdrawn.
