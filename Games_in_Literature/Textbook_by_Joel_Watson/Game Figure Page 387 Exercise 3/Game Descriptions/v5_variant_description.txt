V5 (cybersecurity response)

At the outset, a company may be in either a high-risk state or a low-risk state, with probability 0.5 for each case.
The security lead learns which state applies.
The outside response firm does not observe whether risk is high or low.

If the company is in the high-risk state, the security lead chooses between the local fix and the escalation route.
If the security lead chooses the local fix in the high-risk state, the final reward is 2 for the security lead and 0 for the outside firm.
If the security lead chooses the escalation route in the high-risk state, the outside firm then chooses between an urgent intervention and a delayed intervention.
If the outside firm chooses an urgent intervention there, the final reward is 3 for the security lead and 2 for the outside firm.
If the outside firm chooses a delayed intervention there, the final reward is 1 for the security lead and 0 for the outside firm.

If the company is in the low-risk state, the security lead chooses between the local fix and the escalation route.
If the security lead chooses the local fix in the low-risk state, the final reward is 2 for the security lead and 0 for the outside firm.
If the security lead chooses the escalation route in the low-risk state, the outside firm then chooses between an urgent intervention and a delayed intervention.
The outside firm cannot tell whether this decision follows the escalation route in the high-risk state or in the low-risk state.
If the outside firm chooses an urgent intervention there, the final reward is 1 for the security lead and 0 for the outside firm.
If the outside firm chooses a delayed intervention there, the final reward is 1 for the security lead and 1 for the outside firm.
