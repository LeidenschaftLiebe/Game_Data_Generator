V2 — Factory Emissions

A factory owner may find it easy or costly to allow a full environmental inspection.

At the start, the owner is one of two types.
With probability 0.6 the inspection cost is low (L).
With probability 0.4 the inspection cost is high (H).

After learning the type, the owner chooses among Open Books and Site (NBO),
Meet Standards (NB), and Pollute Secretly (B).

If the low-cost owner chooses Open Books and Site (NBO), the outcome is reward 0.5 to the owner
and 1.0 to the agency.

If the high-cost owner chooses Open Books and Site (NBO), the outcome is reward 0.1 to the owner
and 1.0 to the agency.

If the owner instead chooses Meet Standards (NB) or Pollute Secretly (B), the agency moves.

The agency only knows that the owner did not open up fully.
It cannot tell whether the owner is type L or H, and cannot tell whether the owner chose
Meet Standards (NB) or Pollute Secretly (B).

The agency chooses between No Raid (NA) and Raid (A).

If the path is Meet Standards (NB), No Raid (NA), the outcome is reward 0.6 to the owner
and 1.0 to the agency.

If the path is Meet Standards (NB), Raid (A), the outcome is reward 0.2 to the owner
and 0.3 to the agency.

If the path is Pollute Secretly (B), No Raid (NA), the outcome is reward 1.0 to the owner
and 0.0 to the agency.

If the path is Pollute Secretly (B), Raid (A), the outcome is reward 0.0 to the owner
and 0.8 to the agency.
