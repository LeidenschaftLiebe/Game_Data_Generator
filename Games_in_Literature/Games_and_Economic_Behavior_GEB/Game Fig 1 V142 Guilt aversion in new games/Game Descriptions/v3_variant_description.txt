V3 [Family loan]

A family member, a relative handling the money, and a bystander are involved in a lending decision.
The first family member chooses between KeepMoney (Out) and LendMoney (In).

If the first family member chooses KeepMoney (Out), the interaction ends immediately.
The result is 170 for the first family member, 100 for the relative, and 30 for the bystander.

If the first family member chooses LendMoney (In), 25 units from the first family member’s own money are handed to the relative.
The relative then chooses between ReturnSmallPart (Left) and ReturnEverything (Right).

If the relative chooses ReturnSmallPart (Left), the relative keeps 20 and gives back 5.
That returned amount is doubled, so the first family member receives 10.
The result is 155 for the first family member, 120 for the relative, and 30 for the bystander.

If the relative chooses ReturnEverything (Right), the relative gives back the full 25.
That amount is doubled, so the first family member receives 50.
The result is 195 for the first family member, 100 for the relative, and 30 for the bystander.
