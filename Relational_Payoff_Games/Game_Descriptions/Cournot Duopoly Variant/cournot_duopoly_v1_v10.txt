1) Mineral Water Bottlers

Two bottlers compete in a homogeneous water market.
Each simultaneously chooses a High output plan or a Low output plan.
When both choose High, total output is greatest and the market price is lowest.
When one chooses High and the other chooses Low, total output is moderate and the price is in the middle; 
The high-output bottler earns more than the low-output bottler.
When both choose Low, total output is smallest and the price is highest, with both firms earning the same.






2) Data Center Cooling Services

Two providers sell identical cooling capacity to cloud campuses.
Each simultaneously chooses High or Low capacity to deploy.
Both High yields the lowest price and equal earnings.
A mixed outcome yields a middle price; 
The provider choosing High earns more than the one choosing Low.
Both Low yields the highest price and equal earnings.






3) Freight Rail on a Busy Corridor

Two rail operators offer indistinguishable freight slots on the same corridor.

Both Low produces the highest rate and equal earnings.
