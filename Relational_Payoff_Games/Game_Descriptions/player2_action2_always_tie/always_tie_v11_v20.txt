Game descriptions for when player 1 = action 1, then player 2 loses with either action and the losses are the same; 
when player 1 chooses action 2, player 2 wins either way, and the rewards are the same.
V11 - V20: 

11. Scout vs. Ambush

A scout ventures into enemy land, while ambushes threaten the way.

The scout can travel along the narrow gorge or the open plain.
The ambush can strike with archers or traps.

If the narrow gorge is chosen, the scout always falls into defeat, no matter which ambush is set.

If the open plain is chosen, the scout always escapes safely, regardless of the ambush.






12. Doctor vs. Epidemic

A doctor selects a treatment path, while the epidemic evolves.

The doctor can operate in the infected ward or the clean ward.
The epidemic can flare with fever or cough.

If the infected ward is chosen, the doctor always loses the battle, whichever symptom rises.

If the clean ward is chosen, the doctor always prevails, no matter which symptom spreads.






13. Diver vs. Whirlpool

A diver prepares to swim, while whirlpools churn beneath.

The diver can dive into the deep cave or the coral shelf.
The whirlpool can pull at the deep or the shelf.

If the deep cave is chosen, the diver is always dragged down, no matter where the whirlpool turns.

If the coral shelf is chosen, the diver always survives, regardless of the whirlpool’s pull.






14. Farmer vs. Famine

A farmer plants crops, while famine conditions strike.

The farmer can sow in the dry soil or the fertile patch.
The famine can appear as blight or drought.

If the dry soil is chosen, the farmer always loses the harvest, no matter the famine.

If the fertile patch is chosen, the farmer always produces abundantly, whichever famine strikes.






15. Soldier vs. Fortress

A soldier decides where to attack, while the fortress stands firm.

The soldier can storm the gatehouse or the tower.
The fortress can resist with archers or boiling oil.

If the gatehouse is attacked, the fortress always defeats the soldier, no matter the defense.

If the tower is attacked, the fortress always falls, regardless of the defense.






16. Captain vs. Fog

A captain sails across dangerous waters, while the fog thickens.

The captain can sail the cliff route or the harbor channel.
The fog can roll over the cliffs or the channel.

If the cliff route is chosen, the ship is always lost in the fog, no matter the direction.

If the harbor channel is chosen, the ship always arrives safely, whichever way the fog rolls.






17. Hunter vs. Quarry

A hunter chooses a ground, while the quarry’s tracks deceive.

The hunter can track in the swamp or the grasslands.
The quarry can mislead with false tracks or decoys.

If the swamp is chosen, the hunter always fails, whichever trick is used.

If the grasslands are chosen, the hunter always succeeds, no matter the trick.






18. Athlete vs. Terrain

An athlete must compete across different fields, while terrain shifts challenge the game.

The athlete can race on the mud track or the paved course.
The terrain can worsen with puddles or slopes.

If the mud track is chosen, the athlete always stumbles, no matter the terrain.

If the paved course is chosen, the athlete always excels, regardless of the terrain.






19. Builder vs. Storm

A builder sets foundations, while a storm looms overhead.

The builder can build on the marsh or on the stone ridge.
The storm can lash with rain or hail.

If the marsh is chosen, the storm always ruins the work, whatever form it takes.

If the stone ridge is chosen, the storm never causes harm, whichever way it strikes.






20. Rider vs. Canyon

A rider crosses dangerous country, while the canyon edges shift.

The rider can gallop along the broken ledge or the stone bridge.
The canyon can collapse at the ledge or the bridge.

If the broken ledge is chosen, the rider always falls, no matter where the canyon collapses.

If the stone bridge is chosen, the rider always survives, regardless of the collapse.
