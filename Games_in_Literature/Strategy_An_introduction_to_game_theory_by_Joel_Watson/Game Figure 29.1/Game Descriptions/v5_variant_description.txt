V5 (cybersecurity hiring)

At the outset, a candidate may be either a high-ability analyst or a low-ability analyst.
The high-ability type occurs with probability 1/3.
The low-ability type occurs with probability 2/3.
The candidate learns her own type.
The firm does not observe the candidate’s type.

The candidate then chooses whether to earn a costly advanced security certification or not.
The firm observes whether the candidate earned the certification, but it does not observe her true ability.
After that, the firm chooses between assigning her to a major incident-response role or a clerical operations role.

If the candidate is high-ability and does not earn the certification, the firm chooses between the incident-response role and the clerical role.
If the firm assigns the incident-response role there, the final reward is 10 for the candidate and 10 for the firm.
If the firm assigns the clerical role there, the final reward is 4 for the candidate and 4 for the firm.

If the candidate is low-ability and does not earn the certification, the firm again chooses between the incident-response role and the clerical role.
The firm cannot tell whether the no-certification signal came from the high-ability or low-ability candidate.
If the firm assigns the incident-response role there, the final reward is 10 for the candidate and 0 for the firm.
If the firm assigns the clerical role there, the final reward is 4 for the candidate and 4 for the firm.

If the candidate is high-ability and earns the certification, the firm chooses between the incident-response role and the clerical role.
If the firm assigns the incident-response role there, the final reward is 6 for the candidate and 10 for the firm.
If the firm assigns the clerical role there, the final reward is 0 for the candidate and 4 for the firm.

If the candidate is low-ability and earns the certification, the firm again chooses between the incident-response role and the clerical role.
The firm cannot tell whether the certification signal came from the high-ability or low-ability candidate.
If the firm assigns the incident-response role there, the final reward is 3 for the candidate and 0 for the firm.
If the firm assigns the clerical role there, the final reward is -3 for the candidate and 4 for the firm.
