V1 (museum signal game)

A museum curator first decides whether to place a marker on the top shelf or on the floor.
An assistant sees that choice.
Then the assistant decides whether to hang his own marker up high or leave it on the floor.

If the assistant hangs his marker up high, the activity ends and all three people get 0.
If the assistant leaves it on the floor, an observer must guess whether the curator’s marker is up high.
The observer says yes or no.
The observer can see what the assistant did, but not what the curator did.

That means the observer cannot tell which earlier situation led to this guessing point.

If the curator put the marker up high and the observer says yes, the guess is correct.
The final reward is -1 for the curator, 0 for the assistant, and 1 for the observer.
If the curator put the marker up high and the observer says no, the guess is wrong.
The final reward is 1 for the curator, 0 for the assistant, and -1 for the observer.

If the curator put the marker on the floor and the observer says yes, the guess is wrong.
The final reward is 1 for the curator, 0 for the assistant, and -1 for the observer.
If the curator put the marker on the floor and the observer says no, the guess is correct.
The final reward is -1 for the curator, 0 for the assistant, and 1 for the observer.
