V4 (video game studio rivalry)

A creative director is deciding whether to stay at a major game studio or leave to found a new indie studio. If he stays, the rivalry never takes shape, and the outcome is that he gets 35 while the publisher head gets 100. If he leaves, the publisher head must decide whether the company will greenlight its own bug-themed game.

At the same time, the departing director is deciding whether his new studio should develop a competing game. The key tension is that he has to commit to development before he can tell whether the publisher head chose to move forward with the company’s project. So when he chooses whether to build the rival game, he knows he has left, but he does not yet know what the publisher has done.

If the publisher does not greenlight its game, then the new studio’s choice determines the result directly: developing the rival game gives the departing director 80 and the publisher 0, while dropping the idea leaves both at 0. If the publisher does greenlight its game and the new studio chooses not to compete, then the departing director gets 0 and the publisher gets 140. If both studios develop games, the departing director faces one final release decision: he can push for an early launch, which gives him 40 and the publisher 110, or he can release on the regular schedule and end with 13 while the publisher gets 120.
