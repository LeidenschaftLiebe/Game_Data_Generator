Game descriptions for mismatching actions (mismatch is a win for player 2 and match is a win for player 1). 

V31 - V40: 

31. Fisherman vs. Gull

A fisherman (Player 1) casts nets in the Lagoon or the Harbor. A gull (Player 2) swoops.

If nets are in the Lagoon, swooping the Harbor (win for Gull) catches runoff; swooping the Lagoon (loss for Gull) fails.

If nets are in the Harbor, swooping the Lagoon (win for Gull) succeeds; swooping the Harbor (loss for Gull) fails.

This contest is zero-sum: the gull’s catch is the fisherman’s loss.






32. Diplomat vs. Saboteur

A diplomat (Player 1) attends the Summit or the Conference. A saboteur (Player 2) plans disruption.

If the diplomat attends the Summit, disrupting the Conference (win for Saboteur) derails alliances; disrupting the Summit (loss for Saboteur) fails.

If the diplomat attends the Conference, disrupting the Summit (win for Saboteur) works; disrupting the Conference (loss for Saboteur) fails.

This situation is zero-sum: sabotage negates diplomacy.






33. Captain vs. Mutineer

A captain (Player 1) rallies crew on the Deck or in the Mess hall. A mutineer (Player 2) stirs dissent.

If the captain gathers on the Deck, dissent in the Mess (win for Mutineer) undermines him; dissent on the Deck (loss for Mutineer) fails.

If the captain gathers in the Mess, dissent on the Deck (win for Mutineer) prevails; dissent in the Mess (loss for Mutineer) fails.

This struggle is zero-sum: mutiny wins equal captain’s losses.






34. Gamer vs. Stream Sniper

A gamer (Player 1) spawns at Location A or Location B. A stream sniper (Player 2) sets a trap.

If spawning at A, trapping B (win for Sniper) catches rotation; trapping A (loss for Sniper) misses.

If spawning at B, trapping A (win for Sniper) works; trapping B (loss for Sniper) fails.

This encounter is zero-sum: the sniper’s ambush is the gamer’s loss.






35. Inventor vs. Copycat

An inventor (Player 1) develops Device X or Device Y. A copycat (Player 2) markets.

If working on X, marketing Y (win for Copycat) distracts buyers; marketing X (loss for Copycat) confirms the inventor.

If working on Y, marketing X (win for Copycat) succeeds; marketing Y (loss for Copycat) fails.

This rivalry is zero-sum: copycat’s profit reduces inventor’s.






36. Author vs. Critic

An author (Player 1) writes Novel A or Novel B. A critic (Player 2) publishes.

If the author writes A, reviewing B (win for Critic) shifts focus; reviewing A (loss for Critic) boosts them.

If the author writes B, reviewing A (win for Critic) damages them; reviewing B (loss for Critic) fails.

This duel is zero-sum: one’s acclaim is the other’s loss.






37. Athlete vs. Rival Coach

An athlete (Player 1) practices Sprint or Marathon. A rival coach (Player 2) scouts.

If training for Sprint, focusing on Marathon (win for Rival Coach) undercuts; focusing on Sprint (loss for Rival Coach) fails.

If training for Marathon, focusing on Sprint (win for Rival Coach) succeeds; focusing on Marathon (loss for Rival Coach) fails.

This match is zero-sum: scouting gain is athlete’s setback.






38. Journalist vs. Censor

A journalist (Player 1) reports on Topic X or Topic Y. A censor (Player 2) blocks.

If reporting X, blocking Y (win for Censor) redirects headlines; blocking X (loss for Censor) fails.

If reporting Y, blocking X (win for Censor) succeeds; blocking Y (loss for Censor) fails.

This contest is zero-sum: censorship undermines journalism.






39. Diver vs. Octopus

A diver (Player 1) explores the Cave or the Reef. An octopus (Player 2) ambushes.

If at the Cave, ambushing the Reef (win for Octopus) intercepts; ambushing the Cave (loss for Octopus) fails.

If at the Reef, ambushing the Cave (win for Octopus) works; ambushing the Reef (loss for Octopus) fails.

This is zero-sum: catch for octopus is diver’s loss.






40. Engineer vs. Hacker

An engineer (Player 1) fixes System A or System B. A hacker (Player 2) launches attacks.

If fixing A, attacking B (win for Hacker) succeeds; attacking A (loss for Hacker) fails.

If fixing B, attacking A (win for Hacker) works; attacking B (loss for Hacker) fails.

This game is zero-sum: hacker success is engineer failure.
