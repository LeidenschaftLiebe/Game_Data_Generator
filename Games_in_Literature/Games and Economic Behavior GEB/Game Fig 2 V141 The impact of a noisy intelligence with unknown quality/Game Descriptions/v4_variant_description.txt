V4 [Doping control]

An athlete decides whether to use a banned enhancement method.
The athlete first chooses between NoBanUse (NB) and BanUse (B).

A testing system then sends a signal.
It sends PositiveSignal (b) with probability 0.8 if the athlete used the method, and with probability 0.2 if the athlete did not.
It sends NegativeSignal (nb) with probability 0.8 if the athlete did not use the method, and with probability 0.2 if the athlete did.

After seeing the signal, the governing body chooses between Suspend (A) and NoSuspend (NA).

If the athlete chose NoBanUse (NB) and the governing body chooses Suspend (A), the result is 0.2 for the athlete and 0.3 for the governing body.
If the athlete chose NoBanUse (NB) and the governing body chooses NoSuspend (NA), the result is 0.7 for the athlete and 1 for the governing body.

If the athlete chose BanUse (B) and the governing body chooses Suspend (A), the result is 0 for the athlete and 0.8 for the governing body.
If the athlete chose BanUse (B) and the governing body chooses NoSuspend (NA), the result is 1 for the athlete and 0 for the governing body.
