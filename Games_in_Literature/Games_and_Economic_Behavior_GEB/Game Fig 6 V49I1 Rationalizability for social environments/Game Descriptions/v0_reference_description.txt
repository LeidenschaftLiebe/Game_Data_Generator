V0 — Social Environment Example

There are two individuals, 1 and 2, and three possible final results: x0, x1, and x2.

At the start, an outside procedure selects an order between (x1, x2) and (x2, x1).

Then individual 1 decides whether to support the move to x1 by choosing 1 or 0.
Choosing 1 means supporting the move to x1, and choosing 0 means not supporting it.

After that, individual 2 decides whether to support the move to x2 by choosing 1 or 0.
Choosing 1 means supporting the move to x2, and choosing 0 means not supporting it.

Individual 1 does not know which order was selected when making the choice.
Individual 2 does not know which order was selected, and also does not know what individual 1 chose.

If neither individual supports a move, the result is x0.
At x0, both individuals get 0.

If only individual 1 supports a move, the result is x1.
At x1, individual 1 gets 2 and individual 2 gets 1.

If only individual 2 supports a move, the result is x2.
At x2, individual 1 gets 1 and individual 2 gets 2.

If both individuals support their moves, then the outside procedure decides which result is implemented.
If the selected order is (x1, x2), the result is x1.
If the selected order is (x2, x1), the result is x2.
