V3 [Medical consultation]

A patient visits a specialist.
The specialist first posts two prices: BasicTreatmentPrice (p_l) = 4 and AdvancedTreatmentPrice (p_h) = 8.

The patient then chooses between DoNotProceed (out) and Proceed (in).
If the patient chooses DoNotProceed (out), both sides get 1.6.

If the patient chooses Proceed (in), the condition is either SevereCondition (high) or MildCondition (low), each with probability 0.5.
The specialist learns which one it is.

If the patient has a SevereCondition (high), only AdvancedTreatment (q_h) truly works.
AdvancedTreatment (q_h) with AdvancedTreatmentPrice (p_h) gives 2 to the specialist and 2 to the patient.
AdvancedTreatment (q_h) with BasicTreatmentPrice (p_l) gives -2 to the specialist and 6 to the patient.

If the specialist instead provides BasicTreatment (q_l) on a severe condition, the problem remains.
Charging AdvancedTreatmentPrice (p_h) gives 6 to the specialist and -8 to the patient.
Charging BasicTreatmentPrice (p_l) gives 2 to the specialist and -4 to the patient.

If the patient has a MildCondition (low), either treatment solves it.
AdvancedTreatment (q_h) with AdvancedTreatmentPrice (p_h) gives 2 and 2.
AdvancedTreatment (q_h) with BasicTreatmentPrice (p_l) gives -2 and 6.

BasicTreatment (q_l) with AdvancedTreatmentPrice (p_h) gives 6 to the specialist and 2 to the patient.
BasicTreatment (q_l) with BasicTreatmentPrice (p_l) gives 2 to the specialist and 6 to the patient.
