Game descriptions for mismatching actions (mismatch is a win for player 2 and match is a win for player 1). 

V21 - V30: 

21. Miner vs. Cave Beast

A miner explores tunnels while a lurking beast waits to strike.

The miner can enter the north shaft or the south shaft.
The beast can ambush the north shaft or the south shaft.

If both choose the north shaft, or both choose the south shaft, the miner drives the beast away and wins with the same result.

If the miner enters the north shaft while the beast waits in the south, or the other way around, the beast ambushes successfully and wins with the same result.






22. Teacher vs. Cheating Student

A teacher watches during an exam while a student plans to cheat.

The teacher can focus on the front rows or the back rows.
The student can attempt to cheat in the front rows or the back rows.

If both focus on the front, or both on the back, the teacher catches the cheating student and wins with the same outcome.

If the teacher watches the front while the student cheats in the back, or the other way around, the student succeeds and wins with the same outcome.






23. Pilot vs. Enemy Radar

A pilot flies a mission while enemy radar operators look for them.

The pilot can fly over the mountains or across the coastline.
The radar can scan the mountains or the coastline.

If both target the mountains, or both target the coastline, the radar detects the pilot and wins with the same outcome.

If the pilot flies over the mountains while the radar scans the coast, or the other way around, the pilot slips through and wins with the same outcome.






24. Wolf vs. Deer

A wolf hunts while a deer runs for safety.

The wolf can dash to the riverbank or the clearing.
The deer can flee to the riverbank or the clearing.

If both go to the riverbank, or both to the clearing, the wolf catches the deer and wins with the same result.

If the wolf runs to the riverbank while the deer flees to the clearing, or the other way around, the deer escapes and wins with the same result.






25. Inspector vs. Counterfeiter

An inspector checks goods while a counterfeiter tries to slip fakes through.

The inspector can examine the north warehouse or the south warehouse.
The counterfeiter can deliver fakes to the north warehouse or the south warehouse.

If both pick the north, or both pick the south, the inspector seizes the counterfeits and wins with the same outcome.

If the inspector checks the north while the counterfeiter uses the south, or vice versa, the counterfeiter succeeds and wins with the same outcome.






26. Ranger vs. Poacher

A ranger patrols a reserve while a poacher sets traps.

The ranger can patrol the watering hole or the forest edge.
The poacher can lay traps at the watering hole or the forest edge.

If both choose the watering hole, or both choose the forest edge, the ranger catches the poacher and wins with the same result.

If the ranger patrols the watering hole while the poacher sets traps at the forest edge, or the other way around, the poacher succeeds and wins with the same result.






27. Engineer vs. Hacker

An engineer defends a network while a hacker launches attacks.

The engineer can secure the firewall or the database.
The hacker can attack the firewall or the database.

If both choose the firewall, or both choose the database, the engineer blocks the attack and wins with the same outcome.

If the engineer secures the firewall while the hacker targets the database, or vice versa, the hacker succeeds and wins with the same outcome.






28. Gladiator vs. Gladiator

Two gladiators prepare in the arena, each trying to anticipate the other.

One gladiator can strike at the head or the torso.
The other gladiator can block the head or the torso.

If both focus on the head, or both on the torso, the defending gladiator blocks the strike and wins with the same outcome.

If one strikes at the head while the other blocks the torso, or the other way around, the attacker lands a hit and wins with the same outcome.






29. Guard vs. Prisoner

A prisoner plots escape while the guard chooses a post.

The guard can cover the east gate or the west gate.
The prisoner can attempt the east gate or the west gate.

If both pick the east, or both pick the west, the guard intercepts the prisoner and wins with the same result.

If the guard covers the east while the prisoner runs to the west, or vice versa, the prisoner escapes and wins with the same result.






30. Hawk vs. Squirrel

A hawk hunts while a squirrel scrambles for safety.

The hawk can dive into the oak tree or the pine grove.
The squirrel can dash into the oak tree or the pine grove.

If both go to the oak, or both to the pine, the hawk catches the squirrel and wins with the same outcome.

If the hawk dives at the oak while the squirrel runs to the pine, or vice versa, the squirrel escapes and wins with the same outcome.
