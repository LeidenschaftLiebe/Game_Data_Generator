V1 — Security Audit

A company may begin in either Case A or Case B.
Nature selects the case, with probability 1/2 for each.

If the company is in Case A, the director makes an A-case choice between End Review (Ea) and Continue Review (Ca).
Choosing End Review (Ea) ends the process with reward 1 to the director and reward 0 to the auditor.
Choosing Continue Review (Ca) passes the move to the auditor.

If the company is in Case B, the director makes a B-case choice between End Briefing (Eb) and Continue Briefing (Cb).
Choosing End Briefing (Eb) ends the process with reward 1 to the director and reward 0 to the auditor.
Choosing Continue Briefing (Cb) passes the move to the auditor.

If Continue Review (Ca) or Continue Briefing (Cb) is chosen, the auditor then chooses among
Approve (L), Escalate (R), and Cross-Check (C2).
At that moment, the auditor knows that the director continued, but does not know whether the earlier case was A or B.

If the earlier case was A and the auditor chooses Approve (L), the game ends with reward 0 to the director and reward 3 to the auditor.
If the earlier case was A and the auditor chooses Escalate (R), the game ends with reward 3 to the director and reward 0 to the auditor.
If the earlier case was A and the auditor chooses Cross-Check (C2), the director gets another turn in the B-case situation.

If the earlier case was B and the auditor chooses Escalate (R), the game ends with reward 0 to the director and reward 3 to the auditor.
If the earlier case was B and the auditor chooses Approve (L), the game ends with reward 3 to the director and reward 0 to the auditor.
If the earlier case was B and the auditor chooses Cross-Check (C2), the director gets another turn in the A-case situation.

When the director later returns to the B-case situation, the director again chooses between End Briefing (Eb) and Continue Briefing (Cb).
Either choice then ends the process with reward 0 to the director and reward 2 to the auditor.

When the director later returns to the A-case situation, the director again chooses between End Review (Ea) and Continue Review (Ca).
Either choice then ends the process with reward 0 to the director and reward 2 to the auditor.

Whenever the director is making an A-case choice, 
the director cannot tell whether it is the earlier A situation or the later one reached after Cross-Check (C2).
Likewise, whenever the director is making a B-case choice, 
the director cannot tell whether it is the earlier B situation or the later one reached after Cross-Check (C2).
