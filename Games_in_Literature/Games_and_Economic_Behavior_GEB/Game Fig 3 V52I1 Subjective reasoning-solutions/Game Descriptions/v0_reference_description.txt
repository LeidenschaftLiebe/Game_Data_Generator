V0 — Procedural Reasoning Order

Alice moves first and chooses l, m, or r.

If Alice chooses m or r, then Bob moves without knowing which of those two choices Alice made.
Bob then chooses L or R.

If Alice chooses l, the move passes to Emma.
If Alice chooses m and Bob chooses L, the move also passes to Emma.

When Emma moves, she does not know whether she is there because Alice chose l, or because Alice chose m and Bob chose L.
Emma then chooses p or q.

If Alice chose l, then whether Emma picks p or q, Alice gets 0, Bob gets 2, and Emma gets 0.

If Alice chose m and Bob chose L, then whether Emma picks p or q, Alice gets 2, Bob gets 0, and Emma gets 0.

If Alice chose m and Bob chose R, then Alice gets 1, Bob gets 1, and Emma gets 0.

If Alice chose r and Bob chose L, then Alice gets -1, Bob gets -1, and Emma gets 0.

If Alice chose r and Bob chose R, then Alice gets -1, Bob gets 0, and Emma gets 0.
