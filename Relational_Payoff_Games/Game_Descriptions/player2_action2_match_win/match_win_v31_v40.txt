Game descriptions for matching actions (match is a win for player 2 and mismatch is a win for player 1). 

V31 - V40: 

31. Wizard vs. Witch

A wizard (Player 1) hides in the Tower or the Crypt. A witch (Player 2) casts a revealing spell.

If in the Tower, casting at the Tower (win for Witch) uncovers him; casting at the Crypt (loss for Witch) fails.

If in the Crypt, casting at the Crypt (win for Witch) works; casting at the Tower (loss for Witch) fails.

This duel is zero-sum: one’s magic gain is the other’s defeat.






32. Merchant vs. Tax Collector

A merchant (Player 1) transports goods through the Market gate or the Harbor gate. A tax collector (Player 2) waits.

If through the Market gate, waiting at the Market gate (win for Collector) succeeds; waiting at the Harbor gate (loss for Collector) misses.

If through the Harbor gate, waiting at the Harbor gate (win for Collector) succeeds; waiting at the Market gate (loss for Collector) fails.

This encounter is zero-sum: the collector’s revenue is the merchant’s loss.






33. Explorer vs. Guardian Spirit

An explorer (Player 1) seeks treasure in the Temple or the Ruins. A guardian spirit (Player 2) blocks the way.

If entering the Temple, blocking the Temple (win for Spirit) succeeds; blocking the Ruins (loss for Spirit) fails.

If entering the Ruins, blocking the Ruins (win for Spirit) works; blocking the Temple (loss for Spirit) fails.

This struggle is zero-sum: the spirit’s defense cancels the explorer’s gain.






34. Chef vs. Critic

A chef (Player 1) serves either Dish A or Dish B. A food critic (Player 2) prepares their review.

If the chef serves Dish A, reviewing Dish A (win for Critic) succeeds; reviewing Dish B (loss for Critic) misses.

If the chef serves Dish B, reviewing Dish B (win for Critic) succeeds; reviewing Dish A (loss for Critic) fails.

This game is zero-sum: the critic’s sharp remark is the chef’s downfall.






35. Athlete vs. Coach

An athlete (Player 1) chooses Routine A or Routine B. The coach (Player 2) prepares feedback.

If Routine A is chosen, feedback on A (win for Coach) exposes flaws; feedback on B (loss for Coach) misses.

If Routine B is chosen, feedback on B (win for Coach) succeeds; feedback on A (loss for Coach) fails.

This interaction is zero-sum: the coach’s critique is the athlete’s setback.






36. Shepherd vs. Wolf

A shepherd (Player 1) moves sheep to the Pasture or the Barn. A wolf (Player 2) hunts.

If moved to the Pasture, hunting the Pasture (win for Wolf) succeeds; hunting the Barn (loss for Wolf) fails.

If moved to the Barn, hunting the Barn (win for Wolf) succeeds; hunting the Pasture (loss for Wolf) fails.

This chase is zero-sum: the wolf’s meal is the shepherd’s loss.






37. Pilot vs. Missile System

A pilot (Player 1) flies over the Mountains or the Valley. The missile system (Player 2) locks on.

If over the Mountains, locking the Mountains (win for Missile) hits; locking the Valley (loss for Missile) fails.

If over the Valley, locking the Valley (win for Missile) hits; locking the Mountains (loss for Missile) fails.

This duel is zero-sum: one strike means the other’s failure.






38. Diver vs. Octopus

A diver (Player 1) explores the Shipwreck or the Coral reef. An octopus (Player 2) ambushes.

If at the Shipwreck, ambushing there (win for Octopus) succeeds; at the Coral reef (loss for Octopus) fails.

If at the Coral reef, ambushing there (win for Octopus) succeeds; at the Shipwreck (loss for Octopus) fails.

This encounter is zero-sum: the octopus’s catch is the diver’s loss.






39. Knight vs. Assassin

A knight (Player 1) travels along the Stone path or the Forest trail. An assassin (Player 2) sets an ambush.

If the knight takes the Stone path, ambushing there (win for Assassin) succeeds; ambushing the Forest trail (loss for Assassin) fails.

If the knight takes the Forest trail, ambushing there (win for Assassin) succeeds; ambushing the Stone path (loss for Assassin) fails.

This battle is zero-sum: the assassin’s success is the knight’s loss.






40. Batsman vs. Bowler

A batsman (Player 1) plays a shot to the Off side or the Leg side. The bowler (Player 2) sets the field.

If the batsman plays to the Off side, fielding the Off side (win for Bowler) succeeds; fielding the Leg side (loss for Bowler) fails.

If the batsman plays to the Leg side, fielding the Leg side (win for Bowler) succeeds; fielding the Off side (loss for Bowler) fails.

This match-up is zero-sum: every fielding success is the batsman’s failure.
