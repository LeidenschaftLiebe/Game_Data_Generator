V0 (closest to the textbook)

At the outset, the firm may be either high quality or low quality.
The high-quality case occurs with probability 0.5, and the low-quality case occurs with probability 0.5.
The firm learns its own quality.
The worker does not observe the firm’s quality directly.

The firm then decides whether to make a job offer.
If no offer is made, the interaction ends and both sides get 0.

If the firm is high quality and makes an offer, the worker chooses whether to accept or reject it.
If the worker accepts, the final reward is 2 for the firm and 2 for the worker.
If the worker rejects, the final reward is -1 for the firm and 0 for the worker.

If the firm is low quality and makes an offer, the worker again chooses whether to accept or reject it.
The worker cannot tell whether the offer came from a high-quality firm or a low-quality firm.
If the worker accepts, the final reward is 2 for the firm and -1 for the worker.
If the worker rejects, the final reward is -1 for the firm and 0 for the worker.
