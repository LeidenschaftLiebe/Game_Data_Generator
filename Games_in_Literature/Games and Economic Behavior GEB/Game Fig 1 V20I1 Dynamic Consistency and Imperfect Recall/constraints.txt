Strategic Definition:
Better-Response Equivalence

Constraints:

There is one player, 1, who first chooses between T and G.
Choosing T ends the game immediately with payoff 3.
If the player chooses G, chance then chooses between l and r with probability 1/2 each.
When making a move at d3, the player cannot tell whether the earlier chance move was l or r, 
and cannot tell whether the current situation came from d1 or from d2.
