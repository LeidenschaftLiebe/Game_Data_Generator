1. The Duel at Dawn

Two gunslingers face off in a duel. Each must decide whether to shoot or hold fire.

If one holds fire while the other shoots, the shooter gains an advantage, while the one who held back suffers a penalty.
If both hold fire, neither gains the upper hand, but each earns a smaller advantage for walking away unharmed.
If both shoot, they are both struck and each suffer the greatest punishment.






2. The Gladiators’ Standoff

Two gladiators confront each other in the arena. Each must decide whether to charge or hold position.

If one holds position while the other charges, the one holding claims a prize, while the charger suffers a setback.
If both hold, neither gains the upper hand, but each secures a smaller prize for restraint.
If both charge, they collide and each suffer the highest downfall.






3. The Pilots’ Contest
