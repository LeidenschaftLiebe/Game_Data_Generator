V4 (sports training marker game)

A player first decides whether to place a training marker on the rack or on the ground.
A teammate sees that choice.
Then the teammate decides whether to place his own marker on the rack or on the ground.

If the teammate places his marker on the rack, the drill ends and everyone gets 0.
If he places it on the ground, a coach must guess whether the first player’s marker is on the rack.
The coach answers yes or no.
The coach sees the teammate’s move, but not the first player’s move.

So the coach cannot distinguish between the two hidden histories that lead to that guessing point.

If the first marker is on the rack and the coach says yes, the guess is correct.
The final reward is -1 for the first player, 0 for the teammate, and 1 for the coach.
If the first marker is on the rack and the coach says no, the guess is wrong.
The final reward is 1 for the first player, 0 for the teammate, and -1 for the coach.

If the first marker is on the ground and the coach says yes, the guess is wrong.
The final reward is 1 for the first player, 0 for the teammate, and -1 for the coach.
If the first marker is on the ground and the coach says no, the guess is correct.
The final reward is -1 for the first player, 0 for the teammate, and 1 for the coach.
