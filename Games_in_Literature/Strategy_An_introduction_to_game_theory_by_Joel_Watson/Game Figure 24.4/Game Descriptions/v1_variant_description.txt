V1 (rare mineral survey)

There are some randomness in the quality of a mineral site.
With probability 2/3, the site is rich and the hidden value is 12.
With probability 1/3, the site is poor and the hidden value is 0.
The survey leader learns the site quality before choosing a drilling plan.
The investor does not observe the site quality and does not observe which drilling plan was chosen before deciding whether to fund aggressively or cautiously.

If the site is rich and the survey leader chooses plan A while the investor chooses option C, the final reward is 12 for the survey leader and 9 for the investor.
If the site is rich and the survey leader chooses plan A while the investor chooses option D, the final reward is 3 for the survey leader and 6 for the investor.
If the site is rich and the survey leader chooses plan B while the investor chooses option C, the final reward is 6 for the survey leader and 0 for the investor.
If the site is rich and the survey leader chooses plan B while the investor chooses option D, the final reward is 6 for the survey leader and 9 for the investor.

If the site is poor and the survey leader chooses plan A while the investor chooses option C, the final reward is 0 for the survey leader and 9 for the investor.
If the site is poor and the survey leader chooses plan A while the investor chooses option D, the final reward is 3 for the survey leader and 6 for the investor.
If the site is poor and the survey leader chooses plan B while the investor chooses option C, the final reward is 6 for the survey leader and 0 for the investor.
If the site is poor and the survey leader chooses plan B while the investor chooses option D, the final reward is 6 for the survey leader and 9 for the investor.
