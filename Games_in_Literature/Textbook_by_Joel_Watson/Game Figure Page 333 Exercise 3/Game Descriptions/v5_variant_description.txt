V5 (cybersecurity response)

A company may be in either an upper security state or a lower security state, with probability 0.5 for each case.
The internal security lead knows which state has occurred.
The outside incident-response firm does not know which state the company is in when making its response.

If the company is in the upper security state, the security lead chooses between handling the issue internally and escalating it to the outside firm.
If the lead handles it internally, the final reward is 2 for the security lead and 0 for the outside firm.
If the lead escalates it, the outside firm then chooses between an urgent intervention and a delayed intervention.
If the outside firm chooses an urgent intervention after the upper escalation branch, the final reward is 0 for the security lead and 4 for the outside firm.
If the outside firm chooses a delayed intervention after the upper escalation branch, the final reward is 4 for the security lead and 0 for the outside firm.

If the company is in the lower security state, the security lead chooses between handling the issue internally and escalating it to the outside firm.
If the lead handles it internally, the final reward is 2 for the security lead and 0 for the outside firm.
If the lead escalates it, the outside firm again chooses between an urgent intervention and a delayed intervention.
The outside firm cannot tell whether the escalation came from the upper security state or the lower security state.
If the outside firm chooses an urgent intervention after the lower escalation branch, the final reward is 0 for the security lead and 0 for the outside firm.
If the outside firm chooses a delayed intervention after the lower escalation branch, the final reward is 4 for the security lead and 2 for the outside firm.
