V2 [Antarctic supply run]

A station commander and a route officer are deciding how to move supplies across Antarctic terrain.

The station commander moves first and chooses between BaseStorage (T) and IceCrossing (B).

If the station commander chooses BaseStorage (T), then the route officer chooses between UpperIce (U) and DeepIce (D).
If the route officer chooses UpperIce (U), this gives 2 to the station commander, and 2 to the route officer.
If the route officer chooses DeepIce (D), this gives 0 to the station commander, and 0 to the route officer.

If the station commander chooses IceCrossing (B), then the route officer chooses between LeftShelf (L) and RightShelf (R).

If the route officer chooses RightShelf (R), the mission ends there.
This gives 1 to the station commander, and 1 to the route officer.

If the route officer chooses LeftShelf (L), the route officer later chooses between UpperIce (U) and DeepIce (D).
If the route officer chooses UpperIce (U), this gives 0 to the station commander, and 0 to the route officer.
If the route officer chooses DeepIce (D), this gives 3 to the station commander, and 3 to the route officer.

The two UpperIce (U) or DeepIce (D) nodes are linked, so the route officer cannot distinguish whether 
that later choice follows BaseStorage (T) or follows IceCrossing (B) and then LeftShelf (L).
