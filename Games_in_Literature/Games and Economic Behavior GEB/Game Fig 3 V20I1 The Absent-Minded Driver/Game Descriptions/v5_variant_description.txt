V5 — Museum Visitor with Repeating Hallways

A visitor enters a museum hallway that passes three exits.

The exits are HallX [X], HallY [Y], and HallZ [Z].

At each exit, the visitor chooses LeaveNow [EXIT] or KeepGoing [CONT].

The hallways are designed so similarly that the visitor cannot tell whether the current exit is HallX [X], HallY [Y], or HallZ [Z].
So the same decision rule is used whenever an exit appears.

If the visitor leaves at HallX [X], the reward is 7.

If the visitor leaves at HallY [Y], the reward is 0.

If the visitor leaves at HallZ [Z], the reward is 22.

If the visitor keeps going past HallZ [Z], the reward is 2.
