Player 1 moves first and chooses between a, b, and c.
If Player 1 chooses c, the game ends right away.
Player 1 gets 1, and Player 2 gets 0.

If Player 1 chooses a or b, then Player 2 chooses between d, e, and f
without knowing whether Player 1 chose a or b.

If Player 2 chooses e, the game ends right away.
After a then e, Player 1 gets 0 and Player 2 gets 5.
After b then e, Player 1 gets 0 and Player 2 gets 4.

If Player 2 chooses f, the game also ends right away.
After a then f, Player 1 gets 1 and Player 2 gets 4.
After b then f, Player 1 gets 1 and Player 2 gets 5.

If Player 2 chooses d, Player 2 must choose again between g and h,
still without knowing whether the earlier path started with a or b.

After a then d then g, Player 1 gets 2 and Player 2 gets 1.
After a then d then h, Player 1 gets 0 and Player 2 gets 0.
After b then d then g, Player 1 gets 2 and Player 2 gets 0.
After b then d then h, Player 1 gets 0 and Player 2 gets 3.
