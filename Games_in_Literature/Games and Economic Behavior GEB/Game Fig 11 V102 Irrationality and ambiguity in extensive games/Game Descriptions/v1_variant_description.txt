V1 — Product Review

A company is deciding how to handle a new product plan.
The company moves first and chooses between Cancel Plan (O), Limited Rollout (L), and Full Rollout (R).
If it chooses Cancel Plan (O), the process ends immediately with reward 2 to the company and reward 1 to the retail partner.

If the company chooses Limited Rollout (L) or Full Rollout (R), the retail partner moves next.
The retail partner cannot tell which of those two rollout choices was made.
The retail partner then chooses between Cautious Response (A) and Strong Response (B).

If the path is Limited Rollout (L), Cautious Response (A), the company moves again.
It then chooses between Keep Current Plan (C) and Pull Back (D).
If Keep Current Plan (C) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 2 to the company and reward 3 to the retail partner.
If Pull Back (D) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 0 to the company and reward 2 to the retail partner.

If the path is Limited Rollout (L), Strong Response (B), the company moves again.
It then chooses between Expand Carefully (E) and Stop Expansion (F).
If Expand Carefully (E) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 1 to the company and reward 4 to the retail partner.
If Stop Expansion (F) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 0 to both players.

If the path is Full Rollout (R), Cautious Response (A), the company moves again.
It then chooses between Push Nationwide (G) and Slow Down (H).
If Push Nationwide (G) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 3 to the company and reward 5 to the retail partner.
If Slow Down (H) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 0 to the company and reward 1 to the retail partner.

If the path is Full Rollout (R), Strong Response (B), the company moves again.
It then chooses between Keep Momentum (J) and Abandon Push (K).
If Keep Momentum (J) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 1 to the company and reward 4 to the retail partner.
If Abandon Push (K) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 0 to both players.
