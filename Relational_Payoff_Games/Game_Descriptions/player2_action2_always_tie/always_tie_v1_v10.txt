Game descriptions for when player 1 = action 1, then player 2 loses with either action and the losses are the same; 
when player 1 chooses action 2, player 2 wins either way, and the rewards are the same.
V1 - V10: 

1. Prosecutor vs. Defense Attorney

A prosecutor (Player 1) decides to pursue Fraud charges or Assault charges. The defense attorney (Player 2) chooses a defense tactic.

If Fraud charges are filed, the defense attorney always loses. Choosing Lack of intent or Insufficient evidence makes no difference, since both lead to the same losing outcome.

If Assault charges are filed, the defense attorney always wins. Whether using Self-defense or Mistaken identity, both result in the same winning outcome.

Zero-sum: every courtroom gain by one is a loss for the other.






2. Candidate vs. Rival

A candidate (Player 1) runs on Education reform or Tax policy. The rival (Player 2) sets their counter-narrative.

If Education reform is emphasized, the rival always loses. Whether they argue Curriculum or Funding, the outcome is the same loss.

If Tax policy is emphasized, the rival always wins. Picking Corporate cuts or Family relief makes no difference, since both lead to the same win.

Zero-sum: public opinion tips entirely to one side.






3. Teacher vs. Student

A teacher (Player 1) holds a test on History or Mathematics. The student (Player 2) prepares accordingly.

If the exam is on History, the student always loses. Studying Dates or Events leads to the same losing result.

If the exam is on Mathematics, the student always wins. Preparing Algebra or Geometry produces the same winning outcome.

Zero-sum: mastery flows entirely one way.






4. Negotiator vs. Counterparty

A negotiator (Player 1) pushes talks on Tariffs or Security agreements. The counterparty (Player 2) responds.

If Tariffs are the focus, the counterparty always loses. Whether they push Imports or Exports, both lead to the same losing result.

If Security agreements are the focus, the counterparty always wins. Choosing Border patrol or Weapons limits yields the same winning payoff.

Zero-sum: leverage swings completely.






5. Journalist vs. Politician

A journalist (Player 1) chooses to ask about Scandals or Policy details. The politician (Player 2) answers.

If the question is about Scandals, the politician always loses. Deflecting or denying both lead to the same outcome of loss.

If the question is about Policy details, the politician always wins. Highlighting successes or blaming rivals both deliver the same winning result.

Zero-sum: control of the narrative rests entirely with one side.






6. Coach vs. Player

A coach (Player 1) emphasizes Defense drills or Offense drills. The player (Player 2) adjusts training.

If Defense drills are chosen, the player always loses. Practicing Positioning or Tackling makes no difference, since both result in failure.

If Offense drills are chosen, the player always wins. Whether they work on Shooting or Passing, the outcome is the same win.

Zero-sum: practice outcomes are absolute.

8. Student vs. Examiner

9. Debater vs. Challenger

If Security is argued, the challenger always wins. Using Safety or Enforcement leads to the same winning result.

Zero-sum: rhetorical advantage lies wholly with one.
