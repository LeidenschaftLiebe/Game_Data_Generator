V0 — Experimental Signaling Game

There are two players, the Sender and the Receiver.
Nature moves first and determines the Sender’s type.
With probability p, the Sender is t1, and with probability 1-p, the Sender is t2.

The Sender observes the type and then chooses between m1 and m2.
The Receiver does not observe the type.
The Receiver only observes which message was sent.

So after observing m1, the Receiver chooses between a1 and a2 without knowing whether the Sender is t1 or t2.
After observing m2, the Receiver again chooses between a1 and a2 without knowing the Sender’s type.

If the path is t1, m1, a1, the outcome gives reward 15 to the Sender and reward 10 to the Receiver.
If the path is t1, m1, a2, the outcome gives reward 80 to the Sender and reward 80 to the Receiver.

If the path is t1, m2, a1, the outcome gives reward 25 to the Sender and reward 10 to the Receiver.
If the path is t1, m2, a2, the outcome gives reward 50 to the Sender and reward 50 to the Receiver.

If the path is t2, m1, a1, the outcome gives reward 80 to the Sender and reward 80 to the Receiver.
If the path is t2, m1, a2, the outcome gives reward 15 to the Sender and reward 30 to the Receiver.

If the path is t2, m2, a1, the outcome gives reward 50 to the Sender and reward 50 to the Receiver.
If the path is t2, m2, a2, the outcome gives reward 25 to the Sender and reward 30 to the Receiver.
