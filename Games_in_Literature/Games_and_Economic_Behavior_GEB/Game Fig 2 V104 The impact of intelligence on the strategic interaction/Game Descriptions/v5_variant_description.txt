V5 — Customs Inspection

A trading company may find it easy or costly to open all containers to customs.

At the start, the company is one of two types.
With probability 0.6 the inspection cost is low (L).
With probability 0.4 the inspection cost is high (H).

After learning the type, the company chooses among Open Containers (NBO),
Carry Legal Goods (NB), and Hide Contraband (B).

If the low-cost company chooses Open Containers (NBO), the outcome is reward 0.5 to the company
and 1.0 to customs.

If the high-cost company chooses Open Containers (NBO), the outcome is reward 0.1 to the company
and 1.0 to customs.

If the company instead chooses Carry Legal Goods (NB) or Hide Contraband (B), customs moves.

Customs only sees that the company did not open everything.
Customs cannot tell whether the company is low-cost or high-cost, and cannot tell whether the company chose
Carry Legal Goods (NB) or Hide Contraband (B).

Customs chooses between No Seizure (NA) and Seizure (A).

If the path is Carry Legal Goods (NB), No Seizure (NA), the outcome is reward 0.6 to the company
and 1.0 to customs.

If the path is Carry Legal Goods (NB), Seizure (A), the outcome is reward 0.2 to the company
and 0.3 to customs.

If the path is Hide Contraband (B), No Seizure (NA), the outcome is reward 1.0 to the company
and 0.0 to customs.

If the path is Hide Contraband (B), Seizure (A), the outcome is reward 0.0 to the company
and 0.8 to customs.
