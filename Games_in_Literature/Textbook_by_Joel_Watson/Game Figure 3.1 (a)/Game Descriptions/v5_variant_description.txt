V5 (coffee chains)

A coffee chain first decides whether to compete aggressively, compete passively, or leave a local market to its rival.
If it leaves, the game ends immediately and that chain gets 0 while the rival chain gets 4.
If it stays, the rival chain then chooses whether to be aggressive or passive.
The rival knows the first chain stayed in the market, but does not know whether it chose the aggressive or passive stance.
If both chains are aggressive, each gets 3.
If Chain 1 is aggressive and Chain 2 is passive, Chain 1 gets 4 and Chain 2 gets 2.
If Chain 1 is passive and Chain 2 is aggressive, Chain 1 gets 2 and Chain 2 gets 4.
If both chains are passive, each gets 2.
