11) Corporate Merger Negotiation

Two companies are negotiating how to share the benefits of a merger.
The larger company starts and puts forward two proposals that give it a larger portion of the total value.
If the smaller company accepts, the merger proceeds immediately.
If the smaller company rejects, regulatory delays reduce the overall value; 
The smaller company then makes a new proposal that benefits it more.
Finally, the larger company can accept that counterproposal or refuse it, in which case both sides divide the smaller amount equally.






12) International Trade Agreement

The exporting nation proposes two initial offers that leave it ahead.
If the importing nation accepts, the deal is signed right away.
If the importing nation rejects, diplomatic delays reduce the total benefit; 
After which it presents a counterproposal more favorable to itself.
The exporting nation can then accept or reject, and a rejection results in an even division of the smaller gains.
