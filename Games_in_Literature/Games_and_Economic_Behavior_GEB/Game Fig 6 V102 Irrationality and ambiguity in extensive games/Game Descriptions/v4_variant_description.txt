V4 — Sponsorship Agreement

A creator and a sponsor are bargaining over the revenue split from a campaign.

In stage 1, the creator proposes a split:
the creator asks for 1/4, 1/2, or 3/4 of the campaign value.

The sponsor then chooses whether to Accept (A) or Reject (R).

If the sponsor accepts in stage 1, the agreement ends there.

If the creator asks for 1/4 and the sponsor accepts, the result is 1/4 for the creator
and 3/4 for the sponsor.

If the creator asks for 1/2 and the sponsor accepts, the result is 1/2 for each side.

If the creator asks for 3/4 and the sponsor accepts, the result is 3/4 for the creator
and 1/4 for the sponsor.

If the sponsor rejects, the discussion moves to stage 2.

In stage 2, the sponsor makes the offer instead.
The sponsor can offer the creator 1/4, 1/2, or 3/4 of the campaign value.

The creator then chooses whether to Accept (A) or Reject (R).

Because the deal is delayed, accepted stage 2 outcomes are discounted by ρ = 4/5.

So an accepted 1/4 offer gives 1/5 to the creator and 3/5 to the sponsor.

An accepted 1/2 offer gives 2/5 to each side.

An accepted 3/4 offer gives 3/5 to the creator and 1/5 to the sponsor.

If the creator rejects in stage 2, both sides get 0.
