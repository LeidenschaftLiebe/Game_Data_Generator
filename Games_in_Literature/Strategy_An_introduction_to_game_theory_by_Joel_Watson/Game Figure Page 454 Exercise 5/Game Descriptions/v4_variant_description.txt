V4 (training credit relay)

Two athletes each begin with 1 training credit.
On a turn, the active athlete can cash out or pass the relay onward.
Passing onward costs that athlete 1 credit and increases the other athlete’s total by 2 credits.
If someone cashes out, the game ends and both keep their current credits.
If no one cashes out by the end of the fifth move, both finish with 0.

Athlete 1 moves first.
Cashing out immediately gives final rewards of 1 and 1.
Passing onward changes the totals to 0 for Athlete 1 and 3 for Athlete 2.

Athlete 2 then chooses.
Cashing out there gives final rewards of 0 and 3.
Passing onward changes the totals to 2 and 2.

Athlete 1 then chooses again.
Cashing out there gives final rewards of 2 and 2.
Passing onward changes the totals to 1 and 4.

Athlete 2 then chooses again.
Cashing out there gives final rewards of 1 and 4.
Passing onward changes the totals to 3 and 3.

Athlete 1 then has the last move.
Cashing out there gives final rewards of 3 and 3.
Passing onward means the relay went the full five moves without a stop, so both get 0.
