V1 [Product launch under noisy signals]

Market conditions first turn out to be LocalDemand (L), RegionalDemand (R), or OutsideShock (O).

The operations lead can tell whether the situation is OutsideShock (O) or not.
If it is not OutsideShock (O), the operations lead chooses between MassLaunch (M) and NicheLaunch (N).

If the condition is LocalDemand (L) and the choice is MassLaunch (M), the result is 0, 0.
If the condition is LocalDemand (L) and the choice is NicheLaunch (N), the result is 101, 0.

If the condition is RegionalDemand (R) and the choice is MassLaunch (M), the result is 101, 0.
If the condition is RegionalDemand (R) and the choice is NicheLaunch (N), then the distribution lead moves.

If the condition is OutsideShock (O), the distribution lead also moves.
The distribution lead chooses between StorePush (S) and TargetedPush (T) without knowing whether the hidden case 
is RegionalDemand (R) after NicheLaunch (N) or directly OutsideShock (O).

If the hidden case is RegionalDemand (R), both StorePush (S) and TargetedPush (T) give 100, 0.
If the hidden case is OutsideShock (O), both StorePush (S) and TargetedPush (T) give -1, 0.
