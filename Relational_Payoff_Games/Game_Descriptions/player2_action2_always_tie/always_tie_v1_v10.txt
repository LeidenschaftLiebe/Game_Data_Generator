Game descriptions for when player 1 = action 1, then player 2 loses with either action and the losses are the same; 
when player 1 chooses action 2, player 2 wins either way, and the rewards are the same.
V1 - V10: 

1. Explorer vs. Desert

An explorer must choose a path through the desert, while the desert reveals its dangers.

The explorer can march along the dune ridge or across the salt flats.
The desert can shift with sandstorms or scorching sun.

If the dune ridge is chosen, the desert always defeats the explorer, whether itâ€™s storms or sun.

If the salt flats are chosen, the explorer always makes it through, no matter the condition.






2. Farmer vs. Flood

A farmer plants crops while floods threaten.

The farmer can plant in the lowland or the upland.
The flood can strike the lowland or the upland.

If crops are in the lowland, the flood always destroys them, regardless of where it surges.

If crops are in the upland, the farmer always prospers, no matter which field is hit.






3. Pilot vs. Weather

A pilot chooses a route, while the weather shifts violently.

The pilot can fly across the storm belt or the clear skies.
The weather can unleash lightning or turbulence.

If the storm belt is crossed, the pilot always struggles, whichever force of weather appears.

If the clear skies are chosen, the pilot always succeeds, regardless of the disturbance.






4. Miner vs. Cave Hazard

A miner decides where to dig, while hidden hazards threaten.

The miner can work in the unstable shaft or the reinforced shaft.
The hazard can take the form of gas or rockfall.

If the unstable shaft is chosen, the miner always faces disaster, no matter the hazard.

If the reinforced shaft is chosen, the miner always remains safe, whichever hazard arises.






5. General vs. Terrain

A general directs troops, while the terrain dictates success or failure.

The general can move through the swamp or the fortified road.
The terrain can hinder with mud or ambushes.

If the swamp is chosen, the general always loses ground, regardless of the obstacle.

If the fortified road is chosen, the general always advances, no matter the hazard.






6. Fisher vs. Storm

A fisher sets sail, while storms build at sea.

The fisher can cast nets in the exposed bay or in the sheltered cove.
The storm can strike with high waves or fierce winds.

If the exposed bay is chosen, the fisher always fails, no matter the storm.

If the sheltered cove is chosen, the fisher always succeeds, whatever the storm brings.






7. Traveler vs. Mountain

A traveler chooses a route through the mountains, while danger lurks.

The traveler can take the rockslide path or the paved road.
The mountain can threaten with snow or stonefall.

If the rockslide path is taken, the traveler always suffers, regardless of the threat.

If the paved road is taken, the traveler always makes it through, whichever hazard occurs.






8. Merchant vs. Pirates

A merchant decides how to sail, while pirates lurk.

The merchant can pass through the pirate strait or the royal patrol zone.
The pirates can attack with boarding or cannon fire.

If the pirate strait is chosen, the merchant always loses, no matter how the pirates attack.

If the patrol zone is chosen, the merchant always wins, regardless of pirate tactics.






9. Athlete vs. Conditions

An athlete chooses an event, while conditions affect performance.

The athlete can compete on the broken track or the new stadium.
The conditions can bring rain or wind.

If the broken track is chosen, the athlete always loses, whatever the weather.

If the new stadium is chosen, the athlete always wins, no matter the weather.






10. Builder vs. Earthquake

A builder decides where to raise a structure, while quakes rumble beneath.

The builder can build on the fault line or the bedrock hill.
The quake can shake with tremors or shocks.

If the fault line is chosen, the builder always fails, regardless of the quake.

If the bedrock hill is chosen, the builder always succeeds, no matter the tremors.
