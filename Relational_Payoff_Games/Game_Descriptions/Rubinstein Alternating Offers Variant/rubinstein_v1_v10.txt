1) Film Production Profit Division

A film producer and a lead actor are dividing a box-office bonus.
The producer speaks first and presents two options that are both better for the producer than for the actor.
If the actor accepts, the agreement is settled immediately.
If the actor rejects, time passes and the total pool becomes smaller before the actor proposes a response that is better for the actor.
The producer then chooses to accept that response or decline it, in which case the reduced pool is split evenly.
