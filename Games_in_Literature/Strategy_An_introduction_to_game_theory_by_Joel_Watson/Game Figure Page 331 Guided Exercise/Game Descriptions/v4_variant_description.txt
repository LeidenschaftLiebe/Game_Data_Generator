V4 (sports media rights)

An incumbent sports network may be operating with either high costs or low costs, with probability 0.5 for each case.
The network learns its own cost condition before setting a rights price.
A rival network does not know that cost condition.
The rival only sees which of the two price options was posted.

If the incumbent is high-cost, it chooses between rights-price option 1 and rights-price option 2.
If the high-cost incumbent chooses rights-price option 1, the rival then decides whether to enter the market or stay out.
If the rival enters after seeing rights-price option 1 from the high-cost incumbent, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and 1 for the rival.
If the rival stays out, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and 0 for the rival.

If the high-cost incumbent chooses rights-price option 2, the rival again decides whether to enter or stay out.
If the rival enters after seeing rights-price option 2 from the high-cost incumbent, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and 1 for the rival.
If the rival stays out, the final reward is 2 for the incumbent and 0 for the rival.

If the incumbent is low-cost, it also chooses between rights-price option 1 and rights-price option 2.
If the low-cost incumbent chooses rights-price option 1, the rival then decides whether to enter or stay out.
If the rival enters after seeing rights-price option 1 from the low-cost incumbent, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and -1 for the rival.
If the rival stays out, the final reward is 2 for the incumbent and 0 for the rival.

If the low-cost incumbent chooses rights-price option 2, the rival again decides whether to enter or stay out.
If the rival enters after seeing rights-price option 2 from the low-cost incumbent, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and -1 for the rival.
If the rival stays out, the final reward is 4 for the incumbent and 0 for the rival.
