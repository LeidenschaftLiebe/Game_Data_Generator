V1 — Product Positioning

A company is deciding how to position a new product.
The company moves first and chooses between Premium Route (a), Balanced Route (b),
and Budget Route (c).

After that, the retail partner moves.
The retail partner cannot tell which of those three product routes was chosen.
The retail partner then chooses between Push in Stores (d) and Hold Back (e).

If the path is Premium Route (a), Push in Stores (d),
the outcome gives reward 2 to the company and reward 1 to the retail partner.
If the path is Premium Route (a), Hold Back (e),
the outcome gives reward 1 to the company and reward 0 to the retail partner.

If the path is Balanced Route (b), Push in Stores (d),
the outcome gives reward 1 to the company and reward 1 to the retail partner.
If the path is Balanced Route (b), Hold Back (e),
the outcome gives reward 2 to the company and reward 0 to the retail partner.

If the path is Budget Route (c), Push in Stores (d),
the outcome gives reward 0 to the company and reward 0 to the retail partner.
If the path is Budget Route (c), Hold Back (e),
the outcome gives reward 0 to the company and reward 1 to the retail partner.
