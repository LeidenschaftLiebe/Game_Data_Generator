1. Contractor and Client — $20

A small contractor and a client are dividing a $20 refund for a job that finished early.
The contractor can offer an even 50/50 split or keep a larger 70/30 share.
The client may accept or reject. If the client rejects, the refund is forfeited and neither side receives anything.






2. Landlord and Tenant — $100

A landlord and tenant must decide how to divide a $100 damage deposit after moving out.
The landlord can propose a fair 60/40 split or claim more with an 80/20 division.
The tenant may accept or reject. If rejected, the deposit is withheld entirely by the agency.






3. Shop Owner and Supplier — $50

A shop owner receives a $50 refund from a damaged shipment.
They can offer to share the refund equally with the supplier or keep most of it with a 90/10 split.
The supplier decides whether to accept or reject. If rejected, the carrier keeps the refund.






4. Business Partners — $30

Two business partners must decide how to divide a $30 bonus from a shared contract.
The senior partner can offer an even split or a 70/30 division that favours them.
The junior partner can accept or reject. If they reject, the client cancels the payment.






5. Startup Founders — $200

Two startup founders have won a $200 innovation grant.
The lead founder can propose an equal 50/50 split or take the majority with a 75/25 share.
The cofounder can accept or reject. Rejection voids the grant.






6. Bank and Investor — $40

A bank and an investor share a $40 profit from a joint venture.
The bank can suggest either an even division or an 80/20 split in its favour.
The investor can accept or reject. Rejection means the investment is written off entirely.






7. Charity Organiser and Volunteer — $60

A cancelled charity event results in a $60 reimbursement.
The organiser can divide the amount 60/40 or keep most of it with an 85/15 share.
The volunteer can accept or reject. If rejected, the refund goes back to the donor.






8. Engineering Team — $80

An engineering firm awards an $80 project bonus to a two-person team.
The project manager can offer a moderate 55/45 split or demand a 75/25 share.
The assistant engineer can accept or reject. If rejected, the firm keeps the bonus.






9. Advertising Agency — $120

A client pays $120 for an ad campaign completed by two partners.
The creative director can propose a 60/40 split or take nearly all the reward with a 90/10 offer.
The account manager may accept or reject. A rejection voids the payment.






10. Construction Contract — $24

A builder and an electrician are sharing $24 from a completed job.
The builder decides whether to split it evenly or keep most of it with a 75/25 division.
The electrician can accept or reject. If rejected, the payment is withheld by the client.
