V2 [Computer repair]

A customer brings a laptop to a technician.
The technician first posts two prices: SimpleFixPrice (p_l) = 4 and FullRepairPrice (p_h) = 8.

The customer then chooses between Leave (out) and StayForService (in).
If the customer chooses Leave (out), both sides get 1.6.

If the customer chooses StayForService (in), the problem is either SeriousFault (high) or LightFault (low), each with probability 0.5.
The technician learns which one it is.

If the laptop has a SeriousFault (high), only FullRepair (q_h) solves it.
FullRepair (q_h) with FullRepairPrice (p_h) gives 2 to the technician and 2 to the customer.
FullRepair (q_h) with SimpleFixPrice (p_l) gives -2 to the technician and 6 to the customer.

If the technician provides SimpleFix (q_l) instead, the serious fault remains.
Charging FullRepairPrice (p_h) gives 6 to the technician and -8 to the customer.
Charging SimpleFixPrice (p_l) gives 2 to the technician and -4 to the customer.

If the laptop has a LightFault (low), either FullRepair (q_h) or SimpleFix (q_l) solves it.
FullRepair (q_h) with FullRepairPrice (p_h) gives 2 and 2.
FullRepair (q_h) with SimpleFixPrice (p_l) gives -2 and 6.

SimpleFix (q_l) with FullRepairPrice (p_h) gives 6 to the technician and 2 to the customer.
SimpleFix (q_l) with SimpleFixPrice (p_l) gives 2 to the technician and 6 to the customer.
