Game descriptions for when player 1 = action 1, then player 2 loses with either action and the losses are the same; 
when player 1 chooses action 2, player 2 wins either way, and the rewards are the same.
V21 - V30: 

21. Manager vs. Team Lead

A manager (Player 1) assigns focus to Budgeting or Execution. The team lead (Player 2) reacts.

If Budgeting is chosen, the team lead always loses. Choosing Cutting costs or Delaying tasks makes no difference, since both lead to the same loss.

If Execution is chosen, the team lead always wins. Whether they Streamline processes or Reassign staff, both produce the same winning result.

Zero-sum: one side’s control over workflow is the other’s loss.






22. Athlete vs. Rival

An athlete (Player 1) decides to compete in the 100m sprint or the 1500m race. The rival (Player 2) prepares.

If the 100m sprint is chosen, the rival always loses. Training Starts or Speed both result in the same defeat.

If the 1500m race is chosen, the rival always wins. Specializing in Endurance or Pacing both yield the same winning outcome.

Zero-sum: athletic advantage lies entirely with one side.






23. Teacher vs. Student

If the test is on Mathematics, the student always wins. Preparing Algebra or Geometry both produce the same success.

Zero-sum: preparedness is decisive for one over the other.
