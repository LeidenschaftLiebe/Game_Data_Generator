V3 — Flood Barrier

A river authority is deciding what warning to send to a control station.
Nature first determines whether the river condition is Moderate Flow (t1) or Critical Flow (t2).
The authority sees the condition and then sends either Signal North (m1) or Signal South (m2).

The control station observes the signal but does not observe the true river condition.
So after Signal North (m1), the control station chooses between Gate Plan A (a1) and Gate Plan B (a2).
After Signal South (m2), the control station again chooses between Gate Plan A (a1) and Gate Plan B (a2).

If the path is Moderate Flow (t1), Signal North (m1), Gate Plan A (a1), the outcome gives reward 15 to the authority and reward 10 to the control station.
If the path is Moderate Flow (t1), Signal North (m1), Gate Plan B (a2), the outcome gives reward 80 to the authority and reward 80 to the control station.

If the path is Moderate Flow (t1), Signal South (m2), Gate Plan A (a1), the outcome gives reward 25 to the authority and reward 10 to the control station.
If the path is Moderate Flow (t1), Signal South (m2), Gate Plan B (a2), the outcome gives reward 50 to the authority and reward 50 to the control station.

If the path is Critical Flow (t2), Signal North (m1), Gate Plan A (a1), the outcome gives reward 80 to the authority and reward 80 to the control station.
If the path is Critical Flow (t2), Signal North (m1), Gate Plan B (a2), the outcome gives reward 15 to the authority and reward 30 to the control station.

If the path is Critical Flow (t2), Signal South (m2), Gate Plan A (a1), the outcome gives reward 50 to the authority and reward 50 to the control station.
If the path is Critical Flow (t2), Signal South (m2), Gate Plan B (a2), the outcome gives reward 25 to the authority and reward 30 to the control station.
