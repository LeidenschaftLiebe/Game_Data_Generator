V5 (wildlife expedition encounter)

At the outset, an expedition leader may be either strong and recovered or weak and injured, with probability 0.5 for each case.
The expedition leader knows which condition applies.
A hostile local rival does not observe the leader’s condition.

If the leader is strong and recovered, the leader chooses whether to stay in camp or step out and confront the rival.
If the leader stays in camp, the rival then chooses whether to withdraw or attack.
If the rival withdraws after the leader stays in camp, the final reward is 1 for the leader and 0 for the rival.
If the rival attacks after the leader stays in camp, the final reward is 0 for the leader and -2 for the rival.

If the leader steps out while strong and recovered, the rival again chooses whether to withdraw or attack.
The rival observes that the leader stepped out, but still does not know whether the leader is strong and recovered or weak and injured.
If the rival withdraws after the leader steps out while strong and recovered, the final reward is 1 for the leader and 0 for the rival.
If the rival attacks after the leader steps out while strong and recovered, the final reward is 0 for the leader and -2 for the rival.

If the leader is weak and injured, the leader chooses whether to stay in camp or step out.
If the leader stays in camp while weak and injured, the rival then chooses whether to withdraw or attack.
If the rival withdraws there, the final reward is 1 for the leader and 0 for the rival.
If the rival attacks there, the final reward is -1 for the leader and 1 for the rival.

If the leader steps out while weak and injured, the rival again chooses whether to withdraw or attack.
The rival observes the step out, but does not know the leader’s true condition.
If the rival withdraws there, the final reward is 0.5 for the leader and 0 for the rival.
If the rival attacks there, the final reward is -1.5 for the leader and 1 for the rival.
