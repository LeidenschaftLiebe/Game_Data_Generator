V3 — Cloud Pricing Move

A major cloud provider sets its pricing position first, and a smaller competitor reacts later.

The major provider chooses PremiumPush [S] or BalancedPlan [C].

The smaller provider then chooses whether to RunMarketScan [Observe] or SkipScan [Not Observe].
Running the scan costs 30 points.

If it runs the scan, it sees the earlier pricing move before deciding how to respond.
If it skips the scan, it chooses without that information.

The smaller provider then chooses ScaleFast [s] or CutBack [c].

If the major provider chose PremiumPush [S], the smaller provider scanned, and then chose ScaleFast [s], 
the major provider gets 500 and the smaller provider gets 170.
If the major provider chose PremiumPush [S], the smaller provider scanned, and then chose CutBack [c], 
the major provider gets 300 and the smaller provider gets 70.

If the major provider chose PremiumPush [S], the smaller provider skipped the scan, and then chose ScaleFast [s], 
the major provider gets 500 and the smaller provider gets 200.
If the major provider chose PremiumPush [S], the smaller provider skipped the scan, and then chose CutBack [c], 
the major provider gets 300 and the smaller provider gets 100.

If the major provider chose BalancedPlan [C], the smaller provider skipped the scan, and then chose ScaleFast [s], 
the major provider gets 600 and the smaller provider gets 300.
If the major provider chose BalancedPlan [C], the smaller provider skipped the scan, and then chose CutBack [c], 
the major provider gets 400 and the smaller provider gets 400.

If the major provider chose BalancedPlan [C], the smaller provider scanned, and then chose ScaleFast [s], 
the major provider gets 600 and the smaller provider gets 270.
If the major provider chose BalancedPlan [C], the smaller provider scanned, and then chose CutBack [c], 
the major provider gets 400 and the smaller provider gets 370.
