V5 (data center pricing)

An incumbent cloud provider may be either a high-cost operator or a low-cost operator, with probability 0.5 for each case.
The provider learns its own cost condition before posting a service price.
A rival provider does not know that cost condition.
The rival only sees which of the two price options was posted.

If the incumbent is high-cost, it chooses between service-price option 1 and service-price option 2.
If the high-cost incumbent chooses service-price option 1, the rival then decides whether to enter the market or stay out.
If the rival enters after seeing service-price option 1 from the high-cost incumbent, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and 1 for the rival.
If the rival stays out, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and 0 for the rival.

If the high-cost incumbent chooses service-price option 2, the rival again decides whether to enter or stay out.
If the rival enters after seeing service-price option 2 from the high-cost incumbent, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and 1 for the rival.
If the rival stays out, the final reward is 2 for the incumbent and 0 for the rival.

If the incumbent is low-cost, it also chooses between service-price option 1 and service-price option 2.
If the low-cost incumbent chooses service-price option 1, the rival then decides whether to enter or stay out.
If the rival enters after seeing service-price option 1 from the low-cost incumbent, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and -1 for the rival.
If the rival stays out, the final reward is 2 for the incumbent and 0 for the rival.

If the low-cost incumbent chooses service-price option 2, the rival again decides whether to enter or stay out.
If the rival enters after seeing service-price option 2 from the low-cost incumbent, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and -1 for the rival.
If the rival stays out, the final reward is 4 for the incumbent and 0 for the rival.
