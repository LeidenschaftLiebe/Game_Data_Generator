V5 (cybersecurity firm offer)

At the outset, a security company may be either a strong employer or a weak employer.
The strong-employer case occurs with probability 0.5, and the weak-employer case occurs with probability 0.5.
The company knows which kind it is.
The analyst does not observe that directly.

The company then decides whether to make the analyst an offer.
If no offer is made, the interaction ends and both sides get 0.

If a strong employer makes an offer, the analyst chooses whether to accept or reject it.
If the analyst accepts, the final reward is 2 for the company and 2 for the analyst.
If the analyst rejects, the final reward is -1 for the company and 0 for the analyst.

If a weak employer makes an offer, the analyst again chooses whether to accept or reject it.
The analyst cannot tell whether the offer came from a strong employer or a weak one.
If the analyst accepts, the final reward is 2 for the company and -1 for the analyst.
If the analyst rejects, the final reward is -1 for the company and 0 for the analyst.
