V3 [Repair contract]

A technician is deciding whether to take a repair contract from a homeowner.
Chance first determines whether the repair job is EasyTask or DifficultTask, each with probability 0.50.

The technician does not know the true difficulty when making the first choice.
So the technician first chooses between TakeContract (IN) and DeclineContract (OUT).

If the technician chooses DeclineContract (OUT), the interaction ends immediately.
The technician gets 30 and the homeowner gets 25.

If the technician chooses TakeContract (IN), the technician then chooses between HighEffort (HIGH) and LowEffort (LOW).
The technician still does not know whether the repair job is easy or difficult.

If the repair job is easy and the technician chooses HighEffort (HIGH), the result is 20 for the technician and 50 for the homeowner.
If the repair job is easy and the technician chooses LowEffort (LOW), the result is 50 for the technician and 35 for the homeowner.

If the repair job is difficult and the technician chooses HighEffort (HIGH), the result is 20 for the technician and 50 for the homeowner.
If the repair job is difficult and the technician chooses LowEffort (LOW), the result is 0 for the technician and 35 for the homeowner.
