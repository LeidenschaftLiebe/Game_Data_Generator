V4 (airline route strategy)

An airline first chooses between route strategy A, route strategy B, and route strategy C.
If it chooses route strategy A, the rival airline then chooses between response X and response Y.
If the rival chooses X after A, the outcome is 2 for the first airline and 5 for the rival.
If the rival chooses Y after A, the outcome is 5 for the first airline and 2 for the rival.

If the first airline chooses route strategy B, the rival again chooses between response X and response Y.
The rival cannot tell whether the first airline chose route strategy A or route strategy B when making that choice.
If the rival chooses X after B, the outcome is 5 for the first airline and 2 for the rival.
If the rival chooses Y after B, the outcome is 2 for the first airline and 5 for the rival.

If the first airline chooses route strategy C, it then chooses between schedule plan W and schedule plan Z.
Schedule plan W gives 2 to each side.
Schedule plan Z gives 3 to each side.
