V3 (space mission contractor)

At the outset, a contractor may be either highly reliable or poorly reliable.
The highly reliable case occurs with probability 0.5, and the poorly reliable case occurs with probability 0.5.
The contractor knows its own quality.
The engineer considering the job does not.

The contractor then decides whether to make a hiring offer.
If no offer is made, the interaction ends and both sides get 0.

If a highly reliable contractor makes an offer, the engineer chooses whether to accept or reject it.
If the engineer accepts, the final reward is 2 for the contractor and 2 for the engineer.
If the engineer rejects, the final reward is -1 for the contractor and 0 for the engineer.

If a poorly reliable contractor makes an offer, the engineer again chooses whether to accept or reject it.
The engineer cannot tell whether the offer came from a highly reliable contractor or a poorly reliable one.
If the engineer accepts, the final reward is 2 for the contractor and -1 for the engineer.
If the engineer rejects, the final reward is -1 for the contractor and 0 for the engineer.
