V5 — Archive Search Paths

An archivist first places the researcher at Shelf Corridor A or Shelf Corridor B.

At the first aisle split, the researcher chooses FirstLeftAisle [left1] or FirstRightAisle [right1].

At the second aisle split, the researcher chooses SecondLeftAisle [left2] or SecondRightAisle [right2].

From Shelf Corridor A, choosing FirstLeftAisle [left1] always ends at a blocked cabinet.
From Shelf Corridor A, choosing FirstRightAisle [right1] and then SecondRightAisle [right2] also ends at a blocked cabinet.
From Shelf Corridor A, choosing FirstRightAisle [right1] and then SecondLeftAisle [left2] reaches the final archive gate.

From Shelf Corridor B, choosing FirstLeftAisle [left1] always ends at a blocked cabinet.
From Shelf Corridor B, choosing FirstRightAisle [right1] and then SecondRightAisle [right2] ends at a blocked cabinet.
From Shelf Corridor B, choosing FirstRightAisle [right1] and then SecondLeftAisle [left2] reaches the final archive gate.

At the final archive gate, the researcher remembers the turns taken but cannot tell which shelf corridor was the starting point.

Then the researcher chooses LeftGate [left3] or RightGate [right3].

If the route began at Shelf Corridor A, LeftGate [left3] leads to the needed file and RightGate [right3] is blocked.
If the route began at Shelf Corridor B, LeftGate [left3] is blocked and RightGate [right3] leads to the needed file.

Finding the file gives reward 1.
Any blocked route gives reward 0.
