V4 — Charity fundraising campaign

A campaign director moves first.
She can run a simple local fundraiser (A), and the campaign ends with reward 5 to each participant.

Or she can hand part of the process to a major sponsor liaison (B).

The sponsor liaison can stop the broader campaign (C) or keep it moving (D).

If he stops it (C), the result is reward 7 to the director, reward 9 to the liaison,
and a loss of 3 to the beneficiary organization.

If he keeps it moving (D), the director chooses again.

She can launch a standard appeal (E) or a large public campaign (F).

The standard appeal (E) ends with reward 8 to the director, reward 5 to the liaison,
and reward 0 to the beneficiary organization.

The large public campaign (F) lets the beneficiary organization make the final choice.

The beneficiary organization can endorse the campaign publicly (G) or keep some distance (H).

A public endorsement (G) leads to reward 11 to the director, reward 11 to the liaison,
and reward 9 to the beneficiary organization.

Keeping distance (H) leads to reward 2 to the director, reward 7 to the liaison,
and reward 10 to the beneficiary organization.
