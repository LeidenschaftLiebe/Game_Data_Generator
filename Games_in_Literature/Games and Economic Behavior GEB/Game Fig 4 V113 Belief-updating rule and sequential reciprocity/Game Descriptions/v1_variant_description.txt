V1 — Startup partnership

A startup founder moves first.
She can keep the company independent (A), which gives a steady result with reward 5 to each side involved.

Or she can pursue a partnership route (B) and send the matter to the business development lead.

The business development lead can block the deal (C).
That outcome gives reward 7 to the founder, reward 9 to the business lead, and a loss of 3 to the community partner.

He can also approve the process (D) and hand the next step back to the founder.

The founder then chooses between a limited rollout (E) and a full launch with shared visibility (F).

If she chooses the limited rollout (E), the result is reward 8 to the founder, reward 5 to the
business lead, and reward 0 to the community partner.

If she chooses the full launch (F), the community partner makes the final choice.

The community partner can fully commit (G) or stay cautious (H).

A full commitment (G) leads to reward 11 to the founder, reward 11 to the business lead,
and reward 9 to the community partner.

A cautious response (H) leads to reward 2 to the founder, reward 7 to the business lead,
and reward 10 to the community partner.
