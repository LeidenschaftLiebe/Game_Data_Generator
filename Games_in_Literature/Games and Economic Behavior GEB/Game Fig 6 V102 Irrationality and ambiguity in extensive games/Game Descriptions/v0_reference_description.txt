V0 — Bargaining

Set the discount factor to ρ = 4/5 = 0.8
Two players are dividing a pie across two stages.

In stage 1, Player 1 chooses one of three claims:
take 1/4, take 1/2, or take 3/4 of the pie.

After Player 1 makes that claim, Player 2 decides whether to Accept (A) or Reject (R).

If Player 2 accepts in stage 1, the game ends immediately.

If Player 1 claims 1/4 and Player 2 accepts, the result is 1/4 for Player 1 and 3/4 for Player 2.

If Player 1 claims 1/2 and Player 2 accepts, the result is 1/2 for each player.

If Player 1 claims 3/4 and Player 2 accepts, the result is 3/4 for Player 1 and 1/4 for Player 2.

If Player 2 rejects in stage 1, the game moves to stage 2.

In stage 2, Player 2 now makes an offer to Player 1.
The offer can again be 1/4, 1/2, or 3/4 for Player 1.

After that, Player 1 decides whether to Accept (A) or Reject (R).

Because this is the second stage, the payoffs are discounted by ρ = 4/5.

So if Player 2 offers 1/4 to Player 1 in stage 2 and Player 1 accepts,
the result is 1/5 for Player 1 and 3/5 for Player 2.

If Player 2 offers 1/2 to Player 1 in stage 2 and Player 1 accepts,
the result is 2/5 for each player.

If Player 2 offers 3/4 to Player 1 in stage 2 and Player 1 accepts,
the result is 3/5 for Player 1 and 1/5 for Player 2.

If Player 1 rejects in stage 2, the game ends with 0 for both players.
