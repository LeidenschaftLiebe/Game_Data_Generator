V0 — Example 5

There are two players.
Player 1 moves first and chooses between O, L, and R.
If Player 1 chooses O, the game ends immediately with reward 2 to Player 1 and reward 1 to Player 2.

If Player 1 chooses L or R, Player 2 moves next.
At Player 2’s decision point, Player 2 cannot tell whether Player 1 chose L or R.
Player 2 then chooses between A and B.

If the path is L, A, Player 1 moves again and chooses between C and D.
If C is chosen, the outcome gives reward 2 to Player 1 and reward 3 to Player 2.
If D is chosen, the outcome gives reward 0 to Player 1 and reward 2 to Player 2.

If the path is L, B, Player 1 moves again and chooses between E and F.
If E is chosen, the outcome gives reward 1 to Player 1 and reward 4 to Player 2.
If F is chosen, the outcome gives reward 0 to Player 1 and reward 0 to Player 2.

If the path is R, A, Player 1 moves again and chooses between G and H.
If G is chosen, the outcome gives reward 3 to Player 1 and reward 5 to Player 2.
If H is chosen, the outcome gives reward 0 to Player 1 and reward 1 to Player 2.

If the path is R, B, Player 1 moves again and chooses between J and K.
If J is chosen, the outcome gives reward 1 to Player 1 and reward 4 to Player 2.
If K is chosen, the outcome gives reward 0 to Player 1 and reward 0 to Player 2.
