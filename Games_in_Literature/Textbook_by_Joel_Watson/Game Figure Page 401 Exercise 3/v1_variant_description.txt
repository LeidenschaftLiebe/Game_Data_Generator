V1 (record label offer)

At the outset, a record label may be either a strong label or a weak label.
The strong-label case occurs with probability 0.5, and the weak-label case occurs with probability 0.5.
The label knows which kind it is.
The musician does not observe that directly.

The label then decides whether to offer the musician a contract.
If no contract is offered, the story ends there and both sides get 0.

If a strong label offers a contract, the musician decides whether to accept or walk away.
Accepting gives a final reward of 2 to the label and 2 to the musician.
Rejecting gives a final reward of -1 to the label and 0 to the musician.

If a weak label offers a contract, the musician again decides whether to accept or walk away.
The musician cannot tell whether the offer came from a strong label or a weak one.
Accepting gives a final reward of 2 to the label and -1 to the musician.
Rejecting gives a final reward of -1 to the label and 0 to the musician.
