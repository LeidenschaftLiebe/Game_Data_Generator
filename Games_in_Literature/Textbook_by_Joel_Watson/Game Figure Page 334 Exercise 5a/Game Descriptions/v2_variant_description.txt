V2 (space technology alliance)

At the outset, a major aerospace contractor may be either an honest partner or a hostile partner, with probability 0.5 for each.
The contractor knows its own type.
The smaller propulsion lab knows only that the two types are equally likely.

After learning its type, the contractor chooses whether to Support the joint mission or Disrupt it.
The propulsion lab chooses whether to Disclose its engine design notes or Seal them simultaneously.
When the lab makes that choice, it does not know the contractor’s type and does not observe whether the contractor chose Support or Disrupt.

If the contractor is hostile and chooses Support, then Disclose leads to a final reward of 5 for the contractor and 1 for the lab.
If the contractor is hostile and chooses Support, then Seal leads to a final reward of 0 for the contractor and 3 for the lab.

If the contractor is hostile and chooses Disrupt, then Disclose leads to a final reward of 8 for the contractor and -1 for the lab.
If the contractor is hostile and chooses Disrupt, then Seal leads to a final reward of 7 for the contractor and 0 for the lab.

If the contractor is honest and chooses Support, then Disclose leads to a final reward of 9 for the contractor and 9 for the lab.
If the contractor is honest and chooses Support, then Seal leads to a final reward of 0 for the contractor and 0 for the lab.

If the contractor is honest and chooses Disrupt, then Disclose leads to a final reward of 2 for the contractor and 0 for the lab.
If the contractor is honest and chooses Disrupt, then Seal leads to a final reward of 0 for the contractor and 4 for the lab.
