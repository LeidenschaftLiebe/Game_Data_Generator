=== Story 1 ===
Story 1: The Tech Startup Lottery

Four emerging tech companies—Player1 through Player4—compete for venture capital by selecting one of four risky innovation tracks (A1–A4, B1–B4, etc.). Most strategies yield modest outcomes (1–3 units of investor interest), but a few have wild spikes (Player1’s A3 = 15, Player2’s B4 = 20, Player4’s D2 = 30).

Each startup must read the room: go steady and safe, or gamble on the breakthrough. The game models how one strategic moonshot can rocket a team ahead—while others are left in the noise.

=== Story 2 ===
Story 2: Dungeon Relic Hunt

Four adventurers explore a cursed dungeon filled with relics. Each player chooses a path (A1–D4), hoping to claim the rarest artifact. Most paths yield small magical items (1–3 power), but a few contain legendary loot: Player1’s A3 (15 power), Player2’s B4 (20), and Player4’s D2 (30).

Players must guess each other’s moves, as some relics are powerful but trap-laden. The game simulates risk, prediction, and the thrill of treasure-hunting.

=== Story 3 ===
Story 3: Global Resource Draft

In a simulated U.N. summit, four nations select resource allocations for energy, water, minerals, and data channels. Most picks result in stable outcomes (1–3 GDP points), but a few give massive strategic leverage: D2 yields +30 points for Player4, A3 and B4 give +15 and +20.

Nations must balance greed and diplomacy. Choosing too bold a claim risks triggering counterbalancing alliances. A zero-sum world with sparse peaks where opportunity comes in flashes.

=== Story 4 ===
Story 4: Underground Hacker Battle

Four cyber syndicates execute hacks on a global network. Each action (A1–D4) is a target—some routine, some jackpot systems. Most grant small rewards (1–3 crypto credits), but a few offer massive heists: A3 (15), B4 (20), D2 (30). Timing and misdirection are key.

Only one hacker group can dominate a node, and they must avoid overlaps to maximize impact. It’s a game of cyberpunk cat-and-mouse, full of high-stakes bluffs and rare exploits.

=== Story 5 ===
Story 5: Sci-Fi Gladiator Arena

Four alien warriors enter a simulated battle arena, each selecting their weapon mode (A1–D4). Most moves cause light damage (1–3), but hidden among them are explosive strikes: A3 (15), B4 (20), D2 (30). Strategy and timing determine whether a player merely jabs—or lands a critical.

The arena favors cunning. A warrior that draws others toward predictable patterns can slip through the cracks and launch a surprise attack. It's asymmetric, chaotic, and dramatic by design.
