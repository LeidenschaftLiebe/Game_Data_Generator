V3 — International trade project

A country’s trade minister moves first.
She can keep the plan domestic (A), and the process ends with reward 5 to each party.

Or she can open the door to a regional trade arrangement (B) and let the next official respond.

The regional negotiator then decides whether to block the plan (C) or move it ahead (D).

Blocking it (C) gives reward 7 to the minister, reward 9 to the negotiator,
and a loss of 3 to the local industry group.

Moving it ahead (D) sends the decision back to the minister.

The minister then chooses a narrow agreement (E) or a broader cooperative package (F).

The narrow agreement (E) ends with reward 8 to the minister, reward 5 to the negotiator,
and reward 0 to the local industry group.

The broader package (F) gives the local industry group the final choice.

The industry group can support the package (G) or resist it while seeking stronger protection (H).

Support (G) leads to reward 11 to the minister, reward 11 to the negotiator,
and reward 9 to the industry group.

Resistance (H) leads to reward 2 to the minister, reward 7 to the negotiator,
and reward 10 to the industry group.
