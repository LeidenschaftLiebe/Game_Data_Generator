V2 — Art Authentication

An art dealer is deciding how to describe a painting.
Nature first determines whether the painting is Archive Piece (t1) or Collector Piece (t2).
The dealer sees the type and then sends either Label One (m1) or Label Two (m2).

The buyer observes the label but does not observe the true type of the painting.
So after Label One (m1), the buyer chooses between Bid Option A (a1) and Bid Option B (a2).
After Label Two (m2), the buyer again chooses between Bid Option A (a1) and Bid Option B (a2).

If the path is Archive Piece (t1), Label One (m1), Bid Option A (a1), the outcome gives reward 15 to the dealer and reward 10 to the buyer.
If the path is Archive Piece (t1), Label One (m1), Bid Option B (a2), the outcome gives reward 80 to the dealer and reward 80 to the buyer.

If the path is Archive Piece (t1), Label Two (m2), Bid Option A (a1), the outcome gives reward 25 to the dealer and reward 10 to the buyer.
If the path is Archive Piece (t1), Label Two (m2), Bid Option B (a2), the outcome gives reward 50 to the dealer and reward 50 to the buyer.

If the path is Collector Piece (t2), Label One (m1), Bid Option A (a1), the outcome gives reward 80 to the dealer and reward 80 to the buyer.
If the path is Collector Piece (t2), Label One (m1), Bid Option B (a2), the outcome gives reward 15 to the dealer and reward 30 to the buyer.

If the path is Collector Piece (t2), Label Two (m2), Bid Option A (a1), the outcome gives reward 50 to the dealer and reward 50 to the buyer.
If the path is Collector Piece (t2), Label Two (m2), Bid Option B (a2), the outcome gives reward 25 to the dealer and reward 30 to the buyer.
