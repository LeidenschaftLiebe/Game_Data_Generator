V1 — Doping Test

An athlete may find it easy or costly to submit fully to a deep anti-doping review.

At the start, the athlete is one of two types.
With probability 0.6 the review cost is low (L).
With probability 0.4 the review cost is high (H).

After learning the type, the athlete chooses among Open for Full Test (NBO), Stay Clean (NB),
and Dope (B).

If the low-cost athlete chooses Open for Full Test (NBO), the outcome is reward 0.5 to the athlete
and 1.0 to the regulator.

If the high-cost athlete chooses Open for Full Test (NBO), the outcome is reward 0.1 to the athlete
and 1.0 to the regulator.

If the athlete instead chooses Stay Clean (NB) or Dope (B), the regulator moves.

The regulator only sees that there was no full opening.
The regulator cannot tell whether the athlete is low-cost or high-cost, and cannot tell whether the athlete chose
Stay Clean (NB) or Dope (B).

The regulator chooses between No Sanction (NA) and Sanction (A).

If the path is Stay Clean (NB), No Sanction (NA), the outcome is reward 0.6 to the athlete
and 1.0 to the regulator.

If the path is Stay Clean (NB), Sanction (A), the outcome is reward 0.2 to the athlete
and 0.3 to the regulator.

If the path is Dope (B), No Sanction (NA), the outcome is reward 1.0 to the athlete
and 0.0 to the regulator.

If the path is Dope (B), Sanction (A), the outcome is reward 0.0 to the athlete
and 0.8 to the regulator.
