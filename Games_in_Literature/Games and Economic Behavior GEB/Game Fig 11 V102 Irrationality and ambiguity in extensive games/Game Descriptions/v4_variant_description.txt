V4 — Cyber Response

A firm is deciding how to respond to a major security issue.
The firm moves first and chooses between Shut Down System (O), Internal Patch (L), and Public Fix (R).
If the firm chooses Shut Down System (O), the matter ends immediately with reward 2 to the firm and reward 1 to the outside auditor.

If the firm chooses Internal Patch (L) or Public Fix (R), the outside auditor moves next.
The auditor cannot tell which of those two responses was chosen.
The auditor then chooses between Cautious Review (A) and Supportive Review (B).

If the path is Internal Patch (L), Cautious Review (A), the firm moves again.
The firm then chooses between Deploy Fix (C) and Scrap Fix (D).
If Deploy Fix (C) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 2 to the firm and reward 3 to the auditor.
If Scrap Fix (D) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 0 to the firm and reward 2 to the auditor.

If the path is Internal Patch (L), Supportive Review (B), the firm moves again.
The firm then chooses between Continue Update (E) and Stop Update (F).
If Continue Update (E) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 1 to the firm and reward 4 to the auditor.
If Stop Update (F) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 0 to both players.

If the path is Public Fix (R), Cautious Review (A), the firm moves again.
The firm then chooses between Roll Out Fully (G) and Pause Rollout (H).
If Roll Out Fully (G) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 3 to the firm and reward 5 to the auditor.
If Pause Rollout (H) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 0 to the firm and reward 1 to the auditor.

If the path is Public Fix (R), Supportive Review (B), the firm moves again.
The firm then chooses between Maintain Release (J) and Withdraw Release (K).
If Maintain Release (J) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 1 to the firm and reward 4 to the auditor.
If Withdraw Release (K) is chosen, the outcome gives reward 0 to both players.
