11. The Sailors’ Passage

Two sailors race toward a narrow strait. Each must decide whether to yield or hold course.

If one yields while the other holds course, the one holding gains a prize, while the one yielding suffers a penalty.
If both yield, neither gains the upper hand, but each earns a smaller prize for avoiding collision.
If both hold course, they collide and each suffer the greatest ruin.
