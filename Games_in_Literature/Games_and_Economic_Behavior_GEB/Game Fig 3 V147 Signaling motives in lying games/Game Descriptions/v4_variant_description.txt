V4 [Academic self-report]

A student is deciding whether to enter a grading route that may later allow self-reporting after seeing the result.
The student first chooses between VerifiedRoute (i = 0) and SelfReportRoute (i = 1).

After VerifiedRoute (i = 0), chance leads to the self-report stage with probability 1/10 
and to the fixed-result route with probability 9/10.
After SelfReportRoute (i = 1), chance leads to the self-report stage with probability 3/5 
and to another fixed-result route with probability 2/5.

Chance then determines the true score category: LowerScore (j = 1) or HigherScore (j = 2), each with probability 1/2.
If the student ends up on a fixed-result route, the final result matches the true score category: 
0 after LowerScore (j = 1) and 2 after HigherScore (j = 2).

If the student reaches the self-report stage, they choose between ReportLower (a = 1) and ReportHigher (a = 2).
After LowerScore (j = 1), ReportLower (a = 1) gives 0 and ReportHigher (a = 2) gives 2.
After HigherScore (j = 2), ReportLower (a = 1) gives 0 and ReportHigher (a = 2) gives 2.
