31. Soldier vs. Ambusher

A soldier must march through contested territory, while an ambusher waits in hiding.

The soldier can move along the riverbank or through the forest trail.
The ambusher can lie in wait at the riverbank or lie in wait at the forest trail.

If the soldier takes the riverbank, the ambusher succeeds by waiting there, but fails if waiting in the forest.

If the soldier takes the forest, the ambusher succeeds by waiting there, but fails if waiting at the riverbank.
