V3 — Trade Contract

A supplier and a retailer are bargaining over the margin on a supply contract.

In stage 1, the supplier proposes a margin split:
the supplier asks for 1/4, 1/2, or 3/4 of the surplus.

The retailer then chooses whether to Accept (A) or Reject (R).

If the retailer accepts in stage 1, the contract is fixed immediately.

If the supplier asks for 1/4 and the retailer accepts, the result is 1/4 for the supplier
and 3/4 for the retailer.

If the supplier asks for 1/2 and the retailer accepts, the result is 1/2 for each side.

If the supplier asks for 3/4 and the retailer accepts, the result is 3/4 for the supplier
and 1/4 for the retailer.

If the retailer rejects, the bargaining continues to stage 2.

In stage 2, the retailer makes the next offer.
The retailer can offer the supplier 1/4, 1/2, or 3/4 of the surplus.

The supplier then chooses whether to Accept (A) or Reject (R).

Any agreement reached in stage 2 is discounted by ρ = 4/5.

So an accepted 1/4 offer gives 1/5 to the supplier and 3/5 to the retailer.

An accepted 1/2 offer gives 2/5 to each side.

An accepted 3/4 offer gives 3/5 to the supplier and 1/5 to the retailer.

If the supplier rejects in stage 2, both sides get 0.
