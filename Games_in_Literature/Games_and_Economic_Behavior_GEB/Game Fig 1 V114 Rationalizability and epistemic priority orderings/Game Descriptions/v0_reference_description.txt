This is the article’s opening example.
Ann is considering whether to bribe Bob, who is a public officer.
The paper explains the sequence as follows: 
Ann can choose not to try, or try to bribe Bob; if she tries, Bob can report her or accept; 
if Bob accepts, Ann can either carry out her plan or back out and go to the prosecutor, which hurts both of them.

Ann moves first and chooses between N and B.

If Ann chooses N, the interaction ends immediately.
This gives 0 to Ann, and 0 to Bob.

If Ann chooses B, then Bob chooses between R and A.

If Bob chooses R, the interaction ends there.
This gives -2 to Ann, and 0 to Bob.

If Bob chooses A, then Ann chooses between P and I.

If Ann chooses P, this gives -1 to Ann, and -3 to Bob.

If Ann chooses I, this gives 1 to Ann, and 1 to Bob.
