V1 [Job assignment]

A manager learns whether a job is EasyTask or DifficultTask, each with probability 0.50.
After learning that, the manager chooses between NoDisclosure and ImmediateDisclosure.

If the manager chooses ImmediateDisclosure, the worker learns the job difficulty before deciding whether to enter.
If the manager chooses NoDisclosure, the worker does not learn the job difficulty before deciding.

The worker then chooses between AcceptJob (IN) and DeclineJob (OUT).
If the worker chooses DeclineJob (OUT), the worker gets 30 and the manager gets 25.

If the worker chooses AcceptJob (IN), the worker then chooses between WorkHard (HIGH) and WorkLight (LOW).
On the NoDisclosure side, the worker still does not know whether the job is easy or difficult when choosing effort.
On the ImmediateDisclosure side, the worker does know.

If the job is easy and the worker chooses WorkHard (HIGH), the result is 20 for the worker and 50 for the manager.
If the job is easy and the worker chooses WorkLight (LOW), the result is 50 for the worker and 35 for the manager.

If the job is difficult and the worker chooses WorkHard (HIGH), the result is 20 for the worker and 50 for the manager.
If the job is difficult and the worker chooses WorkLight (LOW), the result is 0 for the worker and 35 for the manager.
