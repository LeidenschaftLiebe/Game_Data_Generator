1) Venture Capital Pitch — $200 K

A venture capitalist begins with $200 K.
They may keep the full amount safely or invest it in a promising founder.
If invested, the business triples the funds to $600 K.
The founder can either share $300 K back (a fair split) or keep the full $600 K and leave the investor with nothing.





2) Family Business Expansion — $1 M

A retired parent has $1 million in savings.
They can keep it for themselves or trust their child to expand the family business.
If invested, the value doubles to $2 million.
The child may return $1 million (even split) or keep the entire $2 million.





3) University Innovation Fund — $500 K

A university committee controls $500 K.
They can either retain the amount for central administration or invest it with a research lab.
If trusted, the lab’s innovation triples the funds to $1.5 million.
The lab can then return $750 K or keep all $1.5 million.





4) Humanitarian Aid Partnership — $3 M

An international donor holds $3 million in aid funding.
They can keep it under direct management or transfer it to a local NGO.
If transferred, the funds are tripled to $9 million through community partnerships.
The NGO may return $4.5 million to share equally, or keep the full $9 million for itself.





5) Music Production Deal — $100 K

A music label executive has $100 K to produce a record.
They can keep it or fund an artist.
If invested, the project triples to $300 K in revenue.
The artist may return $150 K or keep $300 K.





6) Small Business Microloan — $20 K

A local lender holds $20 K.
They can either keep it or lend it to a small entrepreneur.
If lent, the business grows the funds to $60 K.
The entrepreneur can return $30 K (equal split) or keep $60 K.





7) Film Investment — $2 M

A film investor controls $2 million.
They may keep it or fund a director’s project.
If funded, the production’s value triples to $6 million.
The director can return $3 million or keep $6 million.





8) NGO Field Grant — $400 K

An international organization controls $400 K in development funds.
It can keep the funds centrally or send them to a field partner.
If sent, the resources double to $800 K through local projects.
The partner can return $400 K or keep the full $800 K.





9) Corporate Supplier Advance — $500 K

A corporation has $500 K to advance to a supplier.
They can hold it back or send it as a prepayment.
If sent, production efficiency doubles the value to $1 million.
The supplier can return $500 K (share evenly) or keep $1 million.





10) Technology Licensing Deal — $5 M

A tech firm holds $5 million in capital.
It can keep the funds or license technology to a startup partner.
If licensed, the value triples to $15 million.
The startup can return $7.5 million to share equally or keep the full $15 million.
