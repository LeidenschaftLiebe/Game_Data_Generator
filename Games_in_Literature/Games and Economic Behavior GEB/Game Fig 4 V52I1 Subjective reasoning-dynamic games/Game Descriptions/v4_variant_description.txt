V4 — Space Mission Alert

A mission team may encounter one of two system states.
Nature first determines whether the state is Minor Fault with probability 0.1 or Major Fault with probability 0.9.

If the system is in Minor Fault, the flight lead chooses between
Keep Local (Out1) and Send Alert (In1).
If the flight lead chooses Keep Local (Out1), the matter ends with reward 0 to the flight lead
and reward 0 to mission control.

If the system is in Major Fault, the flight lead chooses between
Keep Local (Out2) and Send Alert (In2).
If the flight lead chooses Keep Local (Out2), the matter ends with reward 0 to the flight lead
and reward 0 to mission control.

If the flight lead chooses Send Alert (In1/In2), mission control then chooses between
Emergency Override (U), Watch Closely (M), and No Override (D).
When mission control moves, it knows an alert was sent, but does not know which fault level occurred.

If the state was Minor Fault and mission control chooses Emergency Override (U),
the flight lead gets reward -1 and mission control gets reward 3.
If the state was Minor Fault and mission control chooses Watch Closely (M),
the flight lead gets reward -1 and mission control gets reward 2.
If the state was Minor Fault and mission control chooses No Override (D),
the flight lead gets reward 2 and mission control gets reward 0.

If the state was Major Fault and mission control chooses Emergency Override (U),
the flight lead gets reward -3 and mission control gets reward 0.
If the state was Major Fault and mission control chooses Watch Closely (M),
the flight lead gets reward -1 and mission control gets reward 2.
If the state was Major Fault and mission control chooses No Override (D),
the flight lead gets reward 3 and mission control gets reward 3.
