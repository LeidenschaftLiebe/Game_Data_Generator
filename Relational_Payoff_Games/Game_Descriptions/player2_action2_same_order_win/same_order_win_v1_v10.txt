Game descriptions for when player 1 = action 1, then player 2 wins with either action; 
when player 1 chooses action 2, player 2 loses either way, but action 1 results in a smaller loss.
V1 - V10: 

1. Archaeologist vs. Cave Collapse

An archaeologist (Player 1) decides to enter through the Wide entrance or the Narrow shaft. The unstable cave (Player 2) collapses unpredictably.

If the archaeologist enters the Wide entrance, the collapse succeeds either way (Rockfall A (win for Cave) or Rockfall B (win for Cave)).

If the archaeologist enters the Narrow shaft, the collapse always fails, though Rockfall A (smaller loss for Cave) is less damaging than Rockfall B (bigger loss for Cave).

This is zero-sum: the cave’s danger equals the archaeologist’s peril.






2. Chef vs. Food Critic

A chef (Player 1) decides to serve a Signature dish or an Experimental dish. A critic (Player 2) selects which part of the meal to spotlight.

If the chef serves the Signature dish, the critic always wins with their review (Focus A (win for Critic) or Focus B (win for Critic)).

If the chef serves the Experimental dish, the critic always loses, though choosing Focus A (smaller loss for Critic) softens the review compared to Focus B (bigger loss for Critic).

This contest is zero-sum: the critic’s reputation gain comes at the chef’s expense.






3. Explorer vs. River Rapids

An explorer (Player 1) must cross via the Main crossing or the Hidden ford. The rapids (Player 2) shift currents.

If the explorer uses the Main crossing, the rapids always prevail (Current A (win for Rapids) or Current B (win for Rapids)).

If the explorer uses the Hidden ford, the rapids always fail, though pushing Current A (smaller loss for Rapids) is less disastrous than Current B (bigger loss for Rapids).

This struggle is zero-sum: the river’s force is the explorer’s obstacle.






4. Politician vs. Media Cycle

A politician (Player 1) launches a campaign on Issue Alpha or Issue Beta. The media (Player 2) decides what to headline.

If the campaign is on Alpha, the media always shapes the narrative (Headline A (win for Media) or Headline B (win for Media)).

If the campaign is on Beta, the media always loses control, though choosing Headline A (smaller loss for Media) blunts the damage compared to Headline B (bigger loss for Media).

This game is zero-sum: the media’s framing success erodes the politician’s gains.






5. Pilot vs. Crosswinds

A pilot (Player 1) chooses to land on Runway 1 or Runway 2. The crosswinds (Player 2) shift.

If landing on Runway 1, the crosswinds always disrupt (Shift A (win for Crosswinds) or Shift B (win for Crosswinds)).

If landing on Runway 2, the crosswinds always fail to interfere, though Shift A (smaller loss for Crosswinds) is less costly than Shift B (bigger loss for Crosswinds).

This encounter is zero-sum: safe landings are failures for the crosswinds.

This game is zero-sum: favorable weather is unfavorable to the athlete, and vice versa.

10. Inventor vs. Market
