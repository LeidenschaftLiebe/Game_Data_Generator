V2 — Harbor Control

A harbor operation may begin under either Dock A or Dock B.
Nature selects the dock, with probability 1/2 for each.

If the operation begins at Dock A, the harbor chief makes an A-dock choice between Close Dock (Ea) and Continue Dock (Ca).
Choosing Close Dock (Ea) ends the process with reward 1 to the harbor chief and reward 0 to the inspector.
Choosing Continue Dock (Ca) passes the move to the inspector.

If the operation begins at Dock B, the harbor chief makes a B-dock choice between Close Dock (Eb) and Continue Dock (Cb).
Choosing Close Dock (Eb) ends the process with reward 1 to the harbor chief and reward 0 to the inspector.
Choosing Continue Dock (Cb) passes the move to the inspector.

If Continue Dock (Ca) or Continue Dock (Cb) is chosen, the inspector then chooses among
Open Lane (L), Red Flag (R), and Redirect (C2).
At that moment, the inspector knows the harbor chief continued, but does not know whether the earlier situation was Dock A or Dock B.

If the earlier situation was Dock A and the inspector chooses Open Lane (L), the game ends with reward 0 to the harbor chief and reward 3 to the inspector.
If the earlier situation was Dock A and the inspector chooses Red Flag (R), the game ends with reward 3 to the harbor chief and reward 0 to the inspector.
If the earlier situation was Dock A and the inspector chooses Redirect (C2), the harbor chief gets another turn in the B-dock situation.

If the earlier situation was Dock B and the inspector chooses Red Flag (R), the game ends with reward 0 to the harbor chief and reward 3 to the inspector.
If the earlier situation was Dock B and the inspector chooses Open Lane (L), the game ends with reward 3 to the harbor chief and reward 0 to the inspector.
If the earlier situation was Dock B and the inspector chooses Redirect (C2), the harbor chief gets another turn in the A-dock situation.

When the harbor chief later returns to the B-dock situation, the harbor chief again chooses between Close Dock (Eb) and Continue Dock (Cb).
Either choice then ends the process with reward 0 to the harbor chief and reward 2 to the inspector.

When the harbor chief later returns to the A-dock situation, the harbor chief again chooses between Close Dock (Ea) and Continue Dock (Ca).
Either choice then ends the process with reward 0 to the harbor chief and reward 2 to the inspector.

Whenever the harbor chief is making an A-dock choice, 
the harbor chief cannot tell whether it is the earlier A situation or the later one reached after Redirect (C2).
Likewise, whenever the harbor chief is making a B-dock choice, 
the harbor chief cannot tell whether it is the earlier B situation or the later one reached after Redirect (C2).
