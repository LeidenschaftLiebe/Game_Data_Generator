11. The Farmyard Contest

Two farmers pit their animals: rooster, dog, or goat.

Rooster startles dog, but is butted by goat.
Dog drives off goat, but is startled by rooster.
Goat topples rooster, but is driven off by dog.
If both send the same animal, the contest ends in a draw.
Every advantage gained by one farmer equals the setback of the other.






12. The Ancient Ritual

Two priests perform rites with sun, moon, or stars.

Sun outshines moon, but is dimmed by stars.
Moon guides stars, but is outshone by sun.
Stars blot out sun, but are guided by moon.
If both call the same power, the ritual is even.
One priest's rise reflects the other's fall.






13. The Street Game

Two kids play by choosing stick, stone, or leaf.

Stick breaks leaf, but is crushed by stone.
Stone smashes stick, but is covered by leaf.
Leaf wraps stone, but is torn by stick.
If both choose the same, the game is a tie.
One child's win is always the other's loss.
