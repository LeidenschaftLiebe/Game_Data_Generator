V1 — Salary Negotiation

A job candidate and an employer are negotiating pay for a role across two rounds.

In round 1, the candidate proposes one of three salary splits:
the candidate keeps 1/4, 1/2, or 3/4 of the total package value.

The employer then chooses whether to Accept (A) or Reject (R).

If the employer accepts in round 1, the negotiation ends there.

If the candidate asks for 1/4 and the employer accepts, the result is 1/4 for the candidate
and 3/4 for the employer.

If the candidate asks for 1/2 and the employer accepts, the result is 1/2 for each side.

If the candidate asks for 3/4 and the employer accepts, the result is 3/4 for the candidate
and 1/4 for the employer.

If the employer rejects, the negotiation moves to round 2.

In round 2, the employer makes the next offer.
The employer can offer the candidate 1/4, 1/2, or 3/4 of the package value.

The candidate then chooses whether to Accept (A) or Reject (R).

Because the deal is delayed to round 2, value falls by the factor ρ = 4/5.

So an accepted 1/4 offer in round 2 gives 1/5 to the candidate and 3/5 to the employer.

An accepted 1/2 offer in round 2 gives 2/5 to each side.

An accepted 3/4 offer in round 2 gives 3/5 to the candidate and 1/5 to the employer.

If the candidate rejects in round 2, both sides get 0.
