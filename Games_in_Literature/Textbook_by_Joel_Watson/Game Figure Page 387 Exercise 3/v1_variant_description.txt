V1 (wildlife rescue routing)

At the outset, a rescue mission may face either a high-visibility field condition or a low-visibility field condition, with probability 0.5 for each case.
The field leader learns which condition has occurred.
The support coordinator does not observe whether visibility is high or low.

If the mission is in the high-visibility condition, the field leader chooses between the local route and the relay route.
If the leader chooses the local route in the high-visibility condition, the final reward is 2 for the leader and 0 for the coordinator.
If the leader chooses the relay route in the high-visibility condition, the coordinator then chooses between urgent support and delayed support.
If the coordinator chooses urgent support there, the final reward is 3 for the leader and 2 for the coordinator.
If the coordinator chooses delayed support there, the final reward is 1 for the leader and 0 for the coordinator.

If the mission is in the low-visibility condition, the field leader chooses between the local route and the relay route.
If the leader chooses the local route in the low-visibility condition, the final reward is 2 for the leader and 0 for the coordinator.
If the leader chooses the relay route in the low-visibility condition, the coordinator then chooses between urgent support and delayed support.
The coordinator cannot tell whether this decision follows the relay route in the high-visibility condition or in the low-visibility condition.
If the coordinator chooses urgent support there, the final reward is 1 for the leader and 0 for the coordinator.
If the coordinator chooses delayed support there, the final reward is 1 for the leader and 1 for the coordinator.
