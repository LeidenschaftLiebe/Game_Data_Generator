V4 — Lab Procedure

A lab test may begin in either Mode A or Mode B.
Nature selects the mode, with probability 1/2 for each.

If the test begins in Mode A, the lab lead makes an A-mode choice between End Test (Ea) and Continue Test (Ca).
Choosing End Test (Ea) ends the process with reward 1 to the lab lead and reward 0 to the compliance officer.
Choosing Continue Test (Ca) passes the move to the compliance officer.

If the test begins in Mode B, the lab lead makes a B-mode choice between End Test (Eb) and Continue Test (Cb).
Choosing End Test (Eb) ends the process with reward 1 to the lab lead and reward 0 to the compliance officer.
Choosing Continue Test (Cb) passes the move to the compliance officer.

If Continue Test (Ca) or Continue Test (Cb) is chosen, the compliance officer then chooses among
Left Check (L), Right Check (R), and Transfer Case (C2).
At that moment, the compliance officer knows the lab lead continued, but does not know whether the earlier situation was Mode A or Mode B.

If the earlier situation was Mode A and the compliance officer chooses Left Check (L), the game ends with reward 0 to the lab lead and reward 3 to the compliance officer.
If the earlier situation was Mode A and the compliance officer chooses Right Check (R), the game ends with reward 3 to the lab lead and reward 0 to the compliance officer.
If the earlier situation was Mode A and the compliance officer chooses Transfer Case (C2), the lab lead gets another turn in the B-mode situation.

If the earlier situation was Mode B and the compliance officer chooses Right Check (R), the game ends with reward 0 to the lab lead and reward 3 to the compliance officer.
If the earlier situation was Mode B and the compliance officer chooses Left Check (L), the game ends with reward 3 to the lab lead and reward 0 to the compliance officer.
If the earlier situation was Mode B and the compliance officer chooses Transfer Case (C2), the lab lead gets another turn in the A-mode situation.

When the lab lead later returns to the B-mode situation, the lab lead again chooses between End Test (Eb) and Continue Test (Cb).
Either choice then ends the process with reward 0 to the lab lead and reward 2 to the compliance officer.

When the lab lead later returns to the A-mode situation, the lab lead again chooses between End Test (Ea) and Continue Test (Ca).
Either choice then ends the process with reward 0 to the lab lead and reward 2 to the compliance officer.

Whenever the lab lead is making an A-mode choice, 
the lab lead cannot tell whether it is the earlier A situation or the later one reached after Transfer Case (C2).
Likewise, whenever the lab lead is making a B-mode choice, 
the lab lead cannot tell whether it is the earlier B situation or the later one reached after Transfer Case (C2).
