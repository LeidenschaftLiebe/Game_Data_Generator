V0 — Imperfect Recall Example 4

The decision maker first reaches a point called d1 and chooses L or R.

If the choice is R, the interaction ends immediately with reward 0.

If the choice is L, the decision maker reaches another point that is in the same situation d1.
At that point, the decision maker again chooses L or R.

After that second choice, the decision maker reaches a later situation called d2.
At d2, the decision maker cannot tell which of the two possible d2 points has been reached.

If the decision maker reaches the left d2 point and chooses L, the reward is 1.
If the decision maker reaches the left d2 point and chooses R, the reward is 0.

If the decision maker reaches the right d2 point and chooses L, the reward is 0.
If the decision maker reaches the right d2 point and chooses R, the reward is 3.

The key feature is that the decision maker does not distinguish between the two d1 points, and also does not distinguish between the two d2 points.
