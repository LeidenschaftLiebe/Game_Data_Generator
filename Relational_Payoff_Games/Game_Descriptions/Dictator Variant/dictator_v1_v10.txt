1) Museum Exhibit Fund

A curator controls a single exhibit fund. 
They may keep everything for the flagship gallery, share a smaller portion with a community program, or split the fund evenly. 
The community program cannot respond.






2) City Street Beautification

A mayor’s office controls a one-time beautification budget. 
The office may retain the entire budget for central projects, pass a smaller portion to a neighborhood council, or divide the budget evenly. 
The council has no say.






3) Hospital Equipment Drive

A hospital administrator controls a pooled drive. 
They may keep the entire amount for central equipment, send a smaller share to a community clinic, or split evenly. 
The clinic cannot object.






4) Publishing Outreach Fund

A publisher oversees a fund for author outreach. 
They may keep the whole fund for marketing, pass a smaller share to a coauthor’s outreach, or split evenly. 
The coauthor cannot respond.






5) Startup Tooling Budget

A founder controls a tooling budget. 
They may keep everything for core development, pass a smaller part to a prototyping team, or split the budget evenly. 
The prototyping team cannot influence the result.






6) National Team Training Pool

A team captain controls a one-time training pool. 
They may keep the entire pool for elite camps, transfer a smaller portion to the general squad, or split evenly. 
The squad fund cannot respond.






7) University Lab Discretionary

A department chair holds a discretionary pot. 
They may keep everything for central lab upgrades, share a smaller amount with a student group, or split evenly. 
The student group cannot intervene.






8) Farmers’ Cooperative Surplus

A co-op leader controls the season’s surplus. 
They may keep all proceeds for central reserves, distribute a smaller share to member services, or split the surplus evenly. 
Members cannot vote on this round.






9) Festival Revenue Pot

A festival organizer controls the revenue pot. 
They may keep everything for next year’s headliners, transfer a smaller portion to volunteer programs, or split evenly. 
Volunteers cannot respond.






10) Climate Action Pool

A climate fund manager controls a pooled contribution. 
They may retain it fully for carbon accounting tools, route a smaller share to local projects, or divide the pool evenly. 
Local projects cannot alter the decision.
