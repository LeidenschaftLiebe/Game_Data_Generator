V2 — Clinical Escalation

A lead doctor starts by choosing between Standard Track (a) and Specialist Track (b).
If Standard Track (a) is chosen, the department head chooses between
Discharge (c) and Escalate Case (d).

If Discharge (c) is chosen, the case ends with reward 2 to the doctor,
reward 2 to the department head, reward 1 to the specialist, and reward 0 to the final reviewer.
If Escalate Case (d) is chosen, the final reviewer moves later.

If Specialist Track (b) is chosen first, the specialist chooses between
Escalate Case (e) and Treat Directly (f).
If Treat Directly (f) is chosen, the case ends with reward 1 to the doctor,
reward 0 to the department head, reward 2 to the specialist, and reward 0 to the final reviewer.

When the final reviewer moves, the reviewer cannot tell whether the earlier path was
Standard Track (a), Escalate Case (d) or Specialist Track (b), Escalate Case (e).
The reviewer then chooses between Clear (g), Modify Plan (h), and Block (k).

If the path is Standard Track (a), Escalate Case (d), Clear (g),
the outcome gives reward 3 to the doctor, reward 1 to the department head,
reward 0 to the specialist, and reward 0 to the reviewer.
If the path is Standard Track (a), Escalate Case (d), Modify Plan (h),
the outcome gives reward 2 to the doctor, reward 3 to the department head,
reward 1 to the specialist, and reward 2 to the reviewer.
If the path is Standard Track (a), Escalate Case (d), Block (k),
the outcome gives reward 4 to the doctor, reward 4 to the department head,
reward 0 to the specialist, and reward 1 to the reviewer.

If the path is Specialist Track (b), Escalate Case (e), Clear (g),
the outcome gives reward 0 to the doctor, reward 2 to the department head,
reward 3 to the specialist, and reward 2 to the reviewer.
If the path is Specialist Track (b), Escalate Case (e), Modify Plan (h),
the outcome gives reward 3 to the doctor, reward 3 to the department head,
reward 1 to the specialist, and reward 1 to the reviewer.
If the path is Specialist Track (b), Escalate Case (e), Block (k),
the outcome gives reward 4 to the doctor, reward 3 to the department head,
reward 4 to the specialist, and reward 0 to the reviewer.
