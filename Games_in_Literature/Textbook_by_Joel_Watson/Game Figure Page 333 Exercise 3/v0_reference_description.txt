Player 1 may be in the upper situation or the lower situation, with probability 0.5 for each case.
Player 1 knows which situation has been reached.
Player 2 does not know whether the game is at the upper node or the lower node when making a choice.

If the game is at the upper Player 1 node, Player 1 chooses between action L and action R.
If Player 1 chooses action L there, the final reward is 2 for Player 1 and 0 for Player 2.
If Player 1 chooses action R there, Player 2 then chooses between action U and action D.
If Player 2 chooses action U after the upper R branch, the final reward is 0 for Player 1 and 4 for Player 2.
If Player 2 chooses action D after the upper R branch, the final reward is 4 for Player 1 and 0 for Player 2.

If the game is at the lower Player 1 node, Player 1 chooses between action L' and action R'.
If Player 1 chooses action L' there, the final reward is 2 for Player 1 and 0 for Player 2.
If Player 1 chooses action R' there, Player 2 then chooses between action U and action D.
Player 2 cannot tell whether the observed decision point came after R or after R'.
If Player 2 chooses action U after the lower R' branch, the final reward is 0 for Player 1 and 0 for Player 2.
If Player 2 chooses action D after the lower R' branch, the final reward is 4 for Player 1 and 2 for Player 2.
