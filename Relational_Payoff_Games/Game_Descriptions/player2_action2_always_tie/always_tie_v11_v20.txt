Game descriptions for when player 1 = action 1, then player 2 loses with either action and the losses are the same; 
when player 1 chooses action 2, player 2 wins either way, and the rewards are the same.
V11 - V20: 

11. Author vs. Critic

An author (Player 1) publishes a Short story or a Novel. The critic (Player 2) writes a review.

If the Short story is released, the critic always loses. Reviewing Plot or Style makes no difference, since both lead to the same losing outcome.

If the Novel is released, the critic always wins. Focusing on Character depth or Pacing both result in the same winning outcome.

Zero-sum: the critic’s influence undercuts the author’s reception.






12. Coach vs. Player

A coach (Player 1) emphasizes Defense drills or Offense drills. The player (Player 2) reacts.

If Defense drills are chosen, the player always loses. Practicing Positioning or Tackling both end in the same defeat.

If Offense drills are chosen, the player always wins. Practicing Shooting or Passing both lead to the same success.

Zero-sum: training advantage flips completely.






13. Lawyer vs. Judge

A lawyer (Player 1) builds the case on Constitutional law or Case precedent. The judge (Player 2) reacts.

If Constitutional law is emphasized, the judge always loses. Responding with Dismissal or Objection makes no difference, since both result in loss.

If Case precedent is emphasized, the judge always wins. Citing Binding precedent or Similar case both bring the same winning result.

Zero-sum: legal footing rests entirely with one side.






14. Entrepreneur vs. Investor

An entrepreneur (Player 1) pitches a Prototype or a Business plan. The investor (Player 2) responds.

If the Prototype is pitched, the investor always loses. Challenging Feasibility or Scalability both lead to the same failure.

If the Business plan is pitched, the investor always wins. Questioning Valuation or Equity share both give the same winning outcome.

Zero-sum: every deal favors one at the other’s expense.






15. Journalist vs. Interviewee

A journalist (Player 1) asks about Personal life or Policy stance. The interviewee (Player 2) answers.

If the question is about Personal life, the interviewee always loses. Whether they offer Details or Privacy defense, both lead to the same loss.

If the question is about Policy stance, the interviewee always wins. Stressing Achievements or Promises both deliver the same success.

Zero-sum: narrative control shifts completely.






16. Teacher vs. Examiner

A teacher (Player 1) prepares pupils for Writing or Mathematics. The examiner (Player 2) sets the test.

If pupils are trained in Writing, the examiner always loses. Testing Essays or Grammar both produce the same losing outcome.

If pupils are trained in Mathematics, the examiner always wins. Testing Algebra or Geometry both lead to the same winning result.

Zero-sum: preparation advantage lies fully with one.






17. Politician vs. Rival

A politician (Player 1) frames the debate on Jobs or Immigration. The rival (Player 2) counters.

If the topic is Jobs, the rival always loses. Arguing Unemployment or Wages both end in the same defeat.

If the topic is Immigration, the rival always wins. Stressing Border policy or Integration both yield the same success.

Zero-sum: voter support swings entirely one way.






18. Debater vs. Challenger

A debater (Player 1) opens with Freedom or Security. The challenger (Player 2) responds.

If Freedom is the opening, the challenger always loses. Rebutting with Rights or Responsibility makes no difference, since both fail.

If Security is the opening, the challenger always wins. Arguing Safety or Enforcement both lead to the same winning outcome.

Zero-sum: rhetorical control is absolute for one side.






19. Doctor vs. Patient

A doctor (Player 1) prescribes Medication or Lifestyle change. The patient (Player 2) responds.

If Medication is prescribed, the patient always loses. Complying or refusing both bring the same losing result.

If Lifestyle change is prescribed, the patient always wins. Choosing Exercise or Diet both produce the same winning payoff.

Zero-sum: health progress benefits one at the other’s cost.






20. Director vs. Actor

A director (Player 1) focuses rehearsal on Blocking or Emotion. The actor (Player 2) adapts.

If Blocking is emphasized, the actor always loses. Adjusting Movement or Timing both end in failure.

If Emotion is emphasized, the actor always wins. Playing with Expressiveness or Subtlety both result in the same success.

Zero-sum: control of the performance belongs wholly to one.
