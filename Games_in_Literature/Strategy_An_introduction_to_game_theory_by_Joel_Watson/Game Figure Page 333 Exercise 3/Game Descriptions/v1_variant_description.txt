V1 (wildlife rescue mission)

A rescue team may face either an upper-field situation or a lower-field situation, with probability 0.5 for each case.
The field leader knows which situation the team is in.
The air-support coordinator does not know which situation has occurred when making a response.

If the team is in the upper-field situation, the field leader chooses between staying local and requesting remote support.
If the leader stays local, the final reward is 2 for the field leader and 0 for the air-support coordinator.
If the leader requests remote support, the air-support coordinator then chooses between urgent deployment and delayed deployment.
If the coordinator chooses urgent deployment after the upper remote-support branch, the final reward is 0 for the field leader and 4 for the coordinator.
If the coordinator chooses delayed deployment after the upper remote-support branch, the final reward is 4 for the field leader and 0 for the coordinator.

If the team is in the lower-field situation, the field leader chooses between staying local and requesting remote support.
If the leader stays local, the final reward is 2 for the field leader and 0 for the air-support coordinator.
If the leader requests remote support, the air-support coordinator again chooses between urgent deployment and delayed deployment.
The coordinator cannot tell whether the request came from the upper situation or the lower situation.
If the coordinator chooses urgent deployment after the lower remote-support branch, the final reward is 0 for the field leader and 0 for the coordinator.
If the coordinator chooses delayed deployment after the lower remote-support branch, the final reward is 4 for the field leader and 2 for the coordinator.
