V1 — Product Launch under Two Market Conditions

A random market condition is chosen first.

With probability 1/3, the market opens in ConditionV [v].
With probability 2/3, the market opens in ConditionVPrime [v′].

If the market opens in ConditionV [v], firm 1 chooses SmallPush [l] or Escalate [r].

If firm 1 chooses SmallPush [l], both firms get 0.

If the market opens in ConditionVPrime [v′], firm 1 chooses LargePush [L] or HoldBack [R].

If firm 1 chooses HoldBack [R], firm 1 gets 3/2 and firm 2 gets -3/2.

If firm 1 chooses Escalate [r] at ConditionV [v], or LargePush [L] at ConditionVPrime [v′], firm 2 then chooses CounterA [c] or CounterB [d].

When firm 2 moves, firm 2 cannot tell whether the current situation came after Escalate [r] at ConditionV [v] or after LargePush [L] at ConditionVPrime [v′].

If the current situation came after Escalate [r] at ConditionV [v] and firm 2 chooses CounterA [c], the payoffs are 3 and -3.
If the current situation came after Escalate [r] at ConditionV [v] and firm 2 chooses CounterB [d], the payoffs are -3 and 3.

If the current situation came after LargePush [L] at ConditionVPrime [v′] and firm 2 chooses CounterA [c], the payoffs are -3 and 3.
If the current situation came after LargePush [L] at ConditionVPrime [v′] and firm 2 chooses CounterB [d], the payoffs are 6 and -6.
