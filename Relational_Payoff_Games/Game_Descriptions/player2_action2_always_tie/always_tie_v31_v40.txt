Game descriptions for when player 1 = action 1, then player 2 loses with either action and the losses are the same; 
when player 1 chooses action 2, player 2 wins either way, and the rewards are the same.
V31 - V40: 

31. Spy vs. Safehouse

A spy seeks refuge, while the safehouseâ€™s defenses determine survival.

The spy can enter the abandoned warehouse or the fortified embassy.
The defenses can be locked doors or armed guards.

If the warehouse is chosen, the spy is always compromised, whichever defense appears.

If the embassy is chosen, the spy is always safe, no matter the defense.






32. Athlete vs. Stadium

An athlete competes in a tournament, while the stadium conditions shape performance.

The athlete can perform in the old arena or the modern stadium.
The stadium can bring poor lighting or slippery floors.

If the old arena is chosen, the athlete always performs badly, regardless of the condition.

If the modern stadium is chosen, the athlete always performs well, no matter the condition.






33. Merchant vs. Famine

A merchant chooses what goods to bring, while famine spreads.

The merchant can carry luxuries or grain.
The famine can demand bread or water.

If luxuries are carried, the merchant always loses, no matter the demand.

If grain is carried, the merchant always prospers, whatever the famine requires.






34. Soldier vs. Wall

A soldier attacks the enemy, while defenses hold.

The soldier can assault the crumbling wall or the reinforced tower.
The wall can resist with archers or oil.

If the crumbling wall is attacked, the soldier always fails, no matter the defense.

If the reinforced tower is attacked, the soldier always wins, whichever defense is used.






35. Diver vs. Oxygen

A diver plans the descent, while oxygen supplies run thin.

The diver can dive into the deep trench or stay near the reef shelf.
The oxygen loss can be slow leak or fast leak.

If the deep trench is chosen, the diver always suffocates, no matter the leak.

If the reef shelf is chosen, the diver always survives, regardless of the leak.






36. Farmer vs. Frost

A farmer tends crops, while frost threatens.

The farmer can sow in the frost hollow or the sunny slope.
The frost can fall in the hollow or on the slope.

If the frost hollow is chosen, the farmer always loses the crop, whichever way the frost comes.

If the sunny slope is chosen, the farmer always succeeds, no matter the frost.






37. Captain vs. Whirlpool

A captain steers a ship, while whirlpools churn.

The captain can pass through the danger strait or the safe channel.
The whirlpool can pull in the strait or the channel.

If the danger strait is chosen, the captain always sinks, no matter the whirlpool.

If the safe channel is chosen, the captain always sails through, whichever whirlpool appears.






38. Hunter vs. Blizzard

A hunter goes out to track prey, while a blizzard threatens.

The hunter can head into the exposed ridge or the sheltered valley.
The blizzard can strike the ridge or the valley.

If the exposed ridge is chosen, the hunter always fails, whichever way the blizzard strikes.

If the sheltered valley is chosen, the hunter always succeeds, no matter the strike.






39. Rider vs. Bridge

A rider chooses a crossing, while the bridge may give way.

The rider can cross the rotting bridge or the stone span.
The failure can be collapsed planks or broken supports.

If the rotting bridge is chosen, the rider always falls, regardless of the failure.

If the stone span is chosen, the rider always passes, whichever failure occurs.






40. Rebel vs. Patrol

A rebel sneaks into the city, while patrols roam.

The rebel can enter through the sewer grate or the city gate.
The patrol can sweep the sewer or the gate.

If the sewer grate is used, the rebel is always caught, whichever patrol appears.

If the city gate is used, the rebel always slips through, no matter the patrol.
