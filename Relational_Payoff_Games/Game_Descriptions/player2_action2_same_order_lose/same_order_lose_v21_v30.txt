Game descriptions for when player 1 = action 1, then player 2 loses with either action; 
when player 1 chooses action 2, player 2 wins either way, but action 1 results in a bigger win.
V21 - V30: 

21. Manager vs. Team Lead

A manager (Player 1) assigns focus to Budgeting or Execution. The team lead (Player 2) reacts.

If Budgeting is chosen, the team lead always loses, whether responding with Cutting costs (loss for Team Lead) or Delaying tasks (loss for Team Lead).

If Execution is chosen, the team lead always wins, though Streamlining processes (better win for Team Lead) is stronger than Reassigning staff (weaker win for Team Lead).

Zero-sum: one side’s control over workflow is the other’s loss.






22. Athlete vs. Rival

An athlete (Player 1) decides to compete in the 100m sprint or the 1500m race. The rival (Player 2) chooses an event to specialize in.

If the 100m sprint is chosen, the rival always loses, whether training in Starts (loss for Rival) or Speed (loss for Rival).

If the 1500m race is chosen, the rival always wins, though focusing on Endurance (better win for Rival) is stronger than focusing on Pacing (weaker win for Rival).

Zero-sum: athletic advantage lies entirely with one side.






23. Teacher vs. Student

A teacher (Player 1) tests on Geography or Mathematics. The student (Player 2) studies in advance.

If the test is on Mathematics, the student always wins, though Practicing Algebra (better win for Student) is stronger than Practicing Geometry (weaker win for Student).

Zero-sum: preparedness is decisive for one over the other.
