Strategic Definition:
Better-Response Equivalence

Constraints

Nature moves first and chooses between two branches with probability 1/2 each.
On the left branch, Player 2 moves first and chooses between an outside action and an inside action.
On the right branch, Player 1 moves first and chooses between an outside action and an inside action.
Player 1 has one infoset joining the two upper nodes, so at the upper move Player 1 cannot tell 
whether play came from the left branch after Player 2 chose IN, or from the right branch after Player 1 earlier chose In.
Seed payoff constraint: left outside outcome gives 1, -1.
