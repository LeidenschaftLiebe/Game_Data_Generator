V1 (tech product launch rivalry)

A high-profile product leader decides whether to remain at a major tech company or resign to build a competing startup. 
Staying keeps everything calm: the departing leader ends up with 35 and the incumbent CEO ends up with 100. 
If the leader resigns, the incumbent CEO decides whether to green-light a major product launch. 
The catch is that the departing leader must decide whether to build and ship the rival product before knowing whether 
the incumbent has chosen to launch, because internal planning and public announcements don’t line up.

If the incumbent does not launch and the startup ships, the startup leader gets 80 while the incumbent CEO gets 0; 
If the startup does not ship, both end up with 0. If the incumbent does launch and the startup backs off, the startup leader gets 0 while the incumbent CEO gets 140. 
If both commit to launching, the startup leader faces one more decision about timing: 
rushing an early release leads to 40 for the startup leader and 110 for the incumbent, 
while keeping a normal release schedule leads to 13 for the startup leader and 120 for the incumbent.
