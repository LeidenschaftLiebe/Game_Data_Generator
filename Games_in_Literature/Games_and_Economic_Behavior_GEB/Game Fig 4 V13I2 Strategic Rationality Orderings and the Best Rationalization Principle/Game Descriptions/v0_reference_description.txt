V0 — Fig. 4 from the article

Player I moves first and chooses L or R.

Player II moves next and chooses l or r.
When player II moves, player II cannot tell whether player I chose L or R.

If player I chose R and player II chooses r, the interaction ends immediately.
All three players get 0.

In all other cases, the move passes to player III, who chooses a, b, or c.

When player III moves, player III cannot tell which of the three possible earlier situations led to this point.

If player I chose L and player II chose l, then player III choosing a gives reward 1, 1, 0 to each player, respectively.
In that same case, choosing b gives -1, -1, -1, and choosing c gives 1, 1, 0.

If player I chose L and player II chose r, then player III choosing a gives 1, 0, 0.
In that same case, choosing b gives -1, -1, 1, and choosing c gives 1, 0, 2.

If player I chose R and player II chose l, then player III choosing a gives 0, 1, 2.
In that same case, choosing b gives -1, -1, 1, and choosing c gives 0, 1, 0.
