Game descriptions for matching actions (match is a win for player 2 and mismatch is a win for player 1). 

V21 - V30: 

21. Detective vs. Thief

A thief (Player 1) hides stolen goods in the Warehouse or the Basement. A detective (Player 2) decides where to search.

If the thief hides in the Warehouse, searching the Warehouse (win for Detective) recovers the goods; searching the Basement (loss for Detective) fails.

If the thief hides in the Basement, searching the Basement (win for Detective) works; searching the Warehouse (loss for Detective) misses.

This pursuit is zero-sum: the detective’s recovery is the thief’s loss.






22. Farmer vs. Locust

A farmer (Player 1) plants crops in the North field or the South field. A locust swarm (Player 2) decides where to descend.

If planting in the North field, descending on the North field (win for Locust) destroys the crop; descending on the South field (loss for Locust) fails.

If planting in the South field, descending on the South field (win for Locust) succeeds; descending on the North field (loss for Locust) fails.

This situation is zero-sum: every loss for the farmer is a gain for the locusts.






23. Smuggler vs. Patrol Boat

A smuggler (Player 1) chooses the River route or the Sea route. A patrol boat (Player 2) picks where to intercept.

If the smuggler takes the River, patrolling the River (win for Patrol) intercepts; patrolling the Sea (loss for Patrol) fails.

If the smuggler takes the Sea, patrolling the Sea (win for Patrol) intercepts; patrolling the River (loss for Patrol) fails.

This encounter is zero-sum: patrol success is smuggler failure.






24. Student vs. Teacher

A student (Player 1) cheats using Phone or Notes. The teacher (Player 2) watches.

If the student uses the Phone, watching the Phone (win for Teacher) catches them; watching the Notes (loss for Teacher) misses.

If the student uses Notes, watching the Notes (win for Teacher) succeeds; watching the Phone (loss for Teacher) fails.

This exam is zero-sum: catching the cheat is loss for the student.






25. Deer vs. Hunter

A deer (Player 1) runs into the Thicket or the Glade. The hunter (Player 2) takes aim.

If the deer runs into the Thicket, aiming at the Thicket (win for Hunter) succeeds; aiming at the Glade (loss for Hunter) fails.

If the deer runs into the Glade, aiming at the Glade (win for Hunter) works; aiming at the Thicket (loss for Hunter) fails.

This hunt is zero-sum: the hunter’s gain is the deer’s loss.






26. Courier vs. Spy

A courier (Player 1) delivers documents by Train or by Carriage. A spy (Player 2) attempts interception.

If by Train, intercepting the Train (win for Spy) succeeds; intercepting the Carriage (loss for Spy) fails.

If by Carriage, intercepting the Carriage (win for Spy) works; intercepting the Train (loss for Spy) fails.

This contest is zero-sum: the spy’s success is the courier’s failure.






27. Soldier vs. Sniper

A soldier (Player 1) crosses either the Bridge or the Tunnel. A sniper (Player 2) takes position.

If the soldier crosses the Bridge, aiming at the Bridge (win for Sniper) succeeds; aiming at the Tunnel (loss for Sniper) fails.

If the soldier crosses the Tunnel, aiming at the Tunnel (win for Sniper) works; aiming at the Bridge (loss for Sniper) fails.

This standoff is zero-sum: the sniper’s hit is the soldier’s downfall.






28. Pilot vs. Anti-Air Battery

A pilot (Player 1) flies along the East route or the West route. An anti-air unit (Player 2) positions its guns.

If flying East, firing at the East (win for Battery) succeeds; firing at the West (loss for Battery) fails.

If flying West, firing at the West (win for Battery) succeeds; firing at the East (loss for Battery) fails.

This battle is zero-sum: one side’s hit is the other’s failure.






29. Fisher vs. Seal

A fisher (Player 1) sets nets in the Shallow bay or the Deep inlet. A seal (Player 2) dives for fish.

If nets are in the Shallow bay, diving there (win for Seal) steals fish; diving in the Deep inlet (loss for Seal) fails.

If nets are in the Deep inlet, diving there (win for Seal) succeeds; diving in the Shallow bay (loss for Seal) fails.

This struggle is zero-sum: seal’s feast is fisher’s loss.






30. Spy vs. Border Guard

A spy (Player 1) crosses via the Bridge or the Tunnel. The border guard (Player 2) patrols.

If crossing the Bridge, patrolling the Bridge (win for Guard) catches them; patrolling the Tunnel (loss for Guard) misses.

If crossing the Tunnel, patrolling the Tunnel (win for Guard) succeeds; patrolling the Bridge (loss for Guard) fails.

This operation is zero-sum: the guard’s success equals the spy’s failure.
