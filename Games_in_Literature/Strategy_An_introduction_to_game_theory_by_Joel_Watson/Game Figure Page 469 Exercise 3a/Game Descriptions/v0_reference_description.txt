V0 (closest to the textbook)

At the outset, nature selects A or B with equal probability.
Player 1 observes which case has been selected.
Player 2 does not observe whether the game is in A or B.

If nature selects A, Player 1 chooses between U and D.
After that, Player 2 chooses between L and R without knowing whether Player 1 faced A or B.
If A and U are chosen, then L gives a final reward of 2 for Player 1 and 2 for Player 2, while R gives 0 for both.
If A and D are chosen, then L gives 0 for both, while R gives 4 for Player 1 and 4 for Player 2.

If nature selects B, Player 1 chooses between u and d.
Player 2 again chooses between L and R without knowing whether the game is in A or B.
If B and u are chosen, then L gives a final reward of 0 for Player 1 and 2 for Player 2, while R gives 2 for Player 1 and 0 for Player 2.
If B and d are chosen, then L gives 4 for Player 1 and 0 for Player 2, while R gives 0 for Player 1 and 4 for Player 2.
