V4 (sports recovery planning)

At the outset, an athlete may be in either a strong-recovery condition or a weak-recovery condition, with probability 0.5 for each case.
The athlete learns which condition applies.
The medical staff does not observe whether recovery is strong or weak.

If the athlete is in the strong-recovery condition, the athlete chooses between the local training plan and the supervised training plan.
If the athlete chooses the local training plan in the strong-recovery condition, the final reward is 2 for the athlete and 0 for the medical staff.
If the athlete chooses the supervised training plan in the strong-recovery condition, the medical staff then chooses between an aggressive program and a conservative program.
If the medical staff chooses an aggressive program there, the final reward is 3 for the athlete and 2 for the medical staff.
If the medical staff chooses a conservative program there, the final reward is 1 for the athlete and 0 for the medical staff.

If the athlete is in the weak-recovery condition, the athlete chooses between the local training plan and the supervised training plan.
If the athlete chooses the local training plan in the weak-recovery condition, the final reward is 2 for the athlete and 0 for the medical staff.
If the athlete chooses the supervised training plan in the weak-recovery condition, the medical staff then chooses between an aggressive program and a conservative program.
The medical staff cannot tell whether this decision follows the supervised plan in the strong-recovery condition or in the weak-recovery condition.
If the medical staff chooses an aggressive program there, the final reward is 1 for the athlete and 0 for the medical staff.
If the medical staff chooses a conservative program there, the final reward is 1 for the athlete and 1 for the medical staff.
