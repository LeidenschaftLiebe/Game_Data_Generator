V2 (space technology partnership)

A major aerospace contractor may be either an honest partner or an exploitative partner, with probability 0.5 for each.
The contractor knows its own type.

The contractor chooses whether to Buy a larger ownership share or Sell part of what it already owns.
The propulsion lab observes whether the contractor chose Buy or Sell, but it does not observe whether the contractor is honest or exploitative.
The propulsion lab then chooses whether to Reveal or Hide its engine design.

If the honest contractor chooses Buy:
When the lab reveals the engine design, the payoff is 15 for the contractor and 12 for the lab.
When the lab hides the engine design, the payoff is -1 for the contractor and 1 for the lab.

If the honest contractor chooses Sell:
When the lab reveals the engine design, the final reward is 5 for the contractor and 6 for the lab.
When the lab hides the engine design, the final reward is 5 for the contractor and -1 for the lab.

If the exploitative contractor chooses Buy:
When the lab reveals the engine design, the final reward is 5 for the contractor and -6 for the lab.
When the lab hides the engine design, the final reward is 5 for the contractor and 1 for the lab.

If the exploitative contractor chooses Sell:
When the lab reveals the engine design, the final reward is 15 for the contractor and -14 for the lab.
When the lab hides the engine design, the final reward is 0 for the contractor and -1 for the lab.
