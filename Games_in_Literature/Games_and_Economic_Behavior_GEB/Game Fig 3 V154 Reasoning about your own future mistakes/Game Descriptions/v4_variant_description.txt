V4 [Publishing decision]

An author and an editor are trying to get a manuscript through the final stage.
The author first chooses between the long submission route and the direct submission route.
If the author chooses the long submission route, the author next chooses between the revision branch and the clean branch.
If the author chooses the revision branch, the author then chooses between withdrawing the draft and sending it to final editorial review.
If the author withdraws it there, both sides get 0.

If the author sends it to final editorial review, the editor must decide without knowing whether the manuscript came from 
the revision branch, the clean branch, or the direct submission route.
If the author chooses the clean branch, the editor faces that same blind choice.
If the author chooses the direct submission route at the start, the editor again faces that same blind choice.

At the editor’s move, choosing accept gives both sides 1 after the revision-review path, 
gives the author 1 and the editor 0 after the clean branch, and gives both sides 1 after direct submission.
Choosing revise gives the author 1 and the editor 0 after the revision-review path, 
gives both sides 1 after the clean branch, and gives the author 1 and the editor 0 after direct submission.
Choosing reject gives the author 1 and the editor -1 after the revision-review path, 
gives the author 1 and the editor -1 after the clean branch, and gives the author 0 and the editor -1 after direct submission.
