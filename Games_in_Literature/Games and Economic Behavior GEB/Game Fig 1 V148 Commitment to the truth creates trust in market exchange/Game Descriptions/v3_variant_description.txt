V3 [Restaurant reservation]

A customer and a restaurant manager are arranging a prepaid booking.
The manager moves first and sends one of three messages: NoCommitment (m(E)), WillHonorBooking (m(S)), or WillKeepDeposit (m(K)).

After seeing the message, the customer chooses between Reserve (I) and Leave (N).
If the customer chooses Leave (N), the interaction ends immediately and the result is 2 for the customer and 0 for the manager.

If the customer chooses Reserve (I), the manager then chooses between HonorBooking (S) and KeepDeposit (K).
After NoCommitment (m(E)), HonorBooking (S) gives 4 to the customer and 4 to the manager, 
while KeepDeposit (K) gives 0 to the customer and 8 to the manager.

After WillHonorBooking (m(S)), HonorBooking (S) gives 4 to the customer and 4 to the manager.
If the manager instead chooses KeepDeposit (K), the manager is fined for dishonest communication.
With f = 2, that result is 0 for the customer and 6 for the manager.

After WillKeepDeposit (m(K)), KeepDeposit (K) gives 0 to the customer and 8 to the manager.
If the manager instead chooses HonorBooking (S), the manager is fined because the later action does not match the message.
With f = 2, that result is 4 for the customer and 2 for the manager.
