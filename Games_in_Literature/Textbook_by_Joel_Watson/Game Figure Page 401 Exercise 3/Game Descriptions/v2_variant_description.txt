V2 (museum internship offer)

At the outset, a museum may be either a high-quality employer or a low-quality employer.
The high-quality case occurs with probability 0.5, and the low-quality case occurs with probability 0.5.
The museum knows which kind it is.
The applicant does not.

The museum then decides whether to offer an internship.
If it makes no offer, the interaction ends and both sides get 0.

If a high-quality museum makes an offer, the applicant chooses whether to accept or reject it.
If the applicant accepts, the final reward is 2 for the museum and 2 for the applicant.
If the applicant rejects, the final reward is -1 for the museum and 0 for the applicant.

If a low-quality museum makes an offer, the applicant again chooses whether to accept or reject it.
The applicant cannot tell whether the offer came from a high-quality museum or a low-quality one.
If the applicant accepts, the final reward is 2 for the museum and -1 for the applicant.
If the applicant rejects, the final reward is -1 for the museum and 0 for the applicant.
