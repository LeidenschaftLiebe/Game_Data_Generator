V5 [Coral reef rescue]

A marine biologist and a dive navigator are deciding how to save a coral reef section.

The marine biologist first chooses between AnchorBase (X), TopCurrent (T), and BottomCurrent (B).

If the marine biologist chooses AnchorBase (X), the operation ends there.
This gives 4 to the marine biologist, and 4 to the dive navigator.

If the marine biologist chooses TopCurrent (T), the dive navigator chooses between LeftReef (L) and RightReef (R).
If the marine biologist chooses BottomCurrent (B), the dive navigator again chooses between LeftReef (L) and RightReef (R).

When making that choice, the dive navigator cannot tell whether the earlier current choice was TopCurrent (T) or BottomCurrent (B).

After that, the marine biologist chooses between UpperLine (U) and DeepLine (D).
When making that later choice, the marine biologist cannot tell which earlier pair of choices led there.

After TopCurrent (T) and LeftReef (L), UpperLine (U) gives 5 to the marine biologist, and 0 to the dive navigator, 
while DeepLine (D) gives 2 to the marine biologist, and 1 to the dive navigator.

After TopCurrent (T) and RightReef (R), UpperLine (U) gives 2 to the marine biologist, and 0 to the dive navigator, 
while DeepLine (D) gives 0 to the marine biologist, and 1 to the dive navigator.

After BottomCurrent (B) and LeftReef (L), UpperLine (U) gives 0 to the marine biologist, and 1 to the dive navigator, 
while DeepLine (D) gives 2 to the marine biologist, and 0 to the dive navigator.

After BottomCurrent (B) and RightReef (R), UpperLine (U) gives 2 to the marine biologist, and 1 to the dive navigator, 
while DeepLine (D) gives 5 to the marine biologist, and 0 to the dive navigator.
