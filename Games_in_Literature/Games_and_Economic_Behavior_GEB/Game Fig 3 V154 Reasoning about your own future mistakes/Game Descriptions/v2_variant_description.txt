V2 [Airport security]

A traveler and a security officer both want a trip to continue smoothly.
The traveler first chooses between entering the normal screening route and going to a special counter directly.
If the traveler enters the normal screening route, the traveler next chooses between a document-check lane and a rerouting lane.
If the traveler takes the document-check lane, the traveler then chooses between abandoning the trip and stepping into final inspection.
If the traveler abandons the trip there, both sides get 0.

If the traveler steps into final inspection, the security officer must choose without knowing whether the traveler came from the document-check lane, 
the rerouting lane, or the special counter.
If the traveler takes the rerouting lane instead, the security officer faces that same blind choice.
If the traveler goes to the special counter at the start, the security officer again faces that same blind choice.

At the officer’s move, choosing clear gives both sides 1 after the document-check path, 
gives the traveler 1 and the officer 0 after the rerouting path, and gives both sides 1 after the special-counter path.
Choosing hold for review gives the traveler 1 and the officer 0 after the document-check path, 
gives both sides 1 after the rerouting path, and gives the traveler 1 and the officer 0 after the special-counter path.
Choosing deny boarding gives the traveler 1 and the officer -1 after the document-check path, 
gives the traveler 1 and the officer -1 after the rerouting path, and gives the traveler 0 and the officer -1 after the special-counter path.
