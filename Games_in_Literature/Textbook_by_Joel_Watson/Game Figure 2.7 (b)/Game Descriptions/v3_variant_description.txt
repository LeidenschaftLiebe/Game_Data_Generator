V3 (coffee chains)

Two coffee chains are competing for the same morning customers.
Each chain sets its prices before knowing what the other chain has decided.
If both chains go with high prices, each earns 1 million.
If Chain 1 keeps prices high while Chain 2 undercuts with low prices, Chain 1 earns nothing and Chain 2 earns 2 million.
If Chain 1 goes low while Chain 2 stays high, Chain 1 earns 2 million and Chain 2 earns nothing.
If both chains go low, they split the customer base more evenly and each ends up with 0.5 million.
