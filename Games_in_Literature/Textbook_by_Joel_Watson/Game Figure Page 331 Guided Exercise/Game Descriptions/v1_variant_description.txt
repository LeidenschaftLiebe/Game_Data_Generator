V1 (festival ticket pricing)

A festival organizer may be running with either a high-cost budget or a low-cost budget, with probability 0.5 for each case.
The organizer learns that budget condition before posting a ticket price.
A rival promoter does not know the organizer’s cost condition.
The rival promoter only sees which of the two ticket-price options was posted.

If the organizer is high-cost, it chooses between ticket option 1 and ticket option 2.
If the high-cost organizer chooses ticket option 1, the rival promoter then decides whether to launch a competing event or stay out.
If the rival launches after seeing ticket option 1 from the high-cost organizer, the final reward is 0 for the organizer and 1 for the rival.
If the rival stays out, the final reward is 0 for the organizer and 0 for the rival.

If the high-cost organizer chooses ticket option 2, the rival again decides whether to launch or stay out.
If the rival launches after seeing ticket option 2 from the high-cost organizer, the final reward is 0 for the organizer and 1 for the rival.
If the rival stays out, the final reward is 2 for the organizer and 0 for the rival.

If the organizer is low-cost, it also chooses between ticket option 1 and ticket option 2.
If the low-cost organizer chooses ticket option 1, the rival then decides whether to launch or stay out.
If the rival launches after seeing ticket option 1 from the low-cost organizer, the final reward is 0 for the organizer and -1 for the rival.
If the rival stays out, the final reward is 2 for the organizer and 0 for the rival.

If the low-cost organizer chooses ticket option 2, the rival again decides whether to launch or stay out.
If the rival launches after seeing ticket option 2 from the low-cost organizer, the final reward is 0 for the organizer and -1 for the rival.
If the rival stays out, the final reward is 4 for the organizer and 0 for the rival.
