V4 — League Compliance

A club may find it easy or costly to open its records to the league.

At the start, the club is one of two types.
With probability 0.6 the disclosure cost is low (L).
With probability 0.4 the disclosure cost is high (H).

After learning the type, the club chooses among Open Records (NBO),
Follow the Rules (NB), and Break Salary Rules (B).

If the low-cost club chooses Open Records (NBO), the outcome is reward 0.5 to the club
and 1.0 to the league.

If the high-cost club chooses Open Records (NBO), the outcome is reward 0.1 to the club
and 1.0 to the league.

If the club instead chooses Follow the Rules (NB) or Break Salary Rules (B), the league moves.

The league only sees that the club did not open its records.
It cannot tell whether the club is type L or H, and cannot tell whether the club chose
Follow the Rules (NB) or Break Salary Rules (B).

The league chooses between No Penalty (NA) and Penalty (A).

If the path is Follow the Rules (NB), No Penalty (NA), the outcome is reward 0.6 to the club
and 1.0 to the league.

If the path is Follow the Rules (NB), Penalty (A), the outcome is reward 0.2 to the club
and 0.3 to the league.

If the path is Break Salary Rules (B), No Penalty (NA), the outcome is reward 1.0 to the club
and 0.0 to the league.

If the path is Break Salary Rules (B), Penalty (A), the outcome is reward 0.0 to the club
and 0.8 to the league.
