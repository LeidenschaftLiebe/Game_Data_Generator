At the outset, Player 1 may be in the upper case H or the lower case L, with probability 0.5 for each case.
Player 1 learns which case has occurred.
Player 2 does not observe whether the game is in case H or case L.

If the game is in case H, Player 1 chooses between action A and action B.
If Player 1 chooses action A in case H, the final reward is 4 for Player 1 and 4 for Player 2.
If Player 1 chooses action B in case H, Player 2 then chooses between action X and action Y.
If Player 2 chooses action X after B in case H, the final reward is 6 for Player 1 and 4 for Player 2.
If Player 2 chooses action Y after B in case H, the final reward is 0 for Player 1 and 0 for Player 2.

If the game is in case L, Player 1 chooses between action A' and action B'.
If Player 1 chooses action A' in case L, the final reward is 4 for Player 1 and 2 for Player 2.
If Player 1 chooses action B' in case L, Player 2 then chooses between action X and action Y.
Player 2 cannot tell whether the current decision point came after B in case H or after B' in case L.
If Player 2 chooses action X after B' in case L, the final reward is 6 for Player 1 and 0 for Player 2.
If Player 2 chooses action Y after B' in case L, the final reward is 0 for Player 1 and 6 for Player 2.
