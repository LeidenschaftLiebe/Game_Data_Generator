V5 — Rare Book Recovery

An archive specialist is deciding what report to send about a damaged manuscript.
Nature first determines whether the manuscript is Light Damage (t1) or Heavy Damage (t2).
The specialist sees the type and then sends either Report One (m1) or Report Two (m2).

The archive director observes the report but does not observe the true damage level.
So after Report One (m1), the director chooses between Recovery Method A (a1) and Recovery Method B (a2).
After Report Two (m2), the director again chooses between Recovery Method A (a1) and Recovery Method B (a2).

If the path is Light Damage (t1), Report One (m1), Recovery Method A (a1), the outcome gives reward 15 to the specialist and reward 10 to the director.
If the path is Light Damage (t1), Report One (m1), Recovery Method B (a2), the outcome gives reward 80 to the specialist and reward 80 to the director.

If the path is Light Damage (t1), Report Two (m2), Recovery Method A (a1), the outcome gives reward 25 to the specialist and reward 10 to the director.
If the path is Light Damage (t1), Report Two (m2), Recovery Method B (a2), the outcome gives reward 50 to the specialist and reward 50 to the director.

If the path is Heavy Damage (t2), Report One (m1), Recovery Method A (a1), the outcome gives reward 80 to the specialist and reward 80 to the director.
If the path is Heavy Damage (t2), Report One (m1), Recovery Method B (a2), the outcome gives reward 15 to the specialist and reward 30 to the director.

If the path is Heavy Damage (t2), Report Two (m2), Recovery Method A (a1), the outcome gives reward 50 to the specialist and reward 50 to the director.
If the path is Heavy Damage (t2), Report Two (m2), Recovery Method B (a2), the outcome gives reward 25 to the specialist and reward 30 to the director.
