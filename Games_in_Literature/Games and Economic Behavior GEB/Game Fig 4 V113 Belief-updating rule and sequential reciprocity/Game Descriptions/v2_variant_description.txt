V2 — Film production deal

A producer moves first.
She can keep the film as a small in-house project (A), which ends with reward 5 to each of the three people.

Or she can bring in an outside distributor (B) and let the negotiations continue.

The distributor then decides whether to shut the deal down (C) or keep it alive (D).

If the distributor shuts it down (C), the project ends with reward 7 to the producer,
reward 9 to the distributor, and a loss of 3 to the lead actor.

If the distributor keeps it alive (D), the producer chooses the next direction.

She can go with a modest release (E) or a major awards campaign (F).

A modest release (E) ends with reward 8 to the producer, reward 5 to the distributor,
and reward 0 to the lead actor.

A major awards campaign (F) gives the lead actor the last move.

The lead actor can actively promote the film (G) or refuse the push and protect personal interests (H).

If the lead actor promotes it (G), the result is reward 11 to the producer, reward 11 to the distributor,
and reward 9 to the lead actor.

If the lead actor refuses (H), the result is reward 2 to the producer, reward 7 to the distributor,
and reward 10 to the lead actor.
