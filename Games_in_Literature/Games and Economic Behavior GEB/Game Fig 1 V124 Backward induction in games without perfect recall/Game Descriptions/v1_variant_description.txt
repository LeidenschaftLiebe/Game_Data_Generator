V1 [Mountain rescue route]

A rescue coordinator and a field guide are trying to route a mountain team.

Weather first determines which side of the situation they are in, with probability 1/2 for each side.

On the left side, the field guide moves first and chooses between TurnBack (OUT) and EnterPass (IN).

If the field guide chooses TurnBack (OUT), the day ends.
This gives the coordinator 1, and the field guide -1.

If the field guide chooses EnterPass (IN), then the coordinator chooses between SouthRidge (S) and DownValley (D).
SouthRidge (S) gives the coordinator -2, and the field guide 2.
DownValley (D) gives the coordinator 2, and the field guide -2.

On the right side, the coordinator moves first and chooses between HoldBase (Out) and GoForward (In).

If the coordinator chooses HoldBase (Out), the result is immediate.
This gives the coordinator -1, and the field guide 1.

If the coordinator chooses GoForward (In), the coordinator later chooses between SouthRidge (S) and DownValley (D) again.
SouthRidge (S) gives the coordinator 2, and the field guide -2.
DownValley (D) gives the coordinator -2, and the field guide 2.

At that later upper choice, the coordinator cannot tell which side of the situation led there.
