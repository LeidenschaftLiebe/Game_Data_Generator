Game descriptions for matching actions (match is a win for player 2 and mismatch is a win for player 1). 

V1 - V10: 

1. Guard vs. Intruder

A late-night break-in is underway at a facility with two corridors. The intruder decides which corridor to enter, and the guard chooses where to block.

The intruder can move through the left corridor or the right corridor.
The guard can block the left corridor or block the right corridor.

If the intruder enters the left corridor, the guard wins by blocking left, but loses if blocking right.

If the intruder enters the right corridor, the guard wins by blocking right, but loses if blocking left.






2. Hacker vs. Security Analyst

A hacker launches an exploit against a company, while the analyst chooses which patch to apply.

The hacker can use Exploit A or Exploit B.
The analyst can deploy Patch A or Patch B.

If the hacker uses Exploit A, the analyst wins by applying Patch A, but loses with Patch B.

If the hacker uses Exploit B, the analyst wins by applying Patch B, but loses with Patch A.






3. Predator vs. Prey

A rabbit tries to escape from a fox by heading toward one of two hiding places. The fox chooses where to pounce.

The rabbit can run to the burrow or the field.
The fox can pounce at the burrow or pounce at the field.

If the rabbit goes to the burrow, the fox wins by pouncing at the burrow, but loses if it pounces at the field.

If the rabbit goes to the field, the fox wins by pouncing at the field, but loses if it pounces at the burrow.






4. Military Maneuver

Two generals face off. One moves troops along a route, while the other chooses where to intercept.

The advancing general can march through the valley or across the hill.
The defending general can intercept in the valley or intercept on the hill.

If troops march through the valley, the defending general wins by intercepting there, but loses if intercepting on the hill.

If troops march across the hill, the defending general wins by intercepting on the hill, but loses if intercepting in the valley.






5. Political Campaign

Two candidates compete for attention on the campaign trail. One sets the dayâ€™s focus, and the other chooses where to counter.

The first candidate can appeal to urban voters or rural voters.
The second candidate can counter in urban areas or counter in rural areas.

If urban voters are targeted, the counter-candidate wins by campaigning in urban areas, but loses if they focus on rural areas.

If rural voters are targeted, the counter-candidate wins by campaigning in rural areas, but loses if they focus on urban areas.






6. Courier vs. Bandit

A courier sets out on horseback, carrying valuables. A bandit waits in ambush.

The courier can take the north road or the south road.
The bandit can ambush the north road or ambush the south road.

If the courier takes the north road, the bandit wins by ambushing north, but loses if ambushing south.

If the courier takes the south road, the bandit wins by ambushing south, but loses if ambushing north.






7. Teacher vs. Cheater

During an exam, a student plans how to cheat, while the teacher decides what to monitor closely.

The student can cheat with a phone or with hidden notes.
The teacher can watch for phone use or watch for notes.

If the student cheats with a phone, the teacher wins by watching phones, but loses if watching notes.

If the student cheats with notes, the teacher wins by watching notes, but loses if watching phones.






8. Spy vs. Border Patrol

A spy attempts to cross a guarded border. Patrol units are stationed selectively.

The spy can cross via the bridge or the tunnel.
The border patrol can guard the bridge or guard the tunnel.

If the spy uses the bridge, the patrol wins by guarding the bridge, but loses if guarding the tunnel.

If the spy uses the tunnel, the patrol wins by guarding the tunnel, but loses if guarding the bridge.

9. Smuggler vs. Harbor Police

A smuggler brings in contraband by ship. Harbor police choose where to inspect.

The smuggler can dock at Pier A or Pier B.
The harbor police can inspect Pier A or inspect Pier B.

If the smuggler docks at Pier A, the police win by inspecting Pier A, but lose if inspecting Pier B.

If the smuggler docks at Pier B, the police win by inspecting Pier B, but lose if inspecting Pier A.






10. Striker vs. Goalkeeper

In a penalty kick, a striker picks a side and the goalkeeper dives to defend.

The striker can shoot left or right.
The goalkeeper can dive left or dive right.

If the striker shoots left, the goalkeeper wins by diving left, but loses if diving right.

If the striker shoots right, the goalkeeper wins by diving right, but loses if diving left.
