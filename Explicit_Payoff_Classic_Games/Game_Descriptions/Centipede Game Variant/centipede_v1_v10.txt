1) Startup Collaboration

Two entrepreneurs take turns deciding whether to take their share of profits or pass the opportunity to grow the business.
Each time someone passes, the project value increases. The game ends once it returns to the first entrepreneur’s final decision.

If the first stops immediately (take), they earn $200 K, and the other gets $100 K.
If the first passes and the second stops, they earn $300 K and $200 K.
If the second passes again and the first then stops, they earn $400 K and $300 K.
If both keep passing until the end, they each earn $500 K.





2) Academic Collaboration

Two researchers decide whether to publish now or pass and collect more data.
Each pass improves the quality of their results.
The game ends when the decision returns to the first researcher for the final round.

If the first publishes immediately, they earn $10 K, and the other earns $5 K.
If the first passes and the second publishes, the payoffs are $20 K and $10 K.
If the second passes again and the first publishes later, they get $30 K and $20 K.
If both pass through to the end, they publish jointly and each earn $40 K.





3) Investment Partnership

Each pass grows the total returns, but whoever takes first secures a higher share.
The game ends when it comes back to the first investor’s final chance.

If the first cashes out immediately, they receive $50 K, the other $20 K.
If the first passes and the second takes, they receive $80 K and $40 K.
If the second passes again and the first takes later, the payoffs are $120 K and $80 K.
If both pass through the end, they each receive $150 K.





4) Humanitarian Fund Allocation

Two directors control a joint aid fund. Each can release now or pass to let it grow through matching grants.
Every pass increases total resources, but whoever acts first secures the larger personal recognition and payout.
The game ends when it returns to the first director’s final decision.

If the first releases early, they receive $400 K, and the other $200 K.
If the first passes and the second releases, the shares are $600 K and $300 K.
If the second passes again and the first releases, they receive $800 K and $500 K.
If both pass until the end, they each get $1 M.





5) Publishing Contract

If the first passes and the second publishes, they receive $30 K and $20 K.
If the second passes again and the first publishes later, they receive $50 K and $30 K.
