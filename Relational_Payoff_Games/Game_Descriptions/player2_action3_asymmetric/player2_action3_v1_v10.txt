1) Chess Master vs. Challenger

A seasoned chess master chooses among three opening moves, while a challenger decides how to reply.

Master’s options: King’s Pawn, Queen’s Pawn, Fianchetto.

Challenger’s replies: Classical Defense, Aggressive Gambit, Flank Attack.

If the master opens King’s Pawn, the challenger does best with Classical Defense (win), still succeeds with Aggressive Gambit (smaller win), and fails with Flank Attack (lose).
If the master opens Queen’s Pawn, the challenger’s Aggressive Gambit is best (win); Flank Attack still loses but is less bad; Classical Defense is the worst loss.
If the master opens Fianchetto, the challenger’s Flank Attack is best (win), Classical Defense still wins but less strongly, and Aggressive Gambit loses.

Each success for one side comes at the expense of the other. 






2) Heist Planner vs. Police Commander

A heist crew selects an entry path; the police choose how to cover the museum.

Crew’s options: Front lobby, Side gallery, Skylight.

If the crew goes Front lobby, Lobby posts work best (win), Gallery patrol still succeeds (smaller win), Roof sensors fail (lose).
If the crew goes Side gallery, Gallery patrol is best (win); Roof sensors still miss but reduce damage; Lobby posts are the worst miss.
If the crew goes Skylight, Roof sensors are best (win), Lobby posts still win but less strongly, and Gallery patrol loses.

The more reward one gains, the more the other is forced to give up.
