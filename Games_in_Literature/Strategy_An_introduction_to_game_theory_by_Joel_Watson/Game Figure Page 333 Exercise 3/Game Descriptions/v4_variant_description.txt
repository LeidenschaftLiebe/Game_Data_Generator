V4 (sports rehab decision)

An athlete may be in either an upper recovery condition or a lower recovery condition, with probability 0.5 for each case.
The athlete knows which condition applies.
The medical staff does not know which condition the athlete is in when choosing a treatment response.

If the athlete is in the upper recovery condition, the athlete chooses between light self-training and requesting a supervised program.
If the athlete chooses light self-training, the final reward is 2 for the athlete and 0 for the medical staff.
If the athlete requests a supervised program, the medical staff then chooses between an intensive plan and a conservative plan.
If the medical staff chooses an intensive plan after the upper supervised branch, the final reward is 0 for the athlete and 4 for the medical staff.
If the medical staff chooses a conservative plan after the upper supervised branch, the final reward is 4 for the athlete and 0 for the medical staff.

If the athlete is in the lower recovery condition, the athlete chooses between light self-training and requesting a supervised program.
If the athlete chooses light self-training, the final reward is 2 for the athlete and 0 for the medical staff.
If the athlete requests a supervised program, the medical staff again chooses between an intensive plan and a conservative plan.
The medical staff cannot tell whether the request came from the upper recovery condition or the lower recovery condition.
If the medical staff chooses an intensive plan after the lower supervised branch, the final reward is 0 for the athlete and 0 for the medical staff.
If the medical staff chooses a conservative plan after the lower supervised branch, the final reward is 4 for the athlete and 2 for the medical staff.
