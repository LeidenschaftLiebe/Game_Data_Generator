Game descriptions for when player 1 = action 1, then player 2 loses with either action; 
when player 1 chooses action 2, player 2 wins either way, but action 1 results in a bigger win.
V31 - V40: 

31. Smuggler vs. Coast Guard

A smuggler charts a route while the coast guard chooses how to intercept.

The smuggler can sail by the reefs or through the open channel.
The coast guard can patrol with cutters or set radar checkpoints.

If the reefs are chosen, the coast guard fails either way; cutters or radar, both end in the same loss for them.

If the open channel is chosen, the coast guard succeeds regardless, with cutters yielding a bigger win than checkpoints.






32. Hacker vs. Security Team

A hacker launches an intrusion while defenders react.

The hacker can probe with a new exploit or with a phishing campaign.
The team can run intrusion detection or trace suspicious emails.

If the new exploit is used, the security team always fails, no matter which approach is taken.

If phishing is used, the team always succeeds, with detection yielding a bigger win than tracing.






33. Thief vs. Police Detective

A thief eyes two heists while the detective considers tactics.

The thief can hit the abandoned mill or the city bank.
The detective can follow informants or watch bank records.

If the abandoned mill is targeted, the detective loses either way; following or watching, the result is the same failure.

If the city bank is targeted, the detective always succeeds, and following informants produces a bigger win than checking records.






34. Soldier vs. Enemy Artillery

A soldier advances as the enemy positions heavy guns.

The soldier can march via the ravine or the ridge.
The artillery can aim at the ravine or the ridge.

If the ravine is chosen, the artillery loses either way; fire at ravine or ridge, both come to nothing.

If the ridge is chosen, the artillery prevails regardless, with firing at the ravine producing a bigger win than firing at the ridge.






35. Rival Entrepreneur vs. Market Regulator

An entrepreneur launches new goods while the regulator decides its move.

The entrepreneur can introduce a cheap knock-off or a premium model.
The regulator can audit imports or inspect pricing.

If the knock-off is launched, the regulator loses whichever path it takes.

If the premium model is launched, the regulator wins either way, with audits producing a bigger win than inspections.






36. Saboteur vs. Engineer

A saboteur infiltrates a factory while the engineer selects a safeguard.

The saboteur can tamper with the power grid or the water supply.
The engineer can monitor energy flow or check valves.

If the power grid is attacked, the engineer fails whichever precaution is used.

If the water supply is attacked, the engineer always prevails, with energy monitoring giving a bigger win than checking valves.






37. Student vs. Examiner

A student faces a timed oral test while the examiner decides which area to press.

The student can begin with ancient philosophy or with modern ethics.
The examiner can question definitions or probe examples.

If ancient philosophy is chosen, the examiner fails either way, whichever angle they press.

If modern ethics is chosen, the examiner always succeeds, with definitions yielding a bigger win than examples.






38. Rebel vs. Patrol

A rebel sneaks through the countryside while patrols search.

The rebel can take the forest path or the riverbank.
The patrol can sweep forests or watch river crossings.

If the forest path is chosen, the patrol loses either way, sweep or watch.

If the riverbank is chosen, the patrol prevails regardless, and sweeping the forest secures a bigger win than watching the crossings.






39. Athlete vs. Referee

An athlete competes while the referee chooses how to judge.

The athlete can attempt the long jump or the high jump.
The referee can scrutinize takeoffs or review landings.

If the long jump is attempted, the referee fails either way, takeoffs or landings.

If the high jump is attempted, the referee succeeds no matter what, and watching takeoffs brings a bigger win than reviewing landings.






40. Merchant vs. Customs Officer

A merchant ships goods while customs prepares inspections.

The merchant can send by the old trade road or the new checkpoint.
Customs can weigh cargo or inspect papers.

If the old trade road is chosen, customs always fails, whichever inspection they apply.

If the new checkpoint is chosen, customs always succeeds, with weighing cargo producing a bigger win than inspecting papers.
