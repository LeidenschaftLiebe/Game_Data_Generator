V4 [Security operations]

The threat environment first turns out to be either HighThreat (omega+) or LowThreat (omega-).

The security chief observes that and chooses between LeftRoute (L) and RightRoute (R).

The field coordinator then observes both the threat environment and the chosen route,
and chooses between TightProtocol (T) and BroadProtocol (B).

If the environment is HighThreat (omega+), then:
LeftRoute (L), TightProtocol (T) gives 3, 2.
LeftRoute (L), BroadProtocol (B) gives 1, 4.
RightRoute (R), TightProtocol (T) gives 4, 1.
RightRoute (R), BroadProtocol (B) gives 2, 3.

If the environment is LowThreat (omega-), then:
LeftRoute (L), TightProtocol (T) gives 2, 3.
LeftRoute (L), BroadProtocol (B) gives 4, 1.
RightRoute (R), TightProtocol (T) gives 1, 4.
RightRoute (R), BroadProtocol (B) gives 3, 2.
