11) International Aid Package

A donor government and a partner government must agree on how to distribute a single round of development funds.
The donor proposes either an even distribution that treats both sides symmetrically, or a donor-priority plan that allocates more to its own programs.
The partner can accept or reject. A rejection cancels the aid this round for both.






12) Trade Tariff Rebate

A national trade ministry and an exporters’ association must decide how to divide a one-time tariff rebate.
The ministry proposes either a shared rebate that treats both sides evenly, or a ministry-favoring rebate that assigns a larger share to government programs.
The association may accept or reject. A rejection voids the rebate for both.






13) Corporate Merger Severance Pool

An acquiring firm and a team representative from the acquired company must split a one-time severance pool.
The acquirer proposes either an even arrangement or one that directs more funds toward its own retention plans.
The team representative can accept or reject. Rejection eliminates the pool for both parties.






14) City Infrastructure Grant

A mayor’s office and a district council must divide a one-time infrastructure grant.
The mayor’s office proposes either an equal allocation across jurisdictions or a plan that prioritizes central projects.
The council may accept or reject. Rejection causes the grant to lapse for everyone.






15) Cross-Border River Usage

An upstream country and a downstream country must agree on a single allocation of water rights for the season.
The upstream side proposes either an equal sharing plan or a plan that gives itself the larger share.
The downstream side can accept or reject. Rejection ends this season’s arrangement, leaving both without the agreed allocation.






16) Disaster Relief Allocation

An international agency and a local NGO must divide a one-time relief shipment.
The agency proposes either a cooperative division that treats both missions evenly, or an agency-first plan that keeps more for its own distribution network.
The NGO can accept or reject. Rejection halts the shipment entirely.






17) University Endowment Allocation

A university board chair and a coalition of departments must divide a one-time endowment disbursement.
The chair proposes either a balanced plan or a chair-heavy plan that routes more funds to central initiatives.
The coalition can accept or reject. Rejection means the disbursement is postponed and the money is not released.






18) Tech Licensing Fee

A patent holder and a licensee must settle a one-time licensing payment division.
The patent holder proposes either an even split of the proceeds or a holder-first split that assigns itself the larger share.
The licensee can accept or reject. If rejected, the deal falls through.






19) Peace Dividend Reallocation

A government must divide a one-time peace dividend between defense and health.
The defense ministry proposes either a balanced reallocation across sectors or a defense-heavy plan.
The health ministry may accept or reject. Rejection means the dividend is not released.






20) Carbon Credit Sale

A forestry cooperative and an energy company must split the proceeds from a one-time carbon credit sale.
The cooperative proposes either an equal split or a cooperative-first split that gives itself the larger share.
The energy company can accept or reject. Rejection cancels the sale.
