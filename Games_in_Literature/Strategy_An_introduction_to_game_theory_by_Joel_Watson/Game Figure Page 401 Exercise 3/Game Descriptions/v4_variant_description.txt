V4 (sports club recruitment)

At the outset, a club may be either well run or badly run.
The well-run case occurs with probability 0.5, and the badly-run case occurs with probability 0.5.
The club knows which type it is.
The player does not observe that directly.

The club then decides whether to make the player an offer.
If no offer is made, the interaction ends and both sides get 0.

If a well-run club makes an offer, the player chooses whether to accept or reject it.
If the player accepts, the final reward is 2 for the club and 2 for the player.
If the player rejects, the final reward is -1 for the club and 0 for the player.

If a badly-run club makes an offer, the player again chooses whether to accept or reject it.
The player cannot tell whether the offer came from a well-run club or a badly-run one.
If the player accepts, the final reward is 2 for the club and -1 for the player.
If the player rejects, the final reward is -1 for the club and 0 for the player.
