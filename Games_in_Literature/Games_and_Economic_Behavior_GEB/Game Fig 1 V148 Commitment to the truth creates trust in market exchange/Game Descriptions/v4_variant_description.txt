V4 [Repair service]

A homeowner and a repair technician are deciding whether to proceed with a paid service call.
The technician moves first and sends one of three messages: NoClearPromise (m(E)), WillCompleteRepair (m(S)), or WillChargeWithoutFixing (m(K)).

After reading the message, the homeowner chooses between BookVisit (I) and Decline (N).
If the homeowner chooses Decline (N), the interaction ends immediately and the result is 2 for the homeowner and 0 for the technician.

If the homeowner chooses BookVisit (I), the technician then chooses between CompleteRepair (S) and ChargeWithoutFixing (K).
After NoClearPromise (m(E)), CompleteRepair (S) gives 4 to the homeowner and 4 to the technician, 
while ChargeWithoutFixing (K) gives 0 to the homeowner and 8 to the technician.

After WillCompleteRepair (m(S)), CompleteRepair (S) gives 4 to the homeowner and 4 to the technician.
If the technician instead chooses ChargeWithoutFixing (K), the technician is fined for dishonest communication.
With f = 2, that result is 0 for the homeowner and 6 for the technician.

After WillChargeWithoutFixing (m(K)), ChargeWithoutFixing (K) gives 0 to the homeowner and 8 to the technician.
If the technician instead chooses CompleteRepair (S), the technician is fined because the later action does not match the message.
With f = 2, that result is 4 for the homeowner and 2 for the technician.
