11) Renewable Energy Subsidy

A regional energy officer receives funding for renewable projects.
They can keep most of the funds for their own department, allocate a smaller portion to independent solar cooperatives, or share the funding equally.
The cooperatives have no authority to contest the decision.






12) University Partnership Award

A senior professor controls an award for an interdisciplinary project.
They may retain most of the credit and resources, grant a smaller share to a junior collaborator, or divide both recognition and funding evenly.
The junior collaborator must accept the outcome.






13) Humanitarian Transport Budget

A logistics coordinator manages a relief transport fund.
They may allocate everything to their own convoy, send a smaller amount to a regional partner, or share the resources evenly between both.
The partner cannot influence the choice.






14) Cultural Exchange Fund

A diplomat manages a cultural exchange fund.
They may reserve all resources for their own delegation, offer a smaller share to the partner country, or share it equally between both sides.
The partner delegation cannot object.






15) Wildlife Conservation Grant

A conservation director receives an international grant.
They can keep the majority for their main reserve, transfer a smaller portion to a rural sanctuary, or split the grant evenly.
The rural sanctuary has no input.






16) Technology Development Bonus

A senior engineer is given a project bonus.
They can keep the entire bonus, give a small share to the junior developer, or share it evenly.
The junior developer cannot influence the result.






17) Research Prize Distribution

A lead scientist controls the division of a prize.
They may keep the majority of the funds, pass a smaller portion to a research assistant, or divide it equally.
The assistant has no power to object.






18) Refugee Shelter Funding

A humanitarian coordinator controls funding for shelters.
They can allocate everything to their own camp, send a smaller portion to a neighboring camp, or split the support equally.
The neighboring camp cannot alter the decision.






19) Political Campaign Donation

A campaign manager receives a political donation.
They may keep all funds for central advertising, pass a smaller share to regional organizers, or divide it evenly between the teams.
Regional organizers have no say.






20) Infrastructure Partnership

A construction firm receives a partnership subsidy.
They may retain it entirely for their headquarters, allocate a smaller portion to the local contractor, or share it evenly.
The contractor cannot dispute the allocation.
