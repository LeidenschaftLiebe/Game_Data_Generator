V5 (pharmaceutical market entry)

A pharmaceutical company first decides whether to launch a competing drug in a market already occupied by a major incumbent.
If the company stays out, the interaction ends immediately and the final reward is 0 for the entrant and 4 for the incumbent firm.

If the company enters, the incumbent firm then decides whether to accommodate the rival or fight through an aggressive price-cutting campaign.
If the incumbent accommodates, the final reward is 2 for the entrant and 2 for the incumbent.
If the incumbent fights with a price war, the final reward is -1 for the entrant and -1 for the incumbent.
