V1 [Car repair]

A driver brings a car to a mechanic.
The mechanic first posts two prices: MinorRepairPrice (p_l) = 4 and MajorRepairPrice (p_h) = 8.

After seeing those prices, the driver chooses between WalkAway (out) and Proceed (in).
If the driver chooses WalkAway (out), both sides get 1.6.

If the driver chooses Proceed (in), the car problem is either MajorProblem (high) or MinorProblem (low), each with probability 0.5.
The mechanic learns which one it is.

If the car has a MajorProblem (high), only MajorRepair (q_h) truly fixes it.
MajorRepair (q_h) with MajorRepairPrice (p_h) gives 2 to the mechanic and 2 to the driver.
MajorRepair (q_h) with MinorRepairPrice (p_l) gives -2 to the mechanic and 6 to the driver.

If the mechanic instead does MinorRepair (q_l) on a MajorProblem (high), the problem stays unsolved.
Charging MajorRepairPrice (p_h) then gives 6 to the mechanic and -8 to the driver.
Charging MinorRepairPrice (p_l) gives 2 to the mechanic and -4 to the driver.

If the car has a MinorProblem (low), both MajorRepair (q_h) and MinorRepair (q_l) solve it.
MajorRepair (q_h) with MajorRepairPrice (p_h) gives 2 and 2.
MajorRepair (q_h) with MinorRepairPrice (p_l) gives -2 and 6.

MinorRepair (q_l) with MajorRepairPrice (p_h) gives 6 to the mechanic and 2 to the driver.
MinorRepair (q_l) with MinorRepairPrice (p_l) gives 2 to the mechanic and 6 to the driver.
