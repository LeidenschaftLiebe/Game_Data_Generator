V1 [Project handoff]

A project lead and a reviewer are trying to get a sensitive report approved.
The lead first chooses between taking the internal route and sending it straight to final review.
If the lead takes the internal route, the lead next chooses between a cautious branch and a shortcut branch.
If the lead takes the cautious branch, the lead then chooses between stopping the process and forwarding the file onward.
If the lead stops the process there, both sides get 0.

If the lead forwards the file onward, the reviewer must decide without knowing whether the file came from the cautious branch, 
the shortcut branch, or the direct route.
If the lead takes the shortcut branch after the internal route, the reviewer faces that same blind choice.
If the lead sends it straight to final review at the start, the reviewer again faces that same blind choice.

At the reviewer’s move, choosing approve gives both sides 1 after the cautious-forward path, 
gives the lead 1 and the reviewer 0 after the shortcut path, and gives both sides 1 after the direct route.
Choosing request changes gives the lead 1 and the reviewer 0 after the cautious-forward path, 
gives both sides 1 after the shortcut path, and gives the lead 1 and the reviewer 0 after the direct route.
Choosing reject gives the lead 1 and the reviewer -1 after the cautious-forward path, 
gives the lead 1 and the reviewer -1 after the shortcut path, and gives the lead 0 and the reviewer -1 after the direct route.
