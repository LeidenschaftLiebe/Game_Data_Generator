V3 (bonus allocation)

A manager and an employee are negotiating over a two-unit bonus.
The manager moves first and chooses whether to give the whole bonus to the employee, split it evenly, or keep all of it.

If the manager offers the whole bonus to the employee, the employee then decides whether to accept or reject.
If the employee accepts, the final amount is 0 for the manager and 2 for the employee.
If the employee rejects, both sides end up with 0.

If the manager offers an even split, the employee again decides whether to accept or reject.
If the employee accepts, the final amount is 1 for the manager and 1 for the employee.
If the employee rejects, both sides end up with 0.

If the manager offers nothing to the employee, the employee again decides whether to accept or reject.
If the employee accepts, the final amount is 2 for the manager and 0 for the employee.
If the employee rejects, both sides end up with 0.
