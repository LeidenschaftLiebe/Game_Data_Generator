V2 — Film Studio under Two Audience Moods

A random audience mood is chosen first.

With probability 1/3, the project starts in MoodV [v].
With probability 2/3, it starts in MoodVPrime [v′].

If the project starts in MoodV [v], studio 1 chooses ArtCut [l] or RiskyCut [r].

If studio 1 chooses ArtCut [l], both sides get 0.

If the project starts in MoodVPrime [v′], studio 1 chooses WideCut [L] or ShelveNow [R].

If studio 1 chooses ShelveNow [R], studio 1 gets 3/2 and studio 2 gets -3/2.

If studio 1 chooses RiskyCut [r] at MoodV [v], or WideCut [L] at MoodVPrime [v′], distributor 2 then chooses PushA [c] or PushB [d].

When distributor 2 moves, distributor 2 cannot tell whether the current situation came after RiskyCut [r] at MoodV [v] or after WideCut [L] at MoodVPrime [v′].

If the current situation came after RiskyCut [r] at MoodV [v] and distributor 2 chooses PushA [c], the payoffs are 3 and -3.
If the current situation came after RiskyCut [r] at MoodV [v] and distributor 2 chooses PushB [d], the payoffs are -3 and 3.

If the current situation came after WideCut [L] at MoodVPrime [v′] and distributor 2 chooses PushA [c], the payoffs are -3 and 3.
If the current situation came after WideCut [L] at MoodVPrime [v′] and distributor 2 chooses PushB [d], the payoffs are 6 and -6.
