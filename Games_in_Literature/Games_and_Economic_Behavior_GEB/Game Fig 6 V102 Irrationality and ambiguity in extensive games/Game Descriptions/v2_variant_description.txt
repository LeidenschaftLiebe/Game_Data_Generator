V2 — Film Rights Deal

A screenwriter and a studio are bargaining over the revenue split for a script.

In stage 1, the screenwriter proposes a split:
the writer asks for 1/4, 1/2, or 3/4 of the rights value.

The studio then chooses whether to Accept (A) or Reject (R).

If the studio accepts in stage 1, the deal ends immediately.

If the writer asks for 1/4 and the studio accepts, the result is 1/4 for the writer
and 3/4 for the studio.

If the writer asks for 1/2 and the studio accepts, the result is 1/2 for each side.

If the writer asks for 3/4 and the studio accepts, the result is 3/4 for the writer
and 1/4 for the studio.

If the studio rejects, the bargaining moves to stage 2.

In stage 2, the studio makes the offer instead.
The studio can offer the writer 1/4, 1/2, or 3/4 of the rights value.

The writer then chooses whether to Accept (A) or Reject (R).

Because this is a delayed deal, accepted stage 2 payoffs are discounted by ρ = 4/5.

So an accepted 1/4 offer gives 1/5 to the writer and 3/5 to the studio.

An accepted 1/2 offer gives 2/5 to each side.

An accepted 3/4 offer gives 3/5 to the writer and 1/5 to the studio.

If the writer rejects in stage 2, both sides get 0.
