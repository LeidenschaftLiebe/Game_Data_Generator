V3 (coffee chains)

Two coffee chains are opening nearby stores and competing for the same morning customers. 
Chain 1 posts its menu prices first, choosing either a high-price menu or a low-price menu. 
Chain 2 sees that choice and then sets its own prices.

If both chains go with high prices, they each earn 1 million. 
If Chain 1 goes high but Chain 2 undercuts with low prices, Chain 1 earns nothing and Chain 2 earns 2 million. 
If Chain 1 goes low and Chain 2 stays high, Chain 1 earns 2 million while Chain 2 earns nothing. 
If both chains go low, neither stands out much and they split the business more evenly, with 0.5 million each.
