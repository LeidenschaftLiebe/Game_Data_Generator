V2 (space launch quotes)

An established launch provider may be operating with either high costs or low costs, with probability 0.5 for each case.
The provider learns its own cost condition before posting a launch quote.
A possible entrant does not know that cost condition.
The entrant only sees which of the two quote options was posted.

If the incumbent is high-cost, it chooses between quote option 1 and quote option 2.
If the high-cost incumbent chooses quote option 1, the entrant then decides whether to enter the market or stay out.
If the entrant enters after seeing quote option 1 from the high-cost incumbent, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and 1 for the entrant.
If the entrant stays out, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and 0 for the entrant.

If the high-cost incumbent chooses quote option 2, the entrant again decides whether to enter or stay out.
If the entrant enters after seeing quote option 2 from the high-cost incumbent, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and 1 for the entrant.
If the entrant stays out, the final reward is 2 for the incumbent and 0 for the entrant.

If the incumbent is low-cost, it also chooses between quote option 1 and quote option 2.
If the low-cost incumbent chooses quote option 1, the entrant then decides whether to enter or stay out.
If the entrant enters after seeing quote option 1 from the low-cost incumbent, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and -1 for the entrant.
If the entrant stays out, the final reward is 2 for the incumbent and 0 for the entrant.

If the low-cost incumbent chooses quote option 2, the entrant again decides whether to enter or stay out.
If the entrant enters after seeing quote option 2 from the low-cost incumbent, the final reward is 0 for the incumbent and -1 for the entrant.
If the entrant stays out, the final reward is 4 for the incumbent and 0 for the entrant.
