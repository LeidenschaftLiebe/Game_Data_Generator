V1 (wildlife rescue transport)

At the outset, a rescue mission may be in a high-access condition with probability 2/3 or a low-access condition with probability 1/3.
The field leader learns which condition applies.
The transport coordinator does not observe whether access is high or low.

If the mission is in the high-access condition, the field leader chooses between the local route and the transfer route.
If the leader chooses the local route in the high-access condition, the final reward is 3 for the leader and 0 for the coordinator.
If the leader chooses the transfer route in the high-access condition, the coordinator then chooses between option L and option M.
If the coordinator chooses option L there, the final reward is 0 for the leader and 6 for the coordinator.
If the coordinator chooses option M there, the final reward is 6 for the leader and 0 for the coordinator.

If the mission is in the low-access condition, the field leader chooses between the local route and the transfer route.
If the leader chooses the local route in the low-access condition, the final reward is 0 for the leader and 0 for the coordinator.
If the leader chooses the transfer route in the low-access condition, the coordinator then chooses between option L and option M.
The coordinator cannot tell whether this choice follows the transfer route in the high-access condition or in the low-access condition.
If the coordinator chooses option L there, the final reward is 3 for the leader and 0 for the coordinator.
If the coordinator chooses option M there, the final reward is 3 for the leader and 6 for the coordinator.
