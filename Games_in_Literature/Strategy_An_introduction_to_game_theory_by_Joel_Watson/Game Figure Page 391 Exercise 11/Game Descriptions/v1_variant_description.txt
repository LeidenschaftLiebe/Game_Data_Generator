V1 (wildlife expedition planning)

At the outset, outside conditions send the expedition onto either the lowland route or the highland route, with probability 0.5 for each.
The expedition leader does not control that initial split.

If the lowland route is selected, the leader chooses between plan A and plan B.
If the leader chooses plan A, the final reward is 2 for the leader and 10 for the logistics chief.
If the leader chooses plan B, the logistics chief then chooses between support option F and support option G.
If the logistics chief chooses support option F after plan B, the final reward is 0 for the leader and 10 for the logistics chief.
If the logistics chief chooses support option G after plan B, the final reward is -10 for the leader and 2 for the logistics chief.

If the highland route is selected, the leader chooses among plan C, plan D, and plan E.
If the leader chooses plan E, the final reward is 2 for the leader and 10 for the logistics chief.
If the leader chooses plan C, the logistics chief then chooses between support option F and support option G.
If the logistics chief chooses support option F after plan C, the final reward is 6 for the leader and 0 for the logistics chief.
If the logistics chief chooses support option G after plan C, the final reward is -10 for the leader and 7 for the logistics chief.

If the leader chooses plan D, the logistics chief again chooses between support option F and support option G.
The logistics chief cannot tell whether the current choice comes after plan B, plan C, or plan D.
If the logistics chief chooses support option F after plan D, the final reward is 3 for the leader and 5 for the logistics chief.
If the logistics chief chooses support option G after plan D, the final reward is 3 for the leader and 5 for the logistics chief.
