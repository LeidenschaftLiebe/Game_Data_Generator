1. The Elements Duel

Two shamans simultaneously call upon fire, water, or wood.

Fire overcomes wood, but is quenched by water.
Water douses fire, but is absorbed by wood.
Wood absorbs water, but burns to fire.
If both call the same element, the contest ends in a draw.
The gain of one side equals the setback of the other.






2. The Animal Clash

Two animals face off, each choosing lion, wolf, or boar.

Lion dominates wolf, but falls to boar.
Wolf overpowers boar, but is beaten by lion.
Boar defeats lion, but loses to wolf.
If both choose the same beast, the clash ends in a tie.
Every triumph for one is mirrored by the defeat of the other.






3. The Martial Arts Forms

Two fighters choose between kick, punch, or block.

Kick breaks through punch, but is stopped by block.
Punch breaks block, but is countered by kick.
Block halts kick, but is undone by punch.
If both choose the same form, the exchange is even.
Each success for one side comes with an equal failure for the other.






4. The Wizard Spells

Two wizards cast flame, frost, or lightning.

Flame melts frost, but is grounded by lightning.
Frost slows lightning, but is melted by flame.
Lightning shatters flame, but is slowed by frost.
If both cast the same spell, the duel ends in a draw.
One's magical gain is measured by the other's magical loss.






5. The Jungle Hunt

Two hunters set traps: net, spear, or pit.

Net catches spear, but fails against pit.
Spear strikes pit, but is tangled by net.
Pit swallows net, but is struck by spear.
If both set the same trap, the result is a tie.
The hunter's fortune is the other's misfortune.






6. The Galactic Skirmish

Two starship captains deploy fighters, bombers, or shields.

Fighters intercept bombers, but are blocked by shields.
Bombers crush shields, but are destroyed by fighters.
Shields stop fighters, but crumble to bombers.
If both deploy the same, the standoff is neutral.
Every gain in battle for one means a setback for the other.






7. The Kingdom's Trial

Two knights declare sword, lance, or shield.

Sword cuts lance, but is blocked by shield.
Lance pierces shield, but is cut by sword.
Shield halts sword, but is pierced by lance.
If both declare the same weapon, the trial ends in a draw.
The victor's glory reflects the other's downfall.






8. The Seasons' Cycle

Two druids call upon spring, summer, or winter.

Spring overtakes winter, but yields to summer.
Summer scorches spring, but falls to winter.
Winter freezes summer, but is melted by spring.
If both invoke the same season, the cycle is even.
Each rise on one side is matched by a fall on the other.






9. The Samurai's Choice

Two samurai face off, choosing strike, parry, or feint.

Strike cuts through feint, but is stopped by parry.
Parry blocks strike, but is undone by feint.
Feint bypasses parry, but is cut by strike.
If both choose the same move, the duel is a tie.
One warrior's advantage is the other's disadvantage.






10. The Cyber Hack

Two hackers launch virus, firewall, or exploit.

Virus breaks exploit, but is stopped by firewall.
Firewall blocks virus, but is bypassed by exploit.
Exploit breaks firewall, but is corrupted by virus.
If both use the same tool, the encounter ends in a draw.
Each digital breakthrough for one side comes at the other's expense.
