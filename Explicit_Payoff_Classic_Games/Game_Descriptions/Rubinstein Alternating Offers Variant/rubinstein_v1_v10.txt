1) Film Production Profit Division

A film producer and a lead actor are dividing a $2 million box-office bonus.
The producer makes the first move, proposing either a 70/30 split or a 60/40 split, both favoring the producer.
If the actor accepts, the deal is finalized instantly.
If the actor rejects, negotiations drag on and the prize shrinks to $1.5 million.
The actor then counters with a 40/60 offer favoring themselves.
Finally, the producer can either accept that counter or reject it, in which case both end up splitting the reduced total evenly.






2) Real-Estate Developers’ Deal

Two real-estate developers must divide $3 million in profits from a joint project.
Developer A proposes first, offering either a 2 million / 1 million or a 1.8 million / 1.2 million split favoring themselves.
If Developer B refuses, delays reduce the total available profit to $2.4 million.
B then makes a counterproposal offering 1.0 million / 1.4 million in their own favor.
A may accept that or reject and settle for an equal split of the reduced amount.






3) Music Distribution Royalties

A record label and an independent artist are sharing $800 K in streaming royalties.
The label first offers either 500 K / 300 K or 550 K / 250 K.
If the artist rejects, the total falls to $600 K due to delays.
The artist can then counter with a 260 K / 340 K division favoring themselves.
The label may accept that or reject and end with a fair 300 K / 300 K split.






4) Tech Patent Licensing

A tech company and a university negotiate how to divide $1 million in licensing fees.
The company offers either 700 K / 300 K or 650 K / 350 K.
If the university rejects, the patent window closes slightly and the total falls to $800 K.
The university counters with 350 K / 450 K, which the company may accept or decline.
If it declines, the reduced pot is shared evenly.






5) Startup Acquisition

Two founders are dividing $900 K from an acquisition.
The CEO founder starts, proposing 600 K / 300 K or 550 K / 350 K.
If the co-founder refuses, the deal’s value drops to $720 K, and the co-founder counters with 320 K / 400 K.
The CEO can accept or reject, defaulting to 360 K each if no agreement.






6) Sports Sponsorship Deal

A sports team and a brand sponsor divide $1.5 million in promotion revenue.
The sponsor offers 900 K / 600 K or 1 million / 500 K.
If the team rejects, the sponsorship value drops to $1.2 million.
The team proposes 500 K / 700 K, which the sponsor can accept or reject for an even $600 K each.






7) Publishing Contract

A publisher and an author share $400 K royalties.
The publisher proposes 250 K / 150 K or 280 K / 120 K.
If rejected, the total value falls to $320 K.
The author proposes 140 K / 180 K, which the publisher can either accept or decline and default to 160 K / 160 K.






8) Construction Joint Venture

Two construction firms divide $2 million in project savings.
Firm A offers either 1.3 M / 0.7 M or 1.2 M / 0.8 M.
If Firm B rejects, delay reduces the savings to $1.6 M.
Firm B then counters with 0.7 M / 0.9 M, and Firm A decides to accept or revert to a 0.8 M / 0.8 M split.






9) Advertising Partnership

A marketing firm and a media outlet share $600 K ad profits.
The marketing firm first offers 400 K / 200 K or 350 K / 250 K.
If the media outlet declines, the pot shrinks to $480 K, and it counters with 220 K / 260 K.
If the firm refuses, both end up with 240 K each.






10) Energy Partnership

A power utility and a renewables startup are dividing $5 million in carbon credits.
The utility offers either 3 M / 2 M or 3.5 M / 1.5 M.
If the startup refuses, delays cut the value to $4 million.
The startup proposes 1.8 M / 2.2 M, which the utility can accept or reject, leading to a final $2 M / $2 M split.
