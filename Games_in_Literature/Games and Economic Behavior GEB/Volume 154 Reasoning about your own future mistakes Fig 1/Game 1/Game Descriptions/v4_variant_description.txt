A cook is deciding how to handle dinner service.
At the beginning, the cook chooses between serve the prepared dish now (b) and try an extra finishing step (a).
Serving now gives outcome 1.
Trying the extra step leads to a later choice between finish it properly (c) and overcook it (d).
Finishing it properly gives outcome 1.
Overcooking it gives outcome 0.
The cook may trust their skill, but still recognizes the risk of a future slip under pressure.
