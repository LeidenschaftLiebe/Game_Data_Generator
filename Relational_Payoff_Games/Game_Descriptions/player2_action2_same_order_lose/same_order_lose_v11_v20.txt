Game descriptions for when player 1 = action 1, then player 2 loses with either action; 
when player 1 chooses action 2, player 2 wins either way, but action 1 results in a bigger win.
V11 - V20: 

11. Author vs. Critic

An author (Player 1) publishes a Short story or a Novel. The critic (Player 2) writes a review.

If the Short story is released, the critic always loses, whether reviewing Plot (loss for Critic) or Style (loss for Critic).

If the Novel is released, the critic always wins, though focusing on Character depth (better win for Critic) is stronger than focusing on Pacing (weaker win for Critic).

Zero-sum: the critic’s influence undercuts the author’s reception.






12. Coach vs. Player

A coach (Player 1) emphasizes Defense drills or Offense drills. The player (Player 2) adapts.

If Defense drills are chosen, the player always loses, whether responding with Positioning (loss for Player) or Tackling (loss for Player).

If Offense drills are chosen, the player always wins, though practicing Shooting (better win for Player) is stronger than Passing (weaker win for Player).

Zero-sum: training advantage flips directly between them.






13. Lawyer vs. Judge

A lawyer (Player 1) bases the argument on Constitutional law or Case precedent. The judge (Player 2) reacts.

If on Constitutional law, the judge always loses, whether responding with Dismissal (loss for Judge) or Objection (loss for Judge).

If on Case precedent, the judge always wins, though citing Binding precedent (better win for Judge) is stronger than citing Similar case (weaker win for Judge).

Zero-sum: rulings mirror who holds the stronger ground.






14. Entrepreneur vs. Investor

An entrepreneur (Player 1) pitches a Prototype or a Business plan. The investor (Player 2) decides how to respond.

If pitching a Prototype, the investor always loses, whether challenging Feasibility (loss for Investor) or Scalability (loss for Investor).

If pitching a Business plan, the investor always wins, though pushing Valuation (better win for Investor) is stronger than pushing Equity share (weaker win for Investor).

Zero-sum: every deal favors one at the other’s cost.






15. Journalist vs. Interviewee

A journalist (Player 1) asks about Personal life or Policy stance. The interviewee (Player 2) answers.

If asked about Personal life, the interviewee always loses, whether answering with Details (loss for Interviewee) or Privacy defense (loss for Interviewee).

If asked about Policy stance, the interviewee always wins, though stressing Achievements (better win for Interviewee) is stronger than stressing Promises (weaker win for Interviewee).

Zero-sum: narrative control shifts completely.






16. Teacher vs. Examiner

A teacher (Player 1) trains pupils for Writing or Mathematics. The examiner (Player 2) designs the test.

If trained for Writing, the examiner always loses, whether testing Essays (loss for Examiner) or Grammar (loss for Examiner).

If trained for Mathematics, the examiner always wins, though testing Algebra (better win for Examiner) is stronger than testing Geometry (weaker win for Examiner).

Zero-sum: preparation advantage shifts one way or the other.






17. Politician vs. Rival

A politician (Player 1) frames debate on Jobs or Immigration. The rival (Player 2) counters.

If on Jobs, the rival always loses, whether arguing Unemployment (loss for Rival) or Wages (loss for Rival).

If on Immigration, the rival always wins, though Border policy (better win for Rival) is stronger than Integration (weaker win for Rival).

Zero-sum: voter support shifts in lockstep.






18. Debater vs. Challenger

A debater (Player 1) opens with Freedom or Security. The challenger (Player 2) replies.

If opening with Freedom, the challenger always loses, whether replying with Rights (loss for Challenger) or Responsibility (loss for Challenger).

If opening with Security, the challenger always wins, though arguing Safety (better win for Challenger) is stronger than arguing Enforcement (weaker win for Challenger).

Zero-sum: rhetorical control belongs fully to one side.






19. Doctor vs. Patient

A doctor (Player 1) prescribes Medication or Lifestyle change. The patient (Player 2) responds.

If Medication is prescribed, the patient always loses, whether responding with Compliance (loss for Patient) or Refusal (loss for Patient).

If Lifestyle change is prescribed, the patient always wins, though Exercise (better win for Patient) is stronger than Diet (weaker win for Patient).

Zero-sum: health progress favors one at the other’s cost.






20. Director vs. Actor

A director (Player 1) emphasizes Blocking or Emotion in rehearsal. The actor (Player 2) adapts.

If focusing on Blocking, the actor always loses, whether adapting with Movement (loss for Actor) or Timing (loss for Actor).

If focusing on Emotion, the actor always wins, though Expressiveness (better win for Actor) is stronger than Subtlety (weaker win for Actor).

Zero-sum: control of the scene flows to one at the other’s expense.
