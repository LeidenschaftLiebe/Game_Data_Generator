V0 — Imperfect Recall Example 6

Chance first chooses L or R, each with probability 1/2.

If chance chooses L, the decision maker first faces one kind of decision situation and chooses S or B.
If the decision maker chooses S, the reward is 3.
If the decision maker chooses B, the decision maker later faces the other kind of decision situation.

If chance chooses R, the decision maker first faces the other kind of decision situation and chooses S or B.
If the decision maker chooses S, the reward is 3.
If the decision maker chooses B, the decision maker later faces the first kind of decision situation.

When making a move at d1 or d2, the decision maker cannot tell whether this is the earlier or the later decision point, 
and also cannot tell whether the path began with L or R.

If the decision maker is at d1 and chooses S, the reward is 0.
If the decision maker is at d1 and chooses B, the reward is 2.

If the decision maker is at d2 and chooses S, the reward is 0.
If the decision maker is at d2 and chooses B, the reward is 2.
