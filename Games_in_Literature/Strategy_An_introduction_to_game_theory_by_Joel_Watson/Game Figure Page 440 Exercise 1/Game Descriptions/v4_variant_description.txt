V4 (wildlife reserve trail game)

At a wildlife reserve, one ranger hides at Trail A or Trail B before the visitor arrives.
The guide sees which trail the ranger chose.
Then the visitor enters, and the guide says either “Red trail” or “Green trail.”
After hearing that, the visitor picks one of the two trails.
If the visitor picks the correct trail, the visitor gets 100 and the ranger gets 0.
If the visitor picks the wrong trail, the ranger gets 100 and the visitor gets 0.
The guide gets 10 whenever Trail A is chosen and 0 whenever Trail B is chosen.

If the ranger hides at Trail A and the guide says Red trail, the visitor’s choice determines the result.
Choosing Trail A gives final rewards of 0 for the ranger, 10 for the guide, and 100 for the visitor.
Choosing Trail B gives final rewards of 100 for the ranger, 0 for the guide, and 0 for the visitor.

If the ranger hides at Trail A and the guide says Green trail, the visitor again chooses between Trail A and Trail B.
Choosing Trail A gives final rewards of 0 for the ranger, 10 for the guide, and 100 for the visitor.
Choosing Trail B gives final rewards of 100 for the ranger, 0 for the guide, and 0 for the visitor.

If the ranger hides at Trail B and the guide says Red trail, the visitor chooses between the two trails.
Choosing Trail A gives final rewards of 100 for the ranger, 10 for the guide, and 0 for the visitor.
Choosing Trail B gives final rewards of 0 for the ranger, 0 for the guide, and 100 for the visitor.

If the ranger hides at Trail B and the guide says Green trail, the visitor again chooses between the two trails.
Choosing Trail A gives final rewards of 100 for the ranger, 10 for the guide, and 0 for the visitor.
Choosing Trail B gives final rewards of 0 for the ranger, 0 for the guide, and 100 for the visitor.
