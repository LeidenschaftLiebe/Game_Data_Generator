V2 — Two Campaigns under Two News Cycles

A random news cycle first turns out to be CalmCycle [u] or TenseCycle [v], each with probability 1/2.

Campaign I sees which news cycle arrived and then chooses ResponseR [R], ResponseS [S], or ResponseP [P].

Campaign II then chooses ResponseR [R], ResponseS [S], or ResponseP [P].
Campaign II knows whether the cycle is CalmCycle [u] or TenseCycle [v], but does not know which response Campaign I selected.

Under either news cycle, choosing the same response gives both campaigns 0.

If Campaign I chooses ResponseR [R] and Campaign II chooses ResponseS [S], Campaign I gets 6 and Campaign II gets -4.
If Campaign I chooses ResponseR [R] and Campaign II chooses ResponseP [P], Campaign I gets -4 and Campaign II gets 2.

If Campaign I chooses ResponseS [S] and Campaign II chooses ResponseR [R], Campaign I gets -4 and Campaign II gets 6.
If Campaign I chooses ResponseS [S] and Campaign II chooses ResponseP [P], Campaign I gets 4 and Campaign II gets -2.

If Campaign I chooses ResponseP [P] and Campaign II chooses ResponseR [R], Campaign I gets 2 and Campaign II gets -4.
If Campaign I chooses ResponseP [P] and Campaign II chooses ResponseS [S], Campaign I gets -2 and Campaign II gets 4.
