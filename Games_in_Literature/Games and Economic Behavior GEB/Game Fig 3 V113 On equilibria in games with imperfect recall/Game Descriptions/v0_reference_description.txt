The article extends the absentminded-driver setting by adding an earlier choice for the driver and an earlier choice for the policeman.
At the start, the Driver chooses between trying to escape Try [T] and surrendering Surrender [S].
If the driver surrenders, the interaction ends immediately with 0 to the Driver and 0 to the Policeman.

If the driver tries to escape, the Policeman chooses between waiting Wait [W] and intercepting immediately Intercept [I].
If the policeman intercepts immediately, there is a crash, giving -1 to the Driver and -1 to the Policeman.
If the policeman waits, the chase continues onto the highway.

Once the chase reaches the highway, the driver faces the same choice each time she comes to an exit: continue Continue [C] or take the exit Exit [E].
She cannot tell whether she is at the first exit or the second one.

If the driver takes the first exit, the policeman later chooses between waiting on the highway Highway [H] and waiting at the first exit FirstExit [E].
Waiting on the highway gives 1 to the Driver and -1 to the Policeman.
Waiting at the first exit gives 0 to the Driver and 0 to the Policeman.

If the driver continues past the first exit, she reaches the same kind of choice again and once more chooses between Continue [C] and Exit [E].
If she takes that later exit, the result is 0, 0.
If she continues again instead, the policeman later chooses between Highway [H] and FirstExit [E].
Waiting on the highway gives 0 to the Driver and 0 to the Policeman.
Waiting at the first exit gives 1 to the Driver and -1 to the Policeman.
