V3 [Repair contract]

A homeowner learns whether a repair job is EasyTask or DifficultTask, each with probability 0.50.
After learning that, the homeowner chooses between NoDisclosure and ImmediateDisclosure.

If the homeowner chooses ImmediateDisclosure, the technician learns the job difficulty before deciding whether to enter.
If the homeowner chooses NoDisclosure, the technician does not learn the job difficulty before deciding.

The technician then chooses between TakeContract (IN) and DeclineContract (OUT).
If the technician chooses DeclineContract (OUT), the technician gets 30 and the homeowner gets 25.

If the technician chooses TakeContract (IN), the technician then chooses between HighEffort (HIGH) and LowEffort (LOW).
On the NoDisclosure side, the technician still does not know whether the repair job is easy or difficult when choosing effort.
On the ImmediateDisclosure side, the technician does know.

If the repair job is easy and the technician chooses HighEffort (HIGH), the result is 20 for the technician and 50 for the homeowner.
If the repair job is easy and the technician chooses LowEffort (LOW), the result is 50 for the technician and 35 for the homeowner.

If the repair job is difficult and the technician chooses HighEffort (HIGH), the result is 20 for the technician and 50 for the homeowner.
If the technician chooses LowEffort (LOW), the result is 0 for the technician and 35 for the homeowner.
