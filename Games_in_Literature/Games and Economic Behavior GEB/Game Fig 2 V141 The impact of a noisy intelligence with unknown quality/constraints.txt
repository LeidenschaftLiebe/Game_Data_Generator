Strategic Definition:
Better-Response Equivalence

Constraints

Player 1 moves first, then the intelligence system sends a signal, then Player 2 chooses whether to attack.
The signal probabilities are fixed: after NB, signal b occurs with probability 1/5 and nb with probability 4/5; 
after B, signal b occurs with probability 4/5 and nb with probability 1/5.
Player 2 has one infoset after signal b and one infoset after signal nb. 
At each of those later choices, Player 2 observes the signal but not whether Player 1 previously chose NB or B.
Seed payoff constraint: after NB, then b, then A, the payoffs are 1/5, 3/10.
