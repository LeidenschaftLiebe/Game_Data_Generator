At the start, a hidden draw determines the two foresight types in the game.
Player 1 may be 01, 11, or 21, and Player 2 may be 02 or 12.

For this dataset instance, use:
rho(01) = rho(11) = rho(21) = 1/3,
rho(02) = 1/10, and rho(12) = 9/10.

After that hidden draw, the action structure is the same in every branch.
Player 1 moves first and chooses between Invest1 and Out1.

If Player 1 chooses Out1, the game ends immediately with 5 for Player 1 and 0 for Player 2.

If Player 1 chooses Invest1, then Player 2 moves and chooses between Invest2 and Out2.

If Player 2 chooses Out2, the game ends with 0, 6.

If Player 2 chooses Invest2, then Player 1 moves again and chooses between Invest3 and Out3.

At this last layer, the same two outcomes appear everywhere.
Invest3 leads to 8, 8; while Out3 leads to 7, 0.

The type labels matter for what each player can foresee and for which opponent type they think they are facing,
but the payoff attached to a given action history is the same across all type combinations.
