V3 [Wildfire response]

A fire chief and a ranger are deciding how to respond to a fast-moving wildfire.

Conditions first choose one of two sides, with probability 1/2 each.

On the left side, the ranger moves first and chooses between PullBack (OUT) and MoveIn (IN).

If the ranger chooses PullBack (OUT), this gives the chief 1, and the ranger -1.

If the ranger chooses MoveIn (IN), then the chief chooses between SouthLine (S) and DirectLine (D).
SouthLine (S) gives the chief -2, and the ranger 2.
DirectLine (D) gives the chief 2, and the ranger -2.

On the right side, the chief moves first and chooses between StandDown (Out) and Advance (In).

If the chief chooses StandDown (Out), this gives the chief -1, and the ranger 1.

If the chief chooses Advance (In), the chief later chooses between SouthLine (S) and DirectLine (D).
SouthLine (S) gives the chief 2, and the ranger -2.
DirectLine (D) gives the chief -2, and the ranger 2.

At that later upper choice, the chief cannot tell whether it followed the ranger’s earlier MoveIn (IN) on the left side 
or the chief’s own earlier Advance (In) on the right side.
