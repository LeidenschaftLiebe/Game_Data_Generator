11. Restaurant Tip Split — $40

A waiter and a busser are splitting a $40 tip left by a large table.
The waiter decides whether to share it evenly or keep most with an 80/20 split.
The busser may accept or reject. If they reject, the manager takes back the tip to the pool.






12. Movie Revenue Share — $100

A film director and a screenwriter are dividing $100 in royalties.
The director can propose a balanced 60/40 split or a controlling 85/15 division.
The writer may accept or reject. Rejection voids the payment from the studio.






13. Farmer and Trader — $60

A farmer sells produce to a trader for $60. The trader offers to share the profit.
They can split it evenly or keep more with a 70/30 offer.
The farmer can accept or reject. Rejection means the deal is cancelled.






14. Lottery Winnings — $200

Two coworkers jointly bought a lottery ticket and won $200.
The one who bought the ticket controls the winnings and can offer a fair 50/50 split or a greedy 90/10 split.
The coworker may accept or reject. A rejection sends the unclaimed prize back to the lottery office.






15. Research Grant Distribution — $80

A principal investigator and an assistant researcher must divide an $80 grant bonus.
The PI can offer a modest 55/45 or claim more with an 85/15 division.
The assistant can accept or reject. Rejection means the grant expires.






16. Art Gallery Sale — $50

An art gallery sells a painting for $50.
The gallery owner can offer the artist either a fair 60/40 division or a low 80/20 share.
The artist decides whether to accept or reject. Rejection voids the sale and both lose the payment.






17. Sports Sponsorship — $120

A sports team wins $120 from a sponsorship deal.
The team captain proposes how to divide it with the vice-captain: either 60/40 or 85/15.
The vice-captain may accept or reject. A rejection cancels the deal.






18. Design Agency Project — $70

A client pays $70 for a joint design project.
The lead designer can propose an equal 50/50 split or a 75/25 share in their favour.
The junior designer can accept or reject. A rejection means the client cancels the payment.






19. Academic Coauthor Royalties — $90

Two coauthors receive $90 in royalties from a book sale.
The lead author can offer 60/40 or claim more with a 70/30 division.
The coauthor can accept or reject. If rejected, the publisher keeps the funds.






20. Fishing Cooperative — $100

Two fishers caught $100 worth of fish.
The one who owns the boat can offer either a fair 50/50 split or keep most with an 80/20 share.
The crew member can accept or reject. If rejected, the fish are left to spoil and neither earns anything.
