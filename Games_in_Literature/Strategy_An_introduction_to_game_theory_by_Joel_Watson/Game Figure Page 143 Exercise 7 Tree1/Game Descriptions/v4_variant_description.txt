V4 (pharmaceutical launch)

A pharmaceutical company first chooses between launch strategy A, launch strategy B, and launch strategy C.
If the company chooses launch strategy C, the process ends immediately and each side gets a final return of 5.

If the company chooses launch strategy A, a partner firm then chooses between reaction X and reaction Y.
If the partner chooses reaction X after launch strategy A, each side gets a final return of 8.
If the partner chooses reaction Y after launch strategy A, each side gets 0.

If the company chooses launch strategy B, the partner again chooses between reaction X and reaction Y.
The partner cannot tell whether the earlier decision was launch strategy A or launch strategy B.
If the partner chooses reaction X after launch strategy B, each side gets a final return of 2.
If the partner chooses reaction Y after launch strategy B, each side gets a final return of 6.
