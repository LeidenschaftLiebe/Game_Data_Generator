V4 — Robot Testing with Alternating Modes

Chance first starts the robot in ModeLeft [L] or ModeRight [R], each with probability 1/2.

If the robot starts in ModeLeft [L], it first faces Mode2 [d2] and chooses ShutDown [S] or BootFurther [B].
Choosing ShutDown [S] gives reward 3.
Choosing BootFurther [B] leads later to Mode1 [d1].

If the robot starts in ModeRight [R], it first faces Mode1 [d1] and chooses ShutDown [S] or BootFurther [B].
Choosing ShutDown [S] gives reward 3.
Choosing BootFurther [B] leads later to Mode2 [d2].

When making a move at Mode1 [d1] or Mode2 [d2], the robot cannot tell whether this is the earlier or the later decision point, 
and also cannot tell whether the path began with ModeLeft [L] or ModeRight [R].

If the robot is at Mode1 [d1] and chooses ShutDown [S], the reward is 0.
If the robot is at Mode1 [d1] and chooses BootFurther [B], the reward is 2.

If the robot is at Mode2 [d2] and chooses ShutDown [S], the reward is 0.
If the robot is at Mode2 [d2] and chooses BootFurther [B], the reward is 2.
