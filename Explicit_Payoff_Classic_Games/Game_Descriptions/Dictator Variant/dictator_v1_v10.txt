1) Startup Equity Distribution — $1 Million

A startup founder controls a $1 million equity bonus.
They can keep the entire $1,000,000, share $300,000 with their cofounder, or split it evenly at $500,000 each.
The cofounder cannot contest the decision.






2) City Budget Allocation — $200 Thousand

A city council member oversees a $200,000 discretionary fund.
They can keep all of it for administrative expenses, allocate $80,000 to community projects, or divide it equally at $100,000 each.
The community group cannot alter the outcome.






3) Research Grant Surplus — $60 Thousand

A principal investigator has a $60,000 surplus from a grant.
They can keep all $60,000, give $20,000 to their coauthor, or divide it evenly at $30,000 each.
The coauthor must accept whatever is chosen.






4) Music Royalties — $5 Thousand

A lead singer receives $5,000 in royalties from a new album.
They may keep all $5,000, share $1,500 with the band, or split evenly at $2,500 each.
The band has no power to object.






5) University Endowment Bonus — $300 Thousand

A university dean receives a $300,000 performance bonus.
They can keep all of it, award $100,000 to the faculty fund, or share evenly at $150,000 each.
Faculty representatives have no input.






6) Film Profit Sharing — $2 Million

A producer receives $2 million in film profits.
They may keep everything, share $600,000 with the director, or split it evenly at $1 million each.
The director has no contractual right to dispute the choice.






7) NGO Donation Decision — $80 Thousand

An NGO director receives an $80,000 anonymous donation.
They can use all of it for administrative costs, forward $30,000 to field operations, or split evenly at $40,000 each.
Field staff cannot appeal the decision.






8) Startup Investment Return — $400 Thousand

Two investors backed a tech startup, but only one controls the return.
The lead investor can keep all $400,000, pay $100,000 to the partner, or divide it evenly at $200,000 each.
The partner cannot challenge the transfer.






9) Real Estate Sale — $1.2 Million

A real estate broker sells a shared property for $1.2 million.
They can keep all proceeds, transfer $400,000 to the partner, or split evenly at $600,000 each.
The partner has no legal recourse in this simplified scenario.






10) Startup Exit Payout — $5 Million

After an acquisition, a CEO must divide a $5 million payout with an early engineer.
The CEO may keep the full $5,000,000, pay $1,000,000 to the engineer, or share evenly at $2,500,000 each.
The engineer has no negotiating rights.
