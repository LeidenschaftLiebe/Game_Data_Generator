V3 — Drug Approval

A biotech firm may find it easy or costly to disclose everything during a regulatory review.

At the start, the firm is one of two types.
With probability 0.6 disclosure is low-cost (L).
With probability 0.4 disclosure is high-cost (H).

After learning the type, the firm chooses among Full Disclosure (NBO),
No Hidden Risk (NB), and Conceal Side Effects (B).

If the low-cost firm chooses Full Disclosure (NBO), the outcome is reward 0.5 to the firm
and 1.0 to the regulator.

If the high-cost firm chooses Full Disclosure (NBO), the outcome is reward 0.1 to the firm
and 1.0 to the regulator.

If the firm instead chooses No Hidden Risk (NB) or Conceal Side Effects (B), the regulator moves.

The regulator only sees that the firm did not fully disclose.
The regulator cannot tell whether the firm is low-cost or high-cost, and cannot tell whether the firm chose
No Hidden Risk (NB) or Conceal Side Effects (B).

The regulator chooses between No Intervention (NA) and Block Approval (A).

If the path is No Hidden Risk (NB), No Intervention (NA), the outcome is reward 0.6 to the firm
and 1.0 to the regulator.

If the path is No Hidden Risk (NB), Block Approval (A), the outcome is reward 0.2 to the firm
and 0.3 to the regulator.

If the path is Conceal Side Effects (B), No Intervention (NA), the outcome is reward 1.0 to the firm
and 0.0 to the regulator.

If the path is Conceal Side Effects (B), Block Approval (A), the outcome is reward 0.0 to the firm
and 0.8 to the regulator.
