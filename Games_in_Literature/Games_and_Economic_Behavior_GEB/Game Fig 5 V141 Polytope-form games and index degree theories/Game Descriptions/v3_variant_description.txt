V3 [Political speech]

A politician’s campaign strength is either Strong (s) with probability 0.9 or Weak (w) with probability 0.1.

After learning that privately, the politician chooses between BoldSpeech (B) and QuietSpeech (Q).
A journalist observes whether the speech was bold or quiet, but does not know the campaign strength.

If the politician gives BoldSpeech (B) after Strong (s), the journalist chooses between NoChallenge (NF) and FactCheck (F).
NoChallenge (NF) gives 3 to the politician and 1 to the journalist.
FactCheck (F) gives 1 to the politician and 0 to the journalist.

If the politician gives BoldSpeech (B) after Weak (w), NoChallenge (NF) gives 2 and 0,
while FactCheck (F) gives 0 and 1.

If the politician gives QuietSpeech (Q) after Strong (s), NoChallenge (NF) gives 2 and 1,
while FactCheck (F) gives 0 and 0.

If the politician gives QuietSpeech (Q) after Weak (w), NoChallenge (NF) gives 3 and 0,
while FactCheck (F) gives 1 and 1.
