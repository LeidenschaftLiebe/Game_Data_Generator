V3 [Emergency response]

A field commander and a control-room analyst are coordinating a response.
The commander first chooses between the layered response and the direct dispatch.
If the commander chooses the layered response, the commander next chooses between a containment branch and a bypass branch.
If the commander chooses the containment branch, the commander then chooses between standing down and escalating to central review.
If the commander stands down there, both sides get 0.

If the commander escalates to central review, the analyst must act without knowing whether the case came from containment, bypass, or direct dispatch.
If the commander chooses the bypass branch, the analyst faces that same blind choice.
If the commander chooses direct dispatch at the start, the analyst again faces that same blind choice.

At the analyst’s move, choosing green light gives both sides 1 after the containment-escalation path, 
gives the commander 1 and the analyst 0 after the bypass path, and gives both sides 1 after direct dispatch.
Choosing caution notice gives the commander 1 and the analyst 0 after the containment-escalation path, 
gives both sides 1 after the bypass path, and gives the commander 1 and the analyst 0 after direct dispatch.
Choosing full lockdown gives the commander 1 and the analyst -1 after the containment-escalation path, 
gives the commander 1 and the analyst -1 after the bypass path, and gives the commander 0 and the analyst -1 after direct dispatch.
