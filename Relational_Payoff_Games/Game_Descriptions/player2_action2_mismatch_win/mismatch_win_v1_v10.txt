Game descriptions for mismatching actions (mismatch is a win for player 2 and match is a win for player 1). 

V1 - V10: 

1. Guard vs. Intruder

A guard protects a facility while an intruder attempts a breach.

The guard can watch the left corridor or the right corridor.
The intruder can try to sneak in through the left corridor or the right corridor.

If both choose the same corridor, the guard intercepts and wins.

If they choose different corridors, the intruder slips past and wins.






2. Goalkeeper vs. Penalty Taker

A striker steps up for a penalty, and the goalkeeper prepares to dive.

The goalkeeper can dive left or right.
The striker can kick left or right.

If they choose the same side, the goalkeeper makes the save.

If they choose different sides, the striker scores.






3. Hunter vs. Prey

A hunter stalks an animal in the forest.

The hunter can wait at the waterhole or at the berry patch.
The prey can approach the waterhole or the berry patch.

If both go to the same place, the hunter catches the prey.

If they go to different places, the prey escapes.






4. Police Officer vs. Thief

A thief plans a getaway while police prepare to intercept.

The police can block the north road or the south road.
The thief can flee by the north road or the south road.

If they pick the same road, the thief is caught.

If they pick different roads, the thief gets away.






5. Cat vs. Mouse

A cat prowls the house, while a mouse searches for food.

The cat can wait in the kitchen or the pantry.
The mouse can scurry into the kitchen or the pantry.

If both choose the same spot, the cat pounces and wins.

If they choose different spots, the mouse finds food safely.






6. Border Patrol vs. Smuggler

Patrol agents guard a border while smugglers attempt a crossing.

The patrol can cover the mountain pass or the river crossing.
The smuggler can attempt the mountain pass or the river crossing.

If they both choose the same route, the patrol intercepts.

If they choose different routes, the smuggler succeeds.






7. Eagle vs. Rabbit

An eagle hunts across the fields while a rabbit runs for cover.

The eagle can swoop over the hilltop or the meadow.
The rabbit can dash to the hilltop or the meadow.

If they meet in the same place, the eagle catches the rabbit.

If they end up in different places, the rabbit survives.






8. Cybersecurity Analyst vs. Hacker

A hacker tries to breach a system, while an analyst sets defenses.

The analyst can guard the database or the email server.
The hacker can attack the database or the email server.

If both target the same asset, the analyst blocks the attack.

If they target different assets, the hacker breaks in.






9. Dog vs. Burglar

A burglar sneaks into a yard, while a guard dog patrols.

The dog can guard the front gate or the back door.
The burglar can enter through the front gate or the back door.

If they meet at the same entry point, the dog stops the burglar.

If they go to different points, the burglar succeeds.






10. Referee vs. Cheating Athlete

An athlete considers cheating in a competition, while the referee watches closely.

The referee can focus on the starting line or the finishing line.
The athlete can attempt to cheat at the starting line or the finishing line.

If both focus on the same spot, the referee catches the athlete.

If they focus on different spots, the athlete cheats successfully.
